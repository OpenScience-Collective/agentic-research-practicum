# Movie ERSP Direction, Why *The Present* Sits at an Underexplored Four-Strand Intersection

A focused review on the four-strand corpus assembled for the Healthy Brain Network electroencephalography (HBN-EEG) Release 3 *The Present* (Pixar 2014) boy-shot vs puppy-shot event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) project. The corpus reveals that no existing study reports per-shot scalp-EEG ERSP, in a child or adolescent cohort, during silent character animation, with shot-onset alignment, while partialling out a per-shot stimulus-side luminance regressor; the four perspectives surveyed (psychophysics, action, language, emotion) make divergent and partly incompatible predictions for what the 0 to 500 ms post-onset window should contain, and this paper argues that *The Present* at HBN-EEG R3 is positioned to discriminate among them. The argument was first articulated in the project plan locked on 21 April 2026 ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)) and is developed here against the 15 themes catalogued in the Phase 1 corpus and the Phase 2 [science-map](../research/synthesis/science-map.md), [psychophysics-ontology](../research/synthesis/psychophysics-ontology.md), [action-ontology](../research/synthesis/action-ontology.md), [language-ontology](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md), [emotion-ontology](../research/synthesis/emotion-ontology.md), [dataset-hierarchy](../research/synthesis/dataset-hierarchy.md), [scope-diagram](../research/synthesis/scope-diagram.md), and [gap-analysis](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md).

Abbreviations: event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP), inter-subject correlation (ISC), log luminance ratio (LLR), theory of mind (ToM), default-mode network (DMN), language model (LM), biological motion (BM), electroencephalography (EEG), magnetoencephalography (MEG), intracranial electroencephalography (iEEG), functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), event-related potential (ERP), event-related desynchronization (ERD), independent component analysis (ICA), independent component (IC), adaptive mixture ICA (AMICA), hidden Markov model (HMM), generalized linear model (GLM), Healthy Brain Network (HBN), HBN-EEG Release 3 (R3), middle temporal area (MT, also V5), middle superior temporal area (MST), primary visual cortex (V1), inferotemporal cortex (IT), superior temporal sulcus (STS), temporo-parietal junction (TPJ), medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC), ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC), nucleus accumbens (NAcc), ventral tegmental area / substantia nigra (VTA/SN), fusiform gyrus (FG), event segmentation theory (EST), temporal response function (TRF), fixation-related potential (FRP), Brain Imaging Data Structure (BIDS).

## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 The arc

Naturalistic-stimulus neuroscience moved from controlled gratings to feature films in two waves. The first wave was functional, with fMRI ISC showing that as much as 45 percent of cortex synchronises across viewers of the same audiovisual movie ([hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../research/collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md), [hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization](../research/collection/language/hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization/card.md)). The second wave was electrophysiological, with correlated component analysis on scalp EEG demonstrating that engagement, attention, memory, and audience preference all scale with the reliability of stimulus-locked variance ([dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../research/collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md), [cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives](../research/collection/psychophysics/cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives/card.md), [dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../research/collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md)). A third wave is now emerging that adds intracranial EEG to the same naturalistic paradigms and reveals whole-brain shot-cut-locked and saccade-locked transients with semantic novelty modulation ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).

In parallel, event segmentation theory consolidated the idea that continuous experience is parsed into discrete events at moments of high prediction error, with hierarchical timescales mapped from sensory cortex to default-mode regions ([zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md), [speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries/card.md), [baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md), [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../research/collection/action/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)). The hippocampus distinguishes within-event camera cuts from across-event narrative boundaries in fMRI ([benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../research/collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md)), while the iEEG cut response is whole-brain ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). Behavioural film cognition supplies the foundation that viewers segment edited films along cuts, independent of dialogue ([magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../research/collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md)).

A separate developmental tradition has used Pixar shorts in fMRI to map theory-of-mind and pain networks in children as young as three ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)) and silent abstract animation to improve MRI compliance and reveal reliable network-level activity ([vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md)). Cross-sectional EEG-ISC across ages 6 to 44 is the closest electrophysiological developmental anchor; ISC is highest in children and declines into adulthood ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)).

The boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast adds a fourth axis: pet- and infant-evoked affective response. Mothers viewing own child versus own dog photographs activate a partly shared affiliative network with reward-system selectivity for the child and fusiform selectivity for the dog ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md)). Baby-schema parametrically modulates nucleus accumbens reward in adults ([glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md)). Children as young as three already show parametric cuteness ratings and gaze bias for human infant, puppy, and kitten faces ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)).

### 1.2 Gap statement

The gap analysis enumerates eight named gaps that *The Present* ERSP is positioned to address ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md)). The corpus has whole-clip developmental EEG-ISC, child-cohort fMRI of Pixar shorts, adult iEEG of film cuts, and adult scalp-EEG ISC of naturalistic stimuli, but it does not contain a single paper-card that reports per-shot ERSP at the 0 to 500 ms post-onset window in a child cohort viewing animation ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md), [dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). The pet-evoked affective theme is the smallest in the corpus, with three primary papers, none of which use a moving stimulus, electroencephalography, or a child cohort ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md), [glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md), [borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)). Free-viewing EEG with eye-movement coregistration is a methodological tradition that assumes a synchronous eye tracker, which HBN-EEG R3 does not provide ([dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md), [dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md)). The dominant high-level regressor framework of the past five years, language-model surprisal aligned to spoken transcripts, structurally cannot transfer to a wordless stimulus; all seven Category G cards (and 12 cards corpus-wide) land at `transfer-to-silent: no` ([goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../research/collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../research/collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md), [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../research/collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md), [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../research/collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../research/collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md), [huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../research/collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md)). LLR as a continuous nuisance regressor has cinematic-fMRI precedent ([kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md)) and an iEEG analogue showing motion outranks luminance for occipitoparietal cortex ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)), but no scalp-EEG study uses a per-shot LLR regressor in a group-level GLM ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md)).

The negative space defined by these absences is precisely where *The Present* at HBN-EEG R3 sits, and the four perspectives surveyed in this paper make different and partly testable predictions about what should be found there. The science-map names five specific cross-strand contradictions in its Open questions sections that the planned analysis can adjudicate ([science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md)).

### 1.3 Thesis

This paper argues four claims, one per perspective. First, psychophysics is the dominant contributor to bottom-up ERSP variance in the 0 to 500 ms shot-onset window and must be partialled out before any social-content claim can be made. Second, action makes the strongest specific oscillatory prediction (mu-band ERD with possible beta-band rebound suppression for shots dominated by character action), inherited from adult hand-action observation but never tested with animated agents. Third, the language perspective enters the analysis as a structural comparator-of-non-transfer, defining what an LM-as-regressor framework cannot say about a silent stimulus, and supplying boundary cases for what silent narrative comprehension does engage. Fourth, emotion makes two distinct predictions, an early visual-cortex emotion-schema response from the EmoNet line and a longer-latency cuteness or affiliative response derived from baby-schema, neither of which has been measured at sub-second timescales in a child cohort viewing film.

The thesis is falsified under either of two pre-registered conditions. The thesis is falsified if the surviving condition-level signal in the LLR-partialled GLM is in language-network IC clusters (left frontotemporal, the [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../research/collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md) negative-control region) rather than central-rolandic (action-prediction territory; mu-band 8 to 13 Hz over electrodes C3, Cz, C4, [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)) or frontal-asymmetric (emotion-prediction territory; alpha-band 8 to 13 Hz over electrodes F3 and F4, [davidson-2000-affective-style](../research/collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md)); a left-frontotemporal effect would relocate the explanation to silent-stimulus engagement of language-network nodes, contradicting the four-perspective ranking the thesis commits to. The thesis is also falsified if no condition-level signal survives at the pre-registered cluster-level alpha (p less than 0.05, corrected by mass-univariate cluster-based permutation as in [crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../research/collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md)) once LLR is partialled, because the four-perspective ranking would then have been overstated relative to bottom-up exhaustion. Sections 2 and 3 develop the four-perspective argument; Section 4 anchors it on Petroni-Cohen 2018 plus the Phase 1 pipeline; Sections 5 to 8 lay out the roadmap, coordination, and discussion.

## 2. Background, the 15 themes

The four-perspective thesis announced in Section 1.3 cuts across 15 corpus themes that the Phase 2 [science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md) catalogues. This section indexes those themes and locates each thesis perspective in the appropriate ones, before Section 3 develops the perspective-by-perspective argument. Themes 1, 3, 4, 5, and 11 carry the psychophysics perspective; themes 2, 6, 8, and 15 carry the action perspective; themes 9, 10, and 14 carry the language perspective and its cross-modal extensions; themes 7, 12, and 13 carry the emotion perspective and the developmental anchor. Theme overlap is intentional, since the perspectives interact at the per-shot ERSP level rather than partitioning cleanly.

The Phase 1 corpus organises 98 entries across four strands. The themes below are summarised in the order they appear in the science-map. Each summary cites the establishing cards.

**Theme 1, Inter-subject correlation as a stimulus-locked reliability metric.** ISC is the most heavily represented analytic theme in the corpus, originating in fMRI free-viewing ([hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../research/collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md)) and migrating to EEG correlated component analysis ([dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md)), then to MEG ([lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../research/collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md)), peripheral physiology ([madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../research/collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md)), and audience prediction ([dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc/card.md)). Attention strongly modulates EEG ISC ([ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../research/collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md)). Cross-sectional developmental work places ISC at its lifetime peak in childhood ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)).

**Theme 2, Event boundaries and segmentation.** EST frames boundaries as moments of high prediction error ([zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md)). HMM applied to fMRI recovers a hierarchy of event states ([baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md)) and human-rated boundaries align with these states ([speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries/card.md)). The hippocampus dissociates within-event camera cuts from across-event narrative boundaries ([benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../research/collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md)), while iEEG shows whole-brain cut-locked transients ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). Behavioural segmentation tracks edits independent of dialogue ([magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../research/collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md)).

**Theme 3, Naturalistic stimuli and the naturalness gradient.** A continuum runs from controlled gratings, to static photographs, to abstract Heider-Simmel triangles, to character animation, to live-action film ([hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../research/collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md), [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../research/collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md), [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../research/collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md)). Pixar shorts in development sit between the abstract-animation and live-action ends ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)). Gaze coherence varies with stimulus class ([dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies](../research/collection/psychophysics/dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies/card.md)).

**Theme 4, Low-level feature regressors and bottom-up drivers.** A lineage runs from V1 receptive fields ([hubel-wiesel-1962-receptive-fields-cat-v1](../research/collection/psychophysics/hubel-wiesel-1962-receptive-fields-cat-v1/card.md)) and divisive normalisation ([carandini-heeger-2011-normalization](../research/collection/psychophysics/carandini-heeger-2011-normalization/card.md)), through natural-image statistics and spatiotemporal energy ([bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes](../research/collection/psychophysics/bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes/card.md), [simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats](../research/collection/psychophysics/simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats/card.md), [adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy](../research/collection/psychophysics/adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy/card.md)), through MT/MST motion machinery ([born-bradley-2005-mt-mst](../research/collection/psychophysics/born-bradley-2005-mt-mst/card.md), [bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt](../research/collection/psychophysics/bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt/card.md), [nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction](../research/collection/psychophysics/nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction/card.md)), to deep CNN regressors of ventral-stream representation ([khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it](../research/collection/psychophysics/khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it/card.md), [riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax](../research/collection/psychophysics/riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax/card.md)) and saliency ([kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii](../research/collection/psychophysics/kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii/card.md)). LLR is the simplest such regressor, and clinical VEP work supports its predicted scalp signature ([tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology](../research/collection/psychophysics/tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology/card.md)).

**Theme 5, Time-resolved EEG/MEG to dynamic naturalistic input.** TRF and correlated component methods dominate the continuous-EEG literature, mostly with speech ([lalor-foxe-2010-natural-speech-trf](../research/collection/psychophysics/lalor-foxe-2010-natural-speech-trf/card.md), [crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../research/collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md)) or audiovisual narrative ([cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives](../research/collection/psychophysics/cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../research/collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md)). Visual-only naturalistic-EEG with shot-locked spectral analysis is sparse; iEEG with film-cut regressors is the closest analogue ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).

**Theme 6, Mu rhythm, sensorimotor desynchronisation, and action observation.** Mu-band desynchronisation (8 to 13 Hz) and beta-band rebound suppression (15 to 25 Hz) over rolandic cortex during action observation are the EEG/MEG signatures most directly compatible with the 0 to 500 ms ERSP window ([hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)). Mu suppression magnitude scales with self-reported social skill ([oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../research/collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md)). Lesion mapping places posterior STS and ventral premotor cortex as causally necessary nodes ([saygin-2007-sts-lesions](../research/collection/action/saygin-2007-sts-lesions/card.md)). Predictive-coding reformulation casts mu responses as scaling with prediction error over goal and intention ([kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../research/collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md), [rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system](../research/collection/action/rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system/card.md), [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../research/collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md)).

**Theme 7, Affective dynamics and emotion-driven engagement.** MVPA decoding of discrete emotions ([saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md)), emotion-modulated ISC ([nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../research/collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md)), distributed-network meta-analyses ([lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md)), frontal alpha asymmetry ([davidson-2000-affective-style](../research/collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md), [coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry/card.md), [reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md)), and alpha-band desynchronisation to emotional pictures ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md), the Codispoti, De Cesarei, and Ferrari (2023) Psychophysiology review; the slug retains its original form for stable cross-references) supply the affective vocabulary. Discrete-emotion taxonomies ([saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md)) and dimensional taxonomies ([cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions/card.md)) disagree on granularity. Amygdala and vmPFC reviews flesh out the regional vocabulary ([ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads](../research/collection/emotion/ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads/card.md), [sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis/card.md), [etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion/card.md)).

**Theme 8, Social cognition, theory of mind, and mentalising during movie watching.** Silent-animation paradigms show that motion alone, without speech or human form, engages the mentalising network ([castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../research/collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../research/collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md)). The right TPJ is selectively engaged by mental-state reasoning ([saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom](../research/collection/emotion/saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom/card.md)). Children as young as three show ToM and pain networks during Pixar viewing ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)). DMN and narrative integration are the cortical substrate ([buckner-2008-default-network](../research/collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md), [simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc](../research/collection/language/simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc/card.md), [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../research/collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md), [mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition](../research/collection/emotion/mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition/card.md), [tamir-2015-fiction-default-network](../research/collection/emotion/tamir-2015-fiction-default-network/card.md)). A dedicated network for two-agent social interaction is documented in macaque fMRI ([sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network](../research/collection/action/sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network/card.md)). Empathy splits into experience-sharing and mentalising branches ([singer-2004-empathy-pain](../research/collection/emotion/singer-2004-empathy-pain/card.md), [zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural](../research/collection/emotion/zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural/card.md)).

**Theme 9, Language models and transcript-based regressors as comparator.** Transformer-based LM regressors aligned to spoken or read transcripts dominate naturalistic neuroimaging in 2020 to 2023 ([goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../research/collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../research/collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md), [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../research/collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md), [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../research/collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../research/collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md)). The N400 is the bridge from lexical to picture and short-video semantic-congruity effects ([kutas-2011-n400-review](../research/collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md), [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../research/collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md), [brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg](../research/collection/language/brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg/card.md), [huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../research/collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md)). The entire family is `transfer-to-silent: no` ([language-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md)). The frontotemporal language network atlas is included as a region-of-interest mask and as a negative-control region ([lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../research/collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md)).

**Theme 10, Audiovisual integration and cross-modal coupling.** Delta- and theta-band phase aligns to attended event onsets across modalities ([schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../research/collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md)); transient gamma and low-frequency phase coupling subserve cross-modal binding ([senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../research/collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md)). Visible mouth movements speed the auditory N1/P2 ([vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech](../research/collection/language/vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech/card.md)). For *The Present* the music score is the only sound; the silent-stimulus contribution and the music contribution are not separable in the current corpus ([magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../research/collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md), [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../research/collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md)).

**Theme 11, Free-viewing EEG with eye-movement coregistration.** FRPs and ICA-based eye-artifact correction depend on eye-tracker timing ([dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md)). Regression-deconvolution of overlapping events is the methodological state of the art ([dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md)). Eye-tracking on dynamic stimuli reveals stimulus-class-dependent gaze coherence ([dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies](../research/collection/psychophysics/dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies/card.md)). Naturalistic gaze allocation in autism is a related developmental signature ([klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md)). HBN-EEG R3 lacks a synchronous eye tracker, which is a methodological boundary the present design accepts.

**Theme 12, Pet, animal, and baby-schema affective response.** This is the smallest theme in the corpus. Adult fMRI of own child versus own dog activates a partly shared affiliative network with VTA/SN selectivity for child and FG selectivity for dog ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md)). Baby-schema parametrically modulates NAcc reward in adults ([glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md)). Children aged 3 to 6 show parametric cuteness ratings and gaze bias for human infant, puppy, and kitten faces ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)). The corpus contains no child-cohort EEG or fMRI of pet-evoked affect.

**Theme 13, Developmental neuroimaging in cinematic paradigms.** Six cards primarily report on a child or developmental cohort, clustering around fMRI of Pixar shorts ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)), Inscapes for fMRI compliance ([vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md)), EEG-ISC across ages 6 to 44 ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)), behavioural baby-schema in children ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)), and eye-tracking in autism ([klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md)). The corpus contains zero cards reporting per-shot EEG ERSP at 0 to 500 ms post-onset in a developmental cohort viewing animation ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md)).

**Theme 14, Multivariate distributed signatures versus regional locationism.** Distributed multivariate signatures (NPS, EmoNet, MVPA decoders, shared-pattern reinstatement) are the corpus's response to the failure of strict regional localisation for emotion and high-level cognition ([lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md), [saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md), [wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature](../research/collection/emotion/wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)). For EEG IC clusters this argues for distributed cluster contrasts rather than single-IC decoding.

**Theme 15, Predictive processing across observation, perception, and language.** Predictive coding unifies the mirror system as hierarchical Bayesian inference ([kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../research/collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md)), event segmentation as prediction-error-driven boundary detection ([zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md)), language-model surprisal as a regressor ([heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../research/collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../research/collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md)), N400 as graded prediction error ([delong-2005-n400-prediction](../research/collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md), [kutas-2011-n400-review](../research/collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md)), divisive normalisation as gain control ([carandini-heeger-2011-normalization](../research/collection/psychophysics/carandini-heeger-2011-normalization/card.md)), and alpha-beta interactions as top-down expectations ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)). *The Present*'s three-act narrative makes prediction error a candidate driver of shot-locked spectral perturbations.

## 3. Argument body

### 3.1 Psychophysics: bottom-up drivers must be partialled before any social claim

The first and most testable beat of the thesis is that low-level visual features will dominate the 0 to 500 ms post-shot-onset variance, and that any boy-vs-puppy effect that survives must be defended by partialling out at least luminance, motion, and gaze. The fMRI literature defines the regressor hierarchy. Hasson and colleagues showed that voxel-level cortical activity synchronises across viewers of the same audiovisual movie in up to 45 percent of cortex, with the early visual cortex carrying the largest stimulus-locked variance ([hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../research/collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md)). Nishimoto and colleagues reconstructed natural movies from BOLD activity in occipitotemporal cortex using a motion-energy front-end derived from Adelson and Bergen, providing existence proof that an Adelson-Bergen feature bank suffices to recover the stimulus from neural activity ([nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction](../research/collection/psychophysics/nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction/card.md), [adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy](../research/collection/psychophysics/adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy/card.md)). Bartels and colleagues separated global flow, local motion, and contrast-driven residuals in natural-vision fMRI; V5/MT+ is contrast-dependent and locally motion-driven, while medial parieto-occipital cortex is contrast-invariant and global-flow driven ([bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt](../research/collection/psychophysics/bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt/card.md), [born-bradley-2005-mt-mst](../research/collection/psychophysics/born-bradley-2005-mt-mst/card.md)). Divisive normalisation predicts compressive responses to luminance steps and motivates the log transform of the luminance ratio ([carandini-heeger-2011-normalization](../research/collection/psychophysics/carandini-heeger-2011-normalization/card.md)). Clinical VEP work confirms that luminance and contrast steps produce reliable scalp signatures with magnocellular and parvocellular pathway assignment ([tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology](../research/collection/psychophysics/tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology/card.md)).

The closest electrophysiological analogue to the planned *The Present* analysis is the iEEG study of Nentwich and colleagues, who recorded 6328 contacts in 23 patients during 43.6 minutes of film clips and regressed responses against optical-flow magnitude, saccade onsets, and film-cut onsets simultaneously ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). They found whole-brain saccade- and cut-locked responses, with motion concentrated in occipitoparietal cortex; critically, motion was a stronger driver than luminance or contrast for occipitoparietal cortex. That result establishes a quantitative ranking among low-level regressors and is the strongest single piece of corpus evidence that the per-shot LLR in `shot_events.tsv` may be only one of several low-level features that needs accounting. ISC at the whole-clip scale is dominated by attention ([ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../research/collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md)) but tracks low-level features at occipital electrodes more strongly than higher-order content ([dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../research/collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md), [cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives](../research/collection/psychophysics/cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives/card.md)). An envelope-only auditory control isolating low-level acoustic structure from higher-level musical structure ([kaneshiro-2021-music-eeg-isc](../research/collection/psychophysics/kaneshiro-2021-music-eeg-isc/card.md)) is the methodological template the LLR-as-covariate plan inherits.

A second class of bottom-up drivers operates through the eye. Free-viewing EEG depends on eye-movement coregistration to separate stimulus-onset responses from saccade-locked and fixation-related potentials ([dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md), [dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md)). Gaze coherence is highest on Hollywood trailers, intermediate on natural footage, and lowest on stop-motion or stills ([dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies](../research/collection/psychophysics/dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies/card.md)); a Pixar short sits between these extremes. HBN-EEG R3 carries no synchronous eye tracker, which means the planned analysis cannot deconvolve overlapping saccade-locked transients from shot-onset responses; ICA-based artifact rejection through AMICA and ICLabel is the operating compromise ([bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes](../research/collection/psychophysics/bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes/card.md), [simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats](../research/collection/psychophysics/simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats/card.md)). The planned LLR regressor in the group-level GLM partials out one low-level driver among several, and this paper proposes that motion-energy, computed offline from the stimulus video, be added as a second regressor in a follow-up pass; the corpus's existing template for such an analysis is the iEEG triple regressor of Nentwich and colleagues ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)) and the cinematic-feature fMRI multi-level regression of Kauttonen and colleagues ([kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md)). The mTRF MATLAB toolbox provides the production-ready regression framework ([crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../research/collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md)).

The key implication for *The Present* is asymmetric. If the boy-only and puppy-only conditions differ in mean LLR or mean motion energy, then a raw boy-vs-puppy ERSP contrast will be partly explained by low-level features and any social claim is unsupported. The locked decision to use LLR as a continuous regressor of no interest in the group-level GLM ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)) is the minimum partialling that the corpus supports; spot-checking the locked condition assignments against per-shot LLR distributions is a Phase 4 quality-control step, not a Phase 5 statistical test. Deep CNN regressors of ventral-stream representation are an upgrade path that the corpus supports in principle but not in scalp-EEG practice ([khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it](../research/collection/psychophysics/khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it/card.md), [riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax](../research/collection/psychophysics/riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax/card.md), [kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii](../research/collection/psychophysics/kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii/card.md)).

### 3.2 Action: mu-band ERD and event boundaries make the strongest specific oscillatory prediction

The action perspective makes the most specific positive prediction in the 0 to 500 ms ERSP window. Hari and colleagues showed by MEG that primary motor cortex is activated during passive observation of hand action via 15 to 25 Hz rolandic rebound suppression that reaches 31 to 46 percent of execution-related suppression ([hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md)). Pineda's review framed the EEG mu rhythm (8 to 13 Hz over electrodes C3, Cz, C4) as a non-invasive proxy for human mirror-system engagement ([pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)). Mu suppression magnitude during action observation correlates with self-reported social skill across neurotypical adults ([oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../research/collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md)). Lesion-symptom mapping places posterior STS and ventral premotor cortex as causally necessary for biological-motion perception ([saygin-2007-sts-lesions](../research/collection/action/saygin-2007-sts-lesions/card.md), [johansson-1973-biological-motion](../research/collection/action/johansson-1973-biological-motion/card.md)). Kilner and colleagues recast the mirror system as hierarchical Bayesian inference over intention, goal, and kinematics, predicting that mirror responses scale with prediction error ([kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../research/collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md), [rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system](../research/collection/action/rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system/card.md), [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../research/collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md)).

These findings together predict a specific signature for *The Present*. Shots dominated by character action, including the boy unwrapping the gift, the puppy bounding across the room, or either character moving on screen, should produce ERD in the mu band over central electrodes, with possible beta-band rebound suppression. Critically, this prediction is an extrapolation. Mu-rhythm and beta-rebound have been validated for hand-action observation in adults, not for animated-character action observation; the corpus contains no EEG study of animal action observation ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 7). The Heider-Simmel tradition shows that even abstract triangle animations recruit posterior STS, mPFC, and temporal poles when motion implies intention ([castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md)). The naturalness gradient places character animation between abstract Heider-Simmel and live-action ([hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../research/collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md)). The inferential bridge from triangle-animation fMRI activation to character-animation mu-band EEG ERD is plausible and untested.

A separate caveat about the underlying tradition is appropriate. The mu-rhythm-mirror-system framing inherits from a lineage with well-known critiques ([rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system](../research/collection/action/rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system/card.md), [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../research/collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)) including Hickok-style objections to one-to-one mirror-system interpretations of mu suppression. Those critiques are not represented as cards in the present corpus; the gap is documented in the limitations section and in [science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md) Theme 6 Open questions. The action-perspective mu-band prediction therefore relies on a tradition whose strongest steelman objections cannot be cited from within this corpus, and the planned analysis treats the mu-band test as exploratory rather than as a confirmatory adjudication of mirror-system theory.

The second action-perspective beat is event segmentation. EST predicts that perceptual event boundaries trigger transient prediction-error responses across the cortical hierarchy ([zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md)). Speer and colleagues found posterior cingulate, MT+, and posterior STS boundary-locked transients in fMRI during narrative listening ([speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries/card.md)). Baldassano and colleagues recovered a hierarchy of event boundaries from Sherlock-movie fMRI using HMM, with hippocampal boundary signals predicting subsequent free recall ([baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md)). Lerner and colleagues mapped temporal receptive windows from sensory cortex (milliseconds) to default-mode regions (tens of seconds) ([lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../research/collection/action/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md)). Chen and colleagues showed that event-specific patterns in DMN are shared across viewers and reactivated at recall ([chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)). Ben-Yakov and Henson distinguished within-event camera cuts, which produce minimal hippocampal responses, from across-event narrative boundaries, which produce robust ones ([benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../research/collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md)). Magliano and Zacks supplied the behavioural foundation that viewers segment edited films along cuts independent of dialogue ([magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../research/collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md)).

*The Present* has 56 shots total but a much smaller number of behavioural event boundaries. The hippocampal dissociation predicts that some shots are within-event camera cuts that should produce minimal hippocampal-locked variance, while a small subset that align with narrative beats (the boy refusing the gift, the reveal of the puppy's leg) should produce stronger transients. Whether the shot-locked EEG response should treat all 56 shots as equivalent events or weight them by behavioural-boundary alignment is an open question that the corpus does not resolve at scalp-EEG resolution; the iEEG evidence supports treating cuts as a strong neural event regardless of content ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).

A third action-perspective beat concerns single-agent versus two-agent shots. Sliwa and Freiwald documented a dedicated cortical network in macaque for processing two-agent social interaction, separable from single-agent action perception ([sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network](../research/collection/action/sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network/card.md)). The locked Contrast 1 deliberately excludes the three shots where both boy and puppy are visible at onset ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)); the macaque evidence motivates this design choice, since the social-interaction network may dominate two-agent shots and confound a clean single-agent contrast. Whether single-agent shots in a child cohort engage the same network remains untested in the corpus.

Counter-evidence within the action perspective is named. Klin and colleagues showed that toddlers with autism orient to audiovisual contingency rather than upright biological motion ([klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md)), and adolescents with autism fixate eyes 50 percent as often and mouths twice as often as typically developing controls during emotionally evocative live-action movie viewing ([klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md)). HBN includes a substantial autism-spectrum subsample, and any pooled boy-vs-puppy contrast may average across subgroups whose gaze allocation and biological-motion orienting differ. The autism subsample is therefore a candidate moderator rather than a noise source, and is named in the roadmap as a Phase 6 stratified analysis.

### 3.3 Language as comparator: a structural non-transfer with a silent-narrative positive sub-thread

The language perspective splits cleanly into two sub-claims. The first, in 3.3a, is that LM-as-regressor methods are structurally non-transferable to a silent stimulus. The second, in 3.3b, is that silent-narrative neural correlates do transfer and constrain the search space for *The Present*'s ERSP signature.

#### 3.3a Language-model regressors are structurally non-transferable

*The Present* is wordless. The entire family of language-model-as-regressor work depends on word-level alignment to spoken or written stimuli, and structurally cannot transfer ([language-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md), Category G). Goldstein and colleagues showed pre-onset prediction, post-onset surprise, and contextual-embedding signatures shared between word-by-word ECoG and autoregressive LMs ([goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../research/collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md)). Each of those signatures depends on speech-onset alignment. Heilbron and colleagues separated lexical, syntactic, and semantic surprisal regressors during MEG audiobook listening, all derived from LMs with word-onset alignment ([heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../research/collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md)). Caucheteux and colleagues showed transformer intermediate layers best predicting fMRI and MEG responses to natural narrative ([caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md)) and a cortical hierarchy of prediction timescales emerging when GPT-2 is augmented with prediction horizons ([caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md)). Antonello and colleagues documented log-linear scaling of brain-prediction performance with LM parameter count up to 30B ([antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../research/collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md)). Schrimpf and colleagues showed that next-word-prediction quality drives brain-score on fMRI, ECoG, and reading-time benchmarks ([schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../research/collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md)). Toneva and Wehbe used BERT to predict reading fMRI and MEG, with attention-head ablations linking brain prediction to NLP performance ([toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../research/collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md)). Huth and colleagues built the canonical voxelwise word-embedding encoding atlas tiling cortex with semantic clusters; this method requires spoken transcripts ([huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../research/collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md)). Brennan and colleagues tracked open-node count during syntactic merge using intracranial high-gamma and theta dynamics, explicitly reading-based ([brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg](../research/collection/language/brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg/card.md)). The N400 review extends the framework to picture-context paradigms, but at the cost of single-word presentations rather than continuous narrative ([kutas-2011-n400-review](../research/collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md), [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../research/collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md)). The frontotemporal language network atlas itself may serve as a negative-control region of interest in a silent paradigm ([lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../research/collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md)). All seven Category G cards (and 12 cards corpus-wide) land at `transfer-to-silent: no` ([language-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md)).

The structural non-transfer is itself informative. The contemporary methodological mainstream in naturalistic neuroimaging is built around language-model surprisal aligned to spoken transcripts. A silent-stimulus analysis is therefore methodologically isolated and cannot inherit the regressor framework. A vision-side analogue would require multimodal vision-language model embeddings or scene-difference deep-network features as continuous regressors. The corpus contains no card that uses such regressors with naturalistic-EEG ERSP ([science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md), Theme 9 Open questions). Deep-CNN visual-cortex modelling exists in fMRI ([khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it](../research/collection/psychophysics/khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md)) but has no scalp-EEG ERSP analogue.

#### 3.3b Silent-narrative neural correlates that do transfer

A second strand of language-strand cards documents what silent narrative comprehension does engage, independent of speech. These results transfer to the planned analysis as priors on cortical loci. Castelli and colleagues showed that silent geometric-shape animations engage mPFC, TPJ, and STS when motion implies social interaction, with no speech required ([castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../research/collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md)). The same paradigm in autism shows reduced social-network engagement ([castelli-2002-autism-animations](../research/collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md)). Vanderwal and colleagues built Inscapes, a purpose-built silent abstract animation that improves MRI compliance and produces reliable network-level activity, used by HBN itself ([vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md)). Naci and colleagues used a Hitchcock excerpt as a covert assessment, showing that high-order cortex can be probed from a near-silent narrative ([naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../research/collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md)). Lankinen and colleagues report source-space MEG reliable across viewers in occipital and temporal cortex during silent-visual and audiovisual movie conditions, the closest electrophysiological analogue with a deliberate silent-visual condition (N = 8 adults) ([lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../research/collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md)). The Studyforrest infrastructure provides an audio-only foundation that has been extended to silent-cohort contrasts ([hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t](../research/collection/language/hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t/card.md)). Schroeder and colleagues described modality-general delta- and theta-band phase alignment to attended event onsets, providing the mechanistic frame for shot-onset ERSP independent of speech ([schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../research/collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md)). Senkowski and colleagues described transient gamma synchronisation and low-frequency phase coupling for cross-modal binding ([senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../research/collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md)). Van Wassenhove and colleagues showed visible mouth movements speed the auditory N1/P2, an analysis-by-synthesis effect that does not transfer here because *The Present* contains no spoken dialogue ([vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech](../research/collection/language/vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech/card.md)). Buckner, Simony, Yeshurun, and colleagues developed the DMN as narrative integrator, with framing-context-driven divergence as a within-stimulus source of variance ([buckner-2008-default-network](../research/collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md), [simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc](../research/collection/language/simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc/card.md), [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../research/collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md)).

The language perspective's role in this paper is therefore double. The negative result, 3.3a, defines the methodological isolation of the silent-stimulus design and limits which regressor families can transfer. The positive sub-thread, 3.3b, supplies the cortical substrates that silent narrative is expected to engage: mPFC, TPJ, STS, and the DMN ([castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../research/collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [buckner-2008-default-network](../research/collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md), [saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom](../research/collection/emotion/saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom/card.md)). Their independent-component analogues in EEG IC clusters are the search regions the planned ERSP analysis targets.

### 3.4 Emotion: two distinct predictions, neither tested at sub-second timescales in children

The emotion perspective makes two predictions, with different latencies and different implicated structures. The first is an early visual-cortex emotion-schema response. Kragel and colleagues built EmoNet, a deep-learning model showing that emotion schemas are encoded in early visual cortex ([kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md)). If emotion-tuned visual representations exist at the level of early visual cortex, they should appear in early-latency occipital ERSP. Saarimaki and colleagues decoded six basic emotions during emotional movie viewing using fMRI MVPA, anchoring the cinematic-emotion-induction literature ([saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md)); Cowen and Keltner extended the taxonomy to 27 distinguishable categories from short videos ([cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions/card.md)). Distributed-network meta-analysis argues for distributed signatures rather than strict regional localisation ([lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md)), with the NPS as a methodological exemplar of multivariate signatures of affect ([wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature](../research/collection/emotion/wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature/card.md)). Amygdala and vmPFC reviews flesh out the regional vocabulary ([ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads](../research/collection/emotion/ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads/card.md), [sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis/card.md), [etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion/card.md)). The closest EEG correlate of emotional content at the 0 to 500 ms scale is alpha-band desynchronisation during emotional pictures ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)); whether this transfers to dynamic naturalistic stimuli at sub-second timescales in a child cohort is untested ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 8).

The second prediction is a longer-latency cuteness or affiliative response specific to the puppy condition. This is the load-bearing prediction for the boy-vs-puppy contrast. Stoeckel and colleagues scanned 14 mothers viewing photographs of own child versus own dog. They reported common ("own > unfamiliar") activation across child and dog spanning emotion, reward, affiliation, visual processing, and social cognition regions, with VTA/SN selectivity for own child and FG selectivity for own dog ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md)). Glocker and colleagues showed that baby-schema parametrically modulates NAcc reward in adults ([glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md)). Borgi and colleagues demonstrated that children aged 3 to 6 already show parametric cuteness ratings and gaze bias for human infant, puppy, and kitten faces ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)). The Borgi finding provides the behavioural anchor that the cuteness response is established and species-general well before adolescence. The framing implication is that Stoeckel measures identity-level pair-bonding (own pet) while Borgi measures generic baby-schema. HBN viewers have no identity-level bond with *The Present*'s animated puppy, so the relevant inference is from generic baby-schema rather than pair-bonding circuitry ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 3).

Two routes connect these two predictions to EEG observables. The first is alpha-band desynchronisation as an arousal-modulated correlate of attentional engagement, which has been validated for static IAPS pictures ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)) but never for shot-locked windows during naturalistic film. The second is frontal alpha asymmetry as an approach-withdrawal index ([davidson-2000-affective-style](../research/collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md), [coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry/card.md)). The asymmetry literature was consolidated at the seconds-to-minutes timescale; an updated meta-analytic critique documents smaller effect sizes and substantial reliability concerns ([reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md)). The corpus contains no card applying asymmetry analysis to per-event sub-second windows during a continuous naturalistic stimulus, and none in a developmental cohort viewing film ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 8). Frontal asymmetry at shot-onset latency is therefore exploratory rather than confirmatory.

A third emotion-perspective beat is social cognition, theory of mind, and DMN-driven engagement. Saxe and Kanwisher localised right TPJ to mental-state reasoning ([saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom](../research/collection/emotion/saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom/card.md)). Richardson and colleagues documented ToM and pain networks present from age three and refining with age, using Pixar shorts in 122 children ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)); this is the load-bearing developmental anchor for naturalistic social-cognitive engagement. Mar synthesised narrative comprehension as a social-cognitive activity ([mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition](../research/collection/emotion/mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition/card.md)); Tamir and colleagues documented fiction reading as preferentially engaging the DMN with lifetime fiction exposure as an individual-differences moderator ([tamir-2015-fiction-default-network](../research/collection/emotion/tamir-2015-fiction-default-network/card.md)). Singer and colleagues documented affective but not sensory pain-region engagement during observed pain ([singer-2004-empathy-pain](../research/collection/emotion/singer-2004-empathy-pain/card.md)); Zaki and Ochsner formalised the tripartite empathy model bridging experience-sharing and mental-state attribution ([zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural](../research/collection/emotion/zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural/card.md)). Nummenmaa and colleagues showed emotion intensity modulates ISC in midline cortex during film viewing ([nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../research/collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md)). Schmaelzle and Grall theorised ISC as audience captivation ([schmalzle-2020-coupled-brains](../research/collection/emotion/schmalzle-2020-coupled-brains/card.md)). Petroni and colleagues reported EEG ISC across ages 6 to 44 with a developmental peak in childhood ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)). Kauttonen and colleagues regressed multi-level cinematic features against fMRI ISC, supplying a methodological template for shot-level feature annotation ([kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md)).

The synthesis across the emotion perspective is that two distinct predictions sit at incompatible latencies and incompatible scalp locations. Early occipital alpha desynchronisation should reflect emotion-schema visual representations and arousal-driven attention ([kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md), [schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)), while later frontal asymmetry and DMN-driven IC clusters should reflect cuteness-driven affiliative response and mentalising ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md), [stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)). Whether a 0 to 500 ms ERSP window captures one, both, or neither is the empirical question.

## 4. Anchor case: external precedent and internal feasibility

The anchor case for the planned direction rests on two assertions, one external and one internal, each deliberately narrow.

**External precedent.** Petroni and colleagues recorded 64-channel EEG at 500 Hz from 114 viewers across ages 6 to 44 during passive viewing of six naturalistic videos including animated and live-action shorts ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)). Whole-clip EEG-ISC was reliable across the age range and peaked in childhood. Petroni and colleagues did not analyse *The Present* specifically, did not compute per-shot ERSP, and did not factor stimulus-side regressors into the per-condition analysis; nevertheless the study demonstrates that scalp-EEG signal exists during developmental naturalistic viewing of short videos and is a defensible electrophysiological precedent for the planned design. Petroni-Cohen 2018 is the closest external existence proof in the four-strand corpus that the kind of measurement the present direction proposes is feasible in adjacent territory.

**Internal feasibility.** Phase 1 of the parallel HBN ERSP processing epic has been committed and partly validated. BIDS import, 1 Hz high-pass filter, conditional cleanline (skipped at 100 Hz on Nyquist grounds), and `clean_rawdata` channel rejection produce a checkpointed `derivatives/preproc/` for the 184-subject local 100 Hz BDF working set ([`.context/research.md`](../.context/research.md), [dataset-hierarchy.md](../research/synthesis/dataset-hierarchy.md)). Recording is via 128-channel HydroCel Geodesic Sensor Net during passive viewing of the 3.5-minute Pixar short *The Present* (Pixar 2014). Stimulus-side annotation in `shot_events.tsv` covers 56 shots with `onset`, `duration`, `LLR`, `has_boy`, and `has_puppy` per shot; after invalidating 3 high-drift rows (`match_diff_s > 1.0 s`), 49 rows are trusted, yielding 20 boy-only and 15 puppy-only shots for the locked Contrast 1 ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md), [project_brief.md](../project_brief.md)). AMICA decomposition (24-way parallel) and dipfit5 inverse modelling are scheduled for Phase 2 ([bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes](../research/collection/psychophysics/bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes/card.md)); ICLabel classification with brain-threshold 0.69 follows in Phase 3 ([ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md)); shot-event expansion via `expand_events` and `pop_epoch` (-0.6 to +0.6 s around shots) follow in Phase 4; STUDY-level ERSP precompute on the 0 to 500 ms window with baseline -500 to -100 ms and IC clustering follow in Phase 5 ([crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../research/collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md)); group contrasts with LLR as a continuous covariate and condition as the categorical predictor follow in Phase 6 ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md), [kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md)). Reference-pipeline knowledge from `~/Documents/git/HBN_BIDS_analysis/study_handy_scripts.m` informs parameter choices but does not substitute for re-derivation.

The two anchor assertions are independent. The Petroni-Cohen 2018 result establishes that the broad measurement class is feasible in adjacent territory. The Phase 1 pipeline establishes that the specific HBN-EEG R3 cohort can in fact be brought to the epoching and ERSP-precompute stage on the local working set. Together they support, but do not prove, that the four-perspective design described in Sections 1 to 3 can be executed end to end. The unique-feature conjunction that previously occupied this section is rhetorical and is moved to Appendix A for readers interested in the design's distinguishing surface attributes; it is not load-bearing for the anchor case.

## 5. Roadmap

The roadmap commits the planned Phases 2 to 6 of the parallel HBN ERSP processing epic to addressing specific gaps from [gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md). Each commitment names the artifact it produces and the gap it closes; the gap definitions themselves are anchored in the corpus cards listed under the corresponding gap entries ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).

**Commitment 1 (closes Gap 1, child-cohort EEG ERSP at shot onsets in animation).** Phase 4 expands `shot_events.tsv` to per-subject `shots` events and epochs each cleaned dataset at -0.6 to +0.6 s around onset. Phase 5 computes STUDY-level ERSP on the 0 to 500 ms post-onset window with baseline -500 to -100 ms via `std_precomp` ([`.context/research.md`](../.context/research.md)). The artifact is the first per-shot ERSP measurement in a developmental cohort viewing animation, anchoring [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md) to a finer temporal grain.

**Commitment 2 (closes Gap 2, LLR as a continuous regressor in EEG ERSP).** Phase 6 group-level GLM uses LLR as a continuous covariate of no interest with the boy-only vs puppy-only condition as the categorical predictor ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)). This delivers the first scalp-EEG ERSP analysis with a per-shot LLR regressor, complementing the iEEG triple-regressor template of [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md). A follow-up pass adds a motion-energy regressor computed offline from the stimulus video, motivated by the Nentwich finding that motion outranks luminance for occipitoparietal cortex.

**Commitment 3 (closes Gap 3, pet- or animal-evoked affective EEG, especially in children).** The boy-vs-puppy contrast itself is the first EEG analysis of pet-evoked affective response in a child cohort. The interpretation explicitly avoids identity-level pair-bonding claims ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md)) and instead grounds any puppy-specific response in the generic baby-schema developmental anchor ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)) and the adult NAcc baby-schema response ([glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md)). The interpretation is that any puppy-specific frontal or central ERSP cluster reflects cuteness-driven attentional and affiliative engagement, not pair-bonding circuitry.

**Commitment 4 (closes Gap 4, silent-narrative ERSP at 0 to 500 ms event boundaries).** *The Present*'s 56-shot annotated stimulus supplies the first scalp-EEG ERSP probe of silent-narrative event boundaries in this corpus. The silent-stimulus framing intentionally factors out the speech-driven regressor families that dominate language ontology Category G ([language-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md)). The deliverable is a per-shot ERSP map at occipital and temporal IC clusters, with the silent-cohort MEG ISC of [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../research/collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md) as the closest electrophysiological precedent.

**Commitment 5 (closes Gap 5, cross-strand integration into a single per-shot ERSP model).** Phase 6 fits a group-level GLM that combines LLR (low-level), condition (boy-only vs puppy-only), and shot-index-in-narrative (a continuous regressor capturing within-stimulus position; see also Section 7.3 narrative-position objection). The follow-up pass adds an event-state regressor derived from a Baldassano-style HMM applied to the *The Present* video stream, scheduled to run after the primary Phase 6 GLM converges and the cluster-level effects on the boy-vs-puppy contrast are catalogued ([baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md), [zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md)). The trigger for the HMM follow-up is completion of the primary GLM with at least one cluster surviving the pre-registered cluster-level alpha (p less than 0.05, corrected); if no cluster survives, the HMM regressor is reported as a null follow-up with the same pre-registered alpha rather than as a fishing expedition. The deliverable is a multi-level cinematic-feature regression on per-shot scalp-EEG ERSP, the first of its kind in this corpus and complementing the cinematic-feature fMRI of [kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md) and the multi-modal sync of [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../research/collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md).

**Commitment 6 (closes Gap 6, free-viewing animation EEG without eye-movement coregistration).** Phase 6 publishes a methodological position paper that names the operating compromise for the absence of a synchronous eye tracker, documents the ICA-only artifact rejection pipeline through AMICA and ICLabel ([ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md), [dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md)), and quantifies the residual saccade-locked variance contamination via an ICLabel rejection-rate audit and an IC-cluster topography check against canonical eye-IC templates. The artifact is a documentation deliverable rather than a primary research finding; for that reason it is reframed in Section 5.1 (Future work) below as an accompanying methodological position paper, not as a gap-closure on the same footing as Commitments 1 to 5 and 7. The 500 Hz validation pass on the full S3 R3 release does not change this gap because eye tracking is not available there either.

**Commitment 7 (closes Gap 7, mu-band action-observation EEG to animated agents).** The Phase 5 ERSP precompute on 4 to 40 Hz and the Phase 6 cluster-level statistics test mu-band ERD over central IC clusters as a hypothesised signature for shots dominated by character action. The deliverable is a mu-band test for animated-character action observation, framed as an extrapolation from human-hand-action mu suppression ([hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md), [oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../research/collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md)) to animated-agent action, with no direct precedent in the corpus. Per Section 3.2's hedge, the test is exploratory with respect to mirror-system theory.

**Commitment 8 (closes Gap 8, frontal alpha asymmetry at sub-second timescales during dynamic naturalistic stimuli).** Phase 6 fits a shot-locked frontal alpha asymmetry contrast at electrodes F3 and F4 with a pre-registered cluster-level alpha of p less than 0.05, corrected by mass-univariate cluster-based permutation. The contrast is positioned against the reliability concerns raised by [reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md) over the canonical [davidson-2000-affective-style](../research/collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md) framework, and is a confirmatory hypothesis at this pre-registered alpha (not exploratory). A null result at this alpha is reported as a planned negative finding that contributes to the corpus-grounded reliability debate; an effect surviving this alpha would be the first sub-second-window asymmetry observation during dynamic naturalistic viewing in a developmental cohort.

The Phase 6 outputs include a STUDY with `_amica`, `_iclabel`, `_clustered`, `_epoched` intermediates under `derivatives/`, group-level ERSP maps per condition on the 0 to 500 ms window with baseline -500 to -100 ms, cluster-level statistics across occipital and temporal IC clusters, and a manuscript-ready conditionwise ERSP-difference figure at the cluster of maximal effect ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md), [project_brief.md](../project_brief.md)). The planned cluster contrasts are calibrated against the distributed-multivariate-signature literature ([lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)) rather than single-IC decoding.

### 5.1 Future work

Several extensions sit outside the Phase 6 confirmatory pre-registration and are catalogued here rather than as gap-closures. The methodological position paper described in Commitment 6 is documentation rather than a primary research artifact; it ships alongside the main analysis but does not close a gap-to-artifact mapping in the same sense as Commitments 1 to 5 and 7. A motion-energy regressor computed offline from the *The Present* video stream, motivated by the Nentwich finding that motion outranks luminance for occipitoparietal cortex ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)), is scheduled as the first follow-up to Commitment 2. A within-act stratified analysis using shot-index-in-narrative, motivated by the narrative-position objection in Section 7.3, is scheduled as the first follow-up to Commitment 5. Stratified analyses by autism-spectrum status, motivated by [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md), and [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../research/collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md), are exploratory and reported as descriptive rather than confirmatory. The 500 Hz validation pass on the full S3 R3 release is a methodological replication, not a new gap-closure, and is scheduled after the local 100 Hz pipeline produces a stable manuscript-ready figure.

## 7. Discussion

### 7.1 Interpretation

If the direction is adopted and the Phase 6 contrast yields a per-shot ERSP difference between boy-only and puppy-only conditions that survives LLR partialling at the pre-registered cluster-level alpha (p less than 0.05, corrected), the field gains its first scalp-EEG ERSP measurement of single-agent social content during silent character animation in a developmental cohort ([petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)). This would convert the four-strand gap into a four-strand point of contact: psychophysics is partialled (Commitment 2), action makes a falsifiable mu-band prediction (Commitment 7), language is comparator-of-non-transfer (Commitments 4 and the 3.3 split), and emotion supplies the load-bearing interpretation of any condition difference (Commitment 3). The corpus ranks the predictions by depth of prior evidence. Mu-band ERD has the deepest adult precedent but is least tested in development ([hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)). Baby-schema cuteness has the strongest developmental anchor but no prior EEG ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md), [glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md)). Emotion-schema occipital alpha desynchronisation has the strongest sub-second EEG precedent but only for static IAPS pictures ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md)).

The pre-registered topographic-and-band rejection region from Section 1.3 governs interpretation as follows. A surviving central-rolandic mu-band cluster supports the action prediction; a surviving frontal-asymmetric alpha cluster supports the emotion prediction; a surviving left-frontotemporal cluster (overlapping the [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../research/collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md) negative-control mask) falsifies the four-perspective ranking by relocating the explanation into a perspective the thesis claims should not transfer. A null result on the LLR-partialled GLM at the pre-registered alpha would equally falsify the four-perspective ranking, by localising per-shot ERSP variance entirely to bottom-up features in this cohort. The null is not the strongest possible disconfirmation, but it does adjudicate the open question whether ISC at the shot-onset ERSP scale is a different signal from whole-stimulus ISC ([science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md), Theme 1 Open questions; [hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../research/collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md)).

If the direction is adopted at the methodological level, the field gains a template for per-shot multi-regressor ERSP analysis on developmental EEG without simultaneous eye tracking ([dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md)). The template combines stimulus-side annotation (LLR, agent presence), event-locked epoching, AMICA-based artifact rejection ([bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes](../research/collection/psychophysics/bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes/card.md)), STUDY-level ERSP, IC clustering, and group-level GLM with continuous covariates. The mTRF toolbox ([crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../research/collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md)) supplies an alternative continuous-regressor framework that the Phase 6 extension can adopt for a follow-up motion-energy regression ([adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy](../research/collection/psychophysics/adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).

### 7.2 Limitations

Several corpus entries do not support the thesis and are named here. The LMs-as-regressors line in language ontology Category G ([language-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/language-ontology.md)) is structurally non-transferable; this paper's claim that language is comparator-of-non-transfer is therefore an admission that the dominant high-level regressor framework of the past five years cannot be applied. Specifically, neither [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../research/collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../research/collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../research/collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md), [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../research/collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md), [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../research/collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md), nor [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../research/collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md) supplies a usable per-shot regressor for *The Present*. The vision-side analogue does not yet exist in the corpus ([science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md), Theme 9 Open questions).

The emotion literature is predominantly adult. Of the 25 emotion cards, three are validated in children ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)), and the remaining 22 carry adult-only-bridged status ([emotion-ontology.md](../research/synthesis/emotion-ontology.md)). Specifically, [stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md) is identity-level pair-bonding in mothers and does not transfer cleanly to anonymous-puppy viewing in children; [glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md) is in nulliparous women with static photographs; [saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md), [nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../research/collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../research/collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md), [cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions](../research/collection/emotion/cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions/card.md), and the regional reviews ([ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads](../research/collection/emotion/ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads/card.md), [sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis/card.md), [etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion/card.md), [lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../research/collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md), [wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature](../research/collection/emotion/wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature/card.md)) are adult-only. The frontal asymmetry tradition's reliability is contested by an updated meta-analytic critique ([reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md)), and the asymmetry analysis at sub-second timescales during dynamic naturalistic stimuli has no precedent ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 8). The corpus contains no pet-evoked EEG of any kind, and the puppy-shot interpretation rests on three primary papers, all fMRI or behavioural ([stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../research/collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md), [glocker-2009-baby-schema](../research/collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md), [borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../research/collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)).

The action-perspective mu-band prediction is an extrapolation that lacks a direct animated-agent precedent ([gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md), Gap 7). Mirror-system interpretations of mu suppression have been challenged outside the corpus, and the corpus does not contain strong critiques such as Hickok 2009 ([science-map.md](../research/synthesis/science-map.md), Theme 6 Open questions). The Heider-Simmel-to-character-animation bridge ([castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../research/collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md)) is plausible but untested at scalp-EEG ERSP resolution.

The sample-size limitation is real. The locked Contrast 1 yields 20 boy-only and 15 puppy-only stim-side shots; the trial-count floor of 10 trials per condition per subject ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)) is achievable only at the group level across 184 subjects. The corpus contains no card with a per-shot trial count this low at the EEG IC scale; the closest analogue is [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md) at the whole-clip level. The 100 Hz sampling rate caps any beta-band and gamma-band claims; a 500 Hz validation pass on the full S3 R3 release is planned ([`.context/research.md`](../.context/research.md), [`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)), but eye tracking is not available there either. The frequency-resolved evidence at gamma frequencies, where some predictions live ([senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../research/collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md)), is therefore inaccessible until the 500 Hz pass.

The single-stimulus design is also a limitation. The brief explicitly forbids generalisation to other HBN movies in this project ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md), [project_brief.md](../project_brief.md)); whether any boy-vs-puppy effect generalises beyond *The Present* is therefore out of scope. The HBN cohort is heterogeneous in psychiatric profile; pooled analyses average across substantial within-cohort variance, and stratified analyses (e.g., by autism-spectrum status) are exploratory follow-ups rather than primary tests ([klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../research/collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md), [oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../research/collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md), [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../research/collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md)).

### 7.3 Counterarguments

The strongest objection is that single-shot ERSP cannot separate boy from puppy in a single 0 to 500 ms window once LLR is partialled out, because LLR, motion energy, and scene-cut transients already explain most of the bottom-up variance at this timescale. Bartels and colleagues showed that natural-vision fMRI separates global flow, local motion, and contrast-driven residuals into anatomically distinct regions, with V5/MT+ contrast-dependent and locally motion-driven and medial parieto-occipital cortex contrast-invariant and global-flow driven ([bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt](../research/collection/psychophysics/bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt/card.md)). Nishimoto and colleagues reconstructed natural movies from BOLD activity using Adelson-Bergen motion-energy features alone ([nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction](../research/collection/psychophysics/nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction/card.md), [adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy](../research/collection/psychophysics/adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy/card.md)); if motion-energy alone suffices for movie reconstruction, then LLR, motion energy, and scene-cut transients together may exhaust the early-window variance. Nentwich and colleagues showed in iEEG that motion is the dominant low-level driver in occipitoparietal cortex, with luminance/contrast as a secondary contributor ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). Boy-only and puppy-only conditions differ on more than identity; they differ on average colour, motion profile, scene composition, and continuity-of-cut, and these confounds are difficult to fully partial with LLR alone. The objection is sharpest at occipital electrodes, where the bottom-up variance is largest.

The corpus-grounded response has three parts. First, the corpus does not establish that LLR plus motion energy plus scene cuts exhaust the per-shot ERSP variance at scalp-EEG resolution; the Nentwich result is in adult iEEG with a sparse clinical sampling pattern, and its scalp-EEG generalisation is approximate ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md) Open questions / limitations). Cross-cohort evidence from EEG ISC suggests that attention-modulated and content-driven variance contributes meaningfully on top of low-level features in occipital and temporal regions ([ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../research/collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md), [dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md)). Second, the prediction made by the action and emotion perspectives is not occipital but central and frontal: mu-band ERD over central electrodes ([hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../research/collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md)) and frontal alpha desynchronisation or asymmetry ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md), [davidson-2000-affective-style](../research/collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md)). The bottom-up exhaustion objection is most lethal for occipital ERSP, where the action and emotion predictions are weakest; it is less lethal for central and frontal IC clusters, where the predictions are strongest. Third, the planned design is built to make this falsifiable. The Phase 6 group-level GLM partials LLR explicitly; the Phase 6 follow-up adds motion-energy. If condition survives both, it survives the steelman objection. If it does not, the corpus has documented an important null result: per-shot ERSP variance in animated-character viewing in a developmental cohort is exhausted by low-level features.

A second objection is narrative-position confounding. Boy-only and puppy-only conditions may differ on where the constituent shots sit in *The Present*'s three-act structure. Boy-only shots cluster in the early-act setup (boy refusing the gift, boy apathetic on the couch), and puppy-only shots cluster in the late-act revelation and resolution (puppy alone in frame, puppy bounding into the room). Any boy-vs-puppy ERSP difference may therefore be confounded with prediction-error or arousal trajectories driven by narrative position rather than by character identity. Event-segmentation theory frames boundaries as moments of high prediction error ([zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../research/collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md)). HMM applied to fMRI recovers a hierarchy of event states aligned with these boundaries ([baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md)). Event-specific patterns in DMN are shared across viewers and reactivated at recall, suggesting the act-level structure is itself a substantial source of cortical variance during naturalistic viewing ([chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)). The corpus-grounded response is to add shot-index-in-narrative as a continuous covariate in the Phase 6 GLM (Commitment 5) and to fit a within-act stratified analysis as a follow-up (Section 5.1 Future work). Both are tractable from the existing `shot_events.tsv` because every shot already carries an `onset` field that locates it within the 3.5-minute clip; act boundaries can be derived from the behavioural-segmentation literature on continuity editing ([magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../research/collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md)) without new annotation.

A third objection comes from the frontal asymmetry literature itself. The reliability and effect-size concerns of [reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../research/collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md) suggest that the asymmetry framework may not survive sub-second window analysis, and this is conceded in Section 7.2. The Commitment 8 pre-registered alpha (p less than 0.05, corrected) is the operational guard.

A fourth objection is methodological. The locked decision to use 100 Hz local BDF for the dev loop ([`.context/ideas.md`](../.context/ideas.md)) means that beta-band and gamma-band predictions cannot be tested at full resolution until the 500 Hz S3 validation pass. The corpus's gamma-band findings ([senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../research/collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md)) are out of scope at the dev tier. The response is that the 100 Hz pass is sufficient for theta, alpha, and low-beta, which is where most of the action and emotion predictions live, and the 500 Hz pass is on the roadmap for the gamma-band component.

A fifth objection is the absence of eye tracking ([dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../research/collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md), [dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading](../research/collection/psychophysics/dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading/card.md)). The response is that ICA-based artifact rejection through AMICA and ICLabel is the operating compromise documented in Commitment 6. The per-shot ERSP analysis does not require deconvolution of overlapping events because the shot-onset latency is widely separated from the typical inter-saccadic interval. Saccade-locked transients within the post-onset window remain a residual confound, named in Commitment 6's audit deliverable.

The pre-registered topographic-and-band rejection region from Section 1.3 also applies here. If the surviving condition-level signal lands in left frontotemporal IC clusters (a [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../research/collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md) negative-control region) rather than central-rolandic or frontal-asymmetric clusters, the four-perspective ranking is falsified. If no condition-level signal survives the LLR-partialled GLM at the pre-registered cluster-level alpha (p less than 0.05, corrected), the four-perspective ranking is also falsified, and the bottom-up exhaustion objection wins. This is the operational form of the falsifiability rejection region introduced in Section 1.3.

## 8. Conclusion

The HBN *The Present* boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP contrast at 0 to 500 ms post-shot-onset, with LLR as a continuous regressor of no interest, sits at an intersection of psychophysics, action, language, and emotion that no card in the four-strand corpus occupies. Of the four perspectives surveyed, action and emotion supply the strongest condition-specific predictions; psychophysics dominates the bottom-up variance and must be partialled; language is structurally a comparator-of-non-transfer because the stimulus is silent. The 184-subject HBN-EEG R3 cohort, the 49 trusted shot-onset annotations, and the partly validated EEGLAB pipeline are the existence proof that the gap is buildable. The roadmap commits Phases 2 to 6 of the parallel HBN ERSP processing epic to addressing the eight gaps named in [gap-analysis.md](../research/synthesis/gap-analysis.md). A null result on the condition contrast that survives LLR partialling would itself be informative; a positive result on mu-band ERD, alpha desynchronisation, or frontal asymmetry would be the first such observation in a developmental cohort viewing animated agents. The direction paper is grounded in roughly 96 distinct cite-card cross-links spanning all four strands and explicitly names the corpus's adversarial limitations.

## Appendix A. Distinguishing surface features of the design

Six surface features distinguish *The Present* at HBN-EEG R3 from the most-cited cards in the corpus. The list is descriptive rather than evidential; the anchor case in Section 4 is carried by the Petroni-Cohen 2018 external precedent and the Phase 1 internal feasibility, not by this conjunction. The features are: (1) per-shot scalp EEG, not whole-clip ISC ([dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../research/collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)); (2) ERSP at the 0 to 500 ms window, not BOLD or static-picture ERP ([baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../research/collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md), [schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../research/collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)); (3) animated character agents (boy and puppy), not abstract triangles, hand actions, or live-action characters ([castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../research/collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../research/collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../research/collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md)); (4) a child and adolescent cohort (ages 5 to 21), not adults ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../research/collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../research/collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md)); (5) silent stimulus, factoring out the speech-driven regressor families ([huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../research/collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../research/collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md)); and (6) stimulus-side LLR per shot as a continuous nuisance regressor, not a matched control ([kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../research/collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../research/collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)).
