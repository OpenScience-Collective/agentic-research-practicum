# Language Ontology

A hierarchical view of the language strand corpus. ThePresent is a wordless animated short, so this strand functions as comparator and boundary. The corpus is organised first by mechanism (what cognitive or neural process the work characterises), then by paradigm (whether the cited evidence depends on speech, transcript, or word-onset alignment). The cross-cutting `transfer-to-silent` tag is the load-bearing axis of this ontology and is reported under cross-cutting tags below.

Each entry links to its paper-card and carries a one-line role statement.

## Top-level categories

### Category A: Naturalistic neural synchrony to continuous stimuli

Establishes that brains entrain reliably to a shared continuous stimulus and gives the methodological grammar (inter-subject correlation, time-frequency reliability) used by every downstream movie study.

- Sub-category A.1: Foundational inter-subject correlation (ISC) on continuous movies
  - [hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization](../collection/language/hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization/card.md): Founding demonstration that watching the same audiovisual movie synchronizes BOLD activity across viewers in up to 45 percent of cortex.
  - [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md): Frontoparietal ISC tracks the rated suspense profile of a Hitchcock excerpt, anchoring movie-watching as a covert probe of higher-order cortex even when dialogue is sparse.
- Sub-category A.2: Electrophysiological reliability during movie viewing
  - [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md): Source-space MEG signals are reliable across viewers in occipital and temporal cortex during silent-visual and audiovisual movie conditions, the closest electrophysiological analogue to the HBN ThePresent setup.
- Sub-category A.3: Cortical timescale hierarchy under continuous narrative
  - [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../collection/language/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md): Maps the hierarchy of temporal receptive windows from auditory cortex (milliseconds) to default-mode regions (minutes), using narrative-scrambling that itself does not transfer to a silent stimulus.

### Category B: Event structure and shot-level segmentation

Treats the continuous stimulus as a sequence of perceptual or narrative events. Directly relevant to shot-locked epoching of ThePresent.

- Sub-category B.1: Behavioural film-cognition foundation
  - [magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md): Provides the behavioural basis for treating shot boundaries as candidate event markers and shows that viewers segment films along edits independent of dialogue.
- Sub-category B.2: Neural event boundaries during movie viewing
  - [benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md): Distinguishes within-event camera cuts from across-event narrative boundaries; the hippocampus responds selectively to the latter, refining what a shot-locked analysis is allowed to claim.
  - [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/language/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md): Default-mode regions reactivate event-specific spatial patterns from movie viewing during free recall, supporting the claim that the brain encodes movies as discrete events.

### Category C: Cross-modal entrainment and audiovisual integration

Explains how oscillatory mechanisms gate sensory processing to expected event onsets. Filtered here so that paradigm-general entrainment claims are separated from speech-bound audiovisual integration.

- Sub-category C.1: Modality-general oscillatory entrainment to attended events
  - [schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md): Delta- and theta-band phase aligns to attended event onsets across modalities, providing the mechanistic frame for shot-onset event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) effects.
- Sub-category C.2: Multisensory binding by neural coherence (review-level framework)
  - [senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md): Transient gamma synchronization and low-frequency phase coupling between modality-specific cortices subserve cross-modal binding; the framework is broader than its mostly audiovisual evidence base.
- Sub-category C.3: Audiovisual speech integration (does not transfer)
  - [vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech](../collection/language/vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech/card.md): Visible mouth movements speed and reduce the auditory N1/P2 event-related potential (ERP), an analysis-by-synthesis effect that requires the auditory speech channel.

### Category D: Silent and wordless storytelling neural correlates

Boundary-survival category. Every entry here was built on visual-only or near-visual-only stimuli and provides direct conceptual transfer to ThePresent.

- Sub-category D.1: Mentalising from silent motion patterns
  - [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md): Silent geometric-shape animations selectively engage medial prefrontal cortex, temporo-parietal junction, and superior temporal sulcus when motion implies social interaction, with no speech required.
  - [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md): Replicates the silent-animation mentalising paradigm in autism, showing reduced social-network engagement; relevant for individual-difference analyses in the HBN sample.
- Sub-category D.2: Silent or visually-driven movie paradigms with reproducible cortical signal
  - [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md): Purpose-built silent abstract animation, used by HBN itself, that improves compliance and produces reliable network-level activity; the closest paradigmatic precedent for ThePresent.
  - [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md): Cross-listed (see A.1); the Hitchcock excerpt is largely visually driven and shows that high-order cortex can be probed from a near-silent narrative.
- Sub-category D.3: Naturalistic-stimulation infrastructure (audio-only foundation, silent-cohort umbrella)
  - [hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t](../collection/language/hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t/card.md): Foundational Studyforrest release; itself audio-only, but the dataset family includes silent-cohort extensions used in subsequent silent-vs-speech contrasts.

### Category E: Story network and default-mode dynamics

Covers the higher-order cortical network most often cited as the substrate of narrative integration. Includes one negative-control reference for the language network proper.

- Sub-category E.1: Default-mode network as narrative integrator
  - [buckner-2008-default-network](../collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md): Authoritative review framing the default-mode network as a stable system supporting internally directed cognition and narrative-frame construction.
  - [simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc](../collection/language/simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc/card.md): Inter-subject functional correlation analysis showing that default-mode connectivity reconfigures dynamically over narrative timescales while sensory cortex stays stimulus-locked.
  - [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md): Framing context, not raw stimulus, drives default-mode response divergence; flags interpretation as a within-stimulus source of variance.
- Sub-category E.2: Language network as a domain-specific control region
  - [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md): Probabilistic atlas of the frontotemporal language network from 800+ precision-fMRI subjects, useful as a region-of-interest mask and as a negative-control region in a silent paradigm.

### Category F: Lexical-semantic and predictive responses to language

Groups effects that index lexical access, semantic prediction, or surprisal. Most entries here are speech- or text-bound; the N400 review is the main bridge to non-linguistic stimuli.

- Sub-category F.1: N400 as a context-sensitive semantic-access component
  - [kutas-2011-n400-review](../collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md): Authoritative review noting that the N400 generalises to pictures and short videos with semantic congruity manipulations, the bridge from lexical to non-linguistic 250-500 ms semantic-access claims.
  - [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md): Graded N400 on articles preceding predictable nouns; demonstrates probabilistic pre-activation but depends on phonological article-form and reading.
- Sub-category F.2: Semantic encoding from naturalistic speech
  - [huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md): Voxelwise word-embedding encoding atlas tiling cortex with semantic clusters; the canonical example of a paradigm whose regressors disappear without spoken transcripts.
- Sub-category F.3: Syntactic and phrase-structure dynamics
  - [brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg](../collection/language/brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg/card.md): Intracranial high-gamma and theta dynamics tracking open-node count during syntactic merge; explicitly reading-based and speech-bound.

### Category G: Language models as regressors in naturalistic neuroimaging

Structural finding: every entry in this category depends on word-level alignment to spoken or written stimuli, and therefore none transfers cleanly to a silent animated stimulus. The category is preserved as the brief specifies, but it is the densest cluster of `transfer-to-silent: no` cards in the corpus, and the ontology marks this explicitly.

- Sub-category G.1: Word-by-word electrophysiology aligned to language-model surprisal
  - [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md): Word-by-word electrocorticography (ECoG) reveals pre-onset prediction, post-onset surprise, and contextual-embedding signatures shared with autoregressive language models.
  - [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md): MEG during audiobook listening separates lexical, syntactic, and semantic surprisal regressors, all derived from large language models with word-onset alignment.
- Sub-category G.2: fMRI encoding from transformer activations
  - [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md): Intermediate transformer layers best predict brain responses to natural narrative across fMRI and MEG datasets; partial-convergence claim is fully speech-bound.
  - [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md): GPT-2 augmented with prediction horizons reveals a cortical hierarchy of prediction timescales during story listening, anchored to spoken-word inputs.
  - [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md): Brain-prediction performance scales log-linearly with language-model parameter count up to 30B during 20+ hours of narrative listening.
- Sub-category G.3: Integrative and reading-based brain-language alignment
  - [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md): Across 43 models, next-word-prediction quality drives brain-score on fMRI, ECoG, and reading-time benchmarks.
  - [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md): Bidirectional analysis using BERT to predict reading fMRI and MEG, with attention-head ablations linking brain prediction to NLP performance.

Structural note on category G: all seven entries land at `transfer-to-silent: no`. The category is included not because it informs the ThePresent analysis, but because it defines an entire class of contemporary naturalistic-neuroimaging methodology that has no counterpart for a wordless stimulus. Its inclusion makes the silent-stimulus boundary explicit at corpus level.

## Cross-cutting tags

### transfer-to-silent

The load-bearing axis of this ontology. Every card carries one of three values.

- `transfer-to-silent: yes` (10 cards): paradigm or framework survives without speech, transcripts, or word-onset alignment.
  - [hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization](../collection/language/hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization/card.md), [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md), [schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md), [magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md), [buckner-2008-default-network](../collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md), [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md), [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md), [benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md)
- `transfer-to-silent: partial` (7 cards): conceptual claim transfers, but the cited operationalisation depends on speech or has only mixed evidence outside speech.
  - [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../collection/language/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md), [kutas-2011-n400-review](../collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md), [senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md), [simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc](../collection/language/simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/language/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md), [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md), [hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t](../collection/language/hanke-2014-studyforrest-7t/card.md)
- `transfer-to-silent: no` (12 cards): paradigm fundamentally requires speech onsets, transcripts, or language-model word-alignment.
  - [huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md), [brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg](../collection/language/brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg/card.md), [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md), [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md), [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md), [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech](../collection/language/vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech/card.md), [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md), [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md), [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md), [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md)
