# Action Strand Ontology

A hierarchical view of the action strand corpus (18 paper-cards) for the Healthy Brain Network (HBN) ThePresent boy-shot vs puppy-shot event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) review. Each leaf links to its paper-card with a one-line role statement. The category tree refactors the brief's 6 categories along the natural joints the corpus revealed: a unified sensorimotor-mirror system cluster spans former biological-motion, mirror-network, and mu-rhythm strands; event-segmentation and temporal-hierarchy work consolidate as one naturalistic-dynamics cluster; an animation-and-abstract-agents cluster gathers stimulus-class results; and a clinical-and-individual-differences cluster pulls autism eye-tracking and mu-suppression evidence across former categories.

## Top-level categories

### Category A: Sensorimotor and mirror system for observed action

Mechanistic account of how observing another agent's movement engages occipito-temporal motion processing and parieto-premotor motor representations, with electroencephalography (EEG) and magnetoencephalography (MEG) oscillatory readouts in alpha-mu (8 to 13 Hz) and beta (15 to 25 Hz) bands.

- Sub-category A.1: Kinematic-form perception and biological motion
  - [johansson-1973-biological-motion](../collection/action/johansson-1973-biological-motion/card.md): Foundational point-light walker paradigm establishing that articulated kinematics alone, without surface form, suffice for agent perception.
  - [saygin-2007-sts-lesions](../collection/action/saygin-2007-sts-lesions/card.md): Voxel-based lesion-symptom mapping showing that intact biological-motion perception requires both posterior superior temporal sulcus (STS) and ventral premotor cortex.

- Sub-category A.2: Parieto-premotor action observation network (mechanism)
  - [rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system](../collection/action/rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system/card.md): Annual-Review consolidation of macaque area F5 and inferior parietal lobule (IPL) mirror-neuron physiology and the homologous human inferior frontal gyrus (IFG) and IPL network.
  - [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md): Extension of the mirror network to facial expression, imitation, empathy, and emotion contagion via insula and amygdala projections.

- Sub-category A.3: Predictive-coding reformulation
  - [kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md): Recasts the mirror system as a hierarchical Bayesian generative model over intention, goal, and kinematics, predicting that mirror responses scale with prediction error.

- Sub-category A.4: Mu and beta oscillatory readouts of action observation
  - [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md): Review framing the EEG mu rhythm (8 to 13 Hz over electrodes C3, Cz, C4) as a non-invasive proxy for human mirror-system engagement.
  - [hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md): MEG demonstration that primary motor cortex (M1) is activated during passive hand-action observation via 15 to 25 Hz rolandic rebound suppression (31 to 46% of execution-related suppression).

### Category B: Event segmentation and naturalistic narrative dynamics

Mechanism and timing of how the brain parses continuous experience into discrete events, with naturalistic movie and story stimuli driving cross-subject reliable responses across a hierarchy of temporal receptive windows.

- Sub-category B.1: Event Segmentation Theory and behavioral foundations
  - [zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md): Defines Event Segmentation Theory (EST); event boundaries arise at moments of high prediction error in working event models.

- Sub-category B.2: Neural correlates of event boundaries in naturalistic narrative
  - [speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries](../collection/action/speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries/card.md): Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) of passive story listening; posterior cingulate, MT+, and posterior STS show transient boundary-locked activity.
  - [baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md): Hidden Markov Model (HMM) recovers a hierarchy of event boundaries from Sherlock-movie fMRI; hippocampal boundary signals predict subsequent free recall.

- Sub-category B.3: Temporal-receptive-window hierarchy and intersubject reliability
  - [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../collection/action/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md): Topographic mapping of temporal receptive windows (TRWs) via narrated-story scrambling at four timescales; default-mode regions integrate over tens of seconds.
  - [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md): Sherlock-movie fMRI; default-mode regions carry event-specific patterns shared across participants and reactivated at recall.
  - [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md): Trends-in-Cognitive-Sciences review of intersubject correlation (ISC); positions animation on the naturalness gradient between abstract geometric stimuli and live-action film.

### Category C: Animation and abstract-agent processing

Cinematic animation and Heider-Simmel-tradition geometric stimuli as probes of agent and intention perception, the primary stimulus-class category for ThePresent.

- Sub-category C.1: Heider-Simmel and intentional-motion animation
  - [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md): Positron emission tomography (PET) shows that triangle animations with theory-of-mind motion engage medial prefrontal cortex, posterior STS, and temporal poles, even without realistic social form.

- Sub-category C.2: Naturalness gradient and the position of animation
  - [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md): Animation is intermediate on the naturalness gradient; intermediate-naturalness stimuli still elicit reliable cross-subject cortical responses (cross-listed with Category B.3).

### Category D: Comparative and specialized social-interaction circuitry

Dedicated cortical machinery for processing observed agent-agent interaction, separable from single-agent action perception.

- Sub-category D.1: Macaque social-interaction network
  - [sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network](../collection/action/sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network/card.md): Awake-macaque fMRI reveals a posterior STS, anterior STS, and medial prefrontal network selective for two-agent social interaction over single-agent action and object motion, motivating exclusion of two-agent shots from the locked boy-only vs puppy-only contrast.

### Category E: Clinical and individual-differences modulation

Autism and continuous social-skill variation as moderators of agent perception, biological-motion orienting, and mu-rhythm engagement.

- Sub-category E.1: Autism and biological-motion orienting in development
  - [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md): Toddlers with autism orient to audiovisual contingency rather than upright biological motion, indicating early redirection of attention from agentive kinematics.

- Sub-category E.2: Autism and naturalistic gaze allocation
  - [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md): Adolescents with autism fixate eyes 50% as often and mouths twice as often as typically developing controls during emotionally evocative live-action movie viewing; eye-fixation time predicts social competence.

- Sub-category E.3: Continuous social-skill variation in mu suppression
  - [oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md): EEG mu suppression during action observation correlates with self-reported social-skill measures across neurotypical adults, supporting an individual-differences extension of the mirror-system readout.

### Animation-relevant subset (key for ThePresent applicability)

These cards directly address animated, cartoon, or abstract-agent stimuli, or position animation on a stimulus-naturalness gradient. They establish the chain from triangle animations to full-character Pixar shorts and motivate why social-cognition and event-segmentation networks should engage during ThePresent.

- `animation-relevant`: [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md) (Heider-Simmel triangle animations recruit mentalizing network), [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md) (animation as intermediate point on the naturalness gradient).
- `naturalistic-narrative-tradition` (live-action narrative neuroscience that the animation paradigm extends): [baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md), [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../collection/action/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md), [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md).
