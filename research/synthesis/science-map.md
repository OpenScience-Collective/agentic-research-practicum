# Science Map

Theme-by-theme inventory of the analytic and methodological themes that the four-strand corpus addresses for the Healthy Brain Network electroencephalography (HBN-EEG) ThePresent boy-shot vs puppy-shot event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) project. Themes cut across the psychophysics, action, language, and emotion strands; per-strand category structure lives in the ontology files. Every concrete claim cites the establishing paper-card; open questions surface contradictions across strands.

Abbreviations defined on first use: ERSP (event-related spectral perturbation), inter-subject correlation (ISC), default-mode network (DMN), theory of mind (ToM), temporo-parietal junction (TPJ), superior temporal sulcus (STS), log luminance ratio (LLR), event-related desynchronization (ERD), fixation-related potential (FRP), independent component analysis (ICA), hidden Markov model (HMM).

## Theme 1: Inter-subject correlation as a stimulus-locked reliability metric

**Defining works**: [hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md), [dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md), [dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc](../collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2014-audience-preferences-isc/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md), [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md), [hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization](../collection/language/hasson-2004-intersubject-synchronization/card.md), [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md), [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md), [kaneshiro-2021-music-eeg-isc](../collection/psychophysics/kaneshiro-2021-music-eeg-isc/card.md), [cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives](../collection/psychophysics/cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives/card.md), [schmalzle-2020-coupled-brains](../collection/emotion/schmalzle-2020-coupled-brains/card.md), [kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri](../collection/emotion/kauttonen-2015-cinematic-fmri/card.md), [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md).

ISC, the across-viewer agreement of stimulus-locked neural responses, is the most heavily represented analytic theme in the corpus. It originated in functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) free-viewing and migrated to EEG via correlated component analysis, then to magnetoencephalography (MEG), peripheral physiology, and audience-prediction settings. Across the corpus, ISC is treated both as a quality control (reliability of stimulus-locked variance) and as a dependent measure (engagement, captivation, memorability, emotion). The shot-onset epoching and cross-subject cluster statistics planned for ThePresent ERSP analysis sit inside this paradigm.

**Open questions**: Is shot-locked ISC at the 0-500 ms ERSP scale a different signal from whole-stimulus ISC? Does ISC track low-level features (luminance, motion energy) versus higher-level narrative content, and in what proportion ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md) suggests motion dominates occipital ISC, while [nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md) attributes midline ISC to emotional content)? Does the developmental ISC peak in childhood reported by [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md) reflect attention or content effects? Within-subject framing manipulations split DMN ISC across groups in [yeshurun-2017-same-story-different](../collection/language/yeshurun-2017-same-story-different/card.md), raising whether passive viewing in a heterogeneous developmental sample even has a single ISC.

## Theme 2: Event boundaries and segmentation

**Defining works**: [zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md), [speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries](../collection/action/speer-2007-narrative-event-boundaries/card.md), [baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md), [benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md), [magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md), [lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows](../collection/action/lerner-2011-temporal-receptive-windows/card.md).

The corpus treats event segmentation as the bridge between continuous stimulation and discrete trial-like analyses. Event Segmentation Theory (EST) frames boundaries as moments of high prediction error; HMM-based fMRI methods recover event states from neural activity; hippocampal responses dissociate event boundaries from within-event camera cuts; intracranial EEG shows shot-cut and saccade-locked responses across the whole brain. The ThePresent shot-onset paradigm is a fast-grained instance of event segmentation, with 56 shots but fewer narrative event boundaries.

**Open questions**: Within-event camera cuts produce minimal hippocampal response in fMRI ([benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor](../collection/language/benyakov-2018-hippocampal-film-editor/card.md)) but drive whole-brain intracranial EEG transients ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)). Whether the EEG event-related response at within-event shot boundaries should be treated as signal or as nuisance is unresolved. Whether ThePresent's 56 shots map onto a small number of EST boundaries or onto 56 fast-grained events is also unsettled.

## Theme 3: Naturalistic stimuli and the naturalness gradient

**Defining works**: [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md), [hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision](../collection/psychophysics/hasson-2004-isc-natural-vision/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md), [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [naci-2014-suspenseful-movie](../collection/language/naci-2014-suspenseful-movie/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md), [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies](../collection/psychophysics/dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies/card.md).

Naturalness is treated as a continuum: from controlled gratings, to static photographs, to abstract animations (Heider-Simmel triangles, Inscapes), to character animation (Pixar shorts including Partly Cloudy and ThePresent), to live-action movies (Sherlock, Hitchcock excerpts, Forrest Gump). Gaze coherence, ISC magnitude, and network engagement scale along this continuum. ThePresent sits roughly between the abstract-animation and live-action ends.

**Open questions**: Whether animation evokes the same low-level driver hierarchy (motion > luminance > contrast per [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)) as live-action; whether the "naturalness gradient" of [hasson-2010-natural-stimulation](../collection/action/hasson-2010-natural-stimulation/card.md) generalizes to children's gaze and engagement signatures.

## Theme 4: Low-level feature regressors and bottom-up drivers

**Defining works**: [adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy](../collection/psychophysics/adelson-bergen-1985-spatiotemporal-energy/card.md), [bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt](../collection/psychophysics/bartels-zeki-2008-natural-vision-mt/card.md), [nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction](../collection/psychophysics/nishimoto-2011-movie-reconstruction/card.md), [carandini-heeger-2011-normalization](../collection/psychophysics/carandini-heeger-2011-normalization/card.md), [tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology](../collection/psychophysics/tobimatsu-celesia-2006-vep-pathophysiology/card.md), [hubel-wiesel-1962-receptive-fields-cat-v1](../collection/psychophysics/hubel-wiesel-1962-receptive-fields-cat-v1/card.md), [bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes](../collection/psychophysics/bell-sejnowski-1997-ica-natural-scenes/card.md), [simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats](../collection/psychophysics/simoncelli-olshausen-2001-natural-image-stats/card.md), [born-bradley-2005-mt-mst](../collection/psychophysics/born-bradley-2005-mt-mst/card.md), [riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax](../collection/psychophysics/riesenhuber-poggio-1999-hmax/card.md), [khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it](../collection/psychophysics/khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it/card.md), [kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii](../collection/psychophysics/kummerer-2016-deepgaze-ii/card.md), [crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md).

The corpus traces a chain from V1 receptive fields (Hubel-Wiesel), through divisive normalization and natural-image statistics, through spatiotemporal energy and HMAX, to deep convolutional networks for early visual cortex and saliency. These models supply candidate continuous regressors for naturalistic-stimulus EEG; LLR per shot is the simplest such regressor and is included as a covariate of no interest in the planned ThePresent group-level generalized linear model (GLM).

**Open questions**: Whether luminance change (LLR) is the right low-level regressor or whether motion energy is a stronger driver ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md) argues motion > luminance for occipitoparietal cortex). Whether deep-CNN visual features add explanatory power beyond classical motion-energy in EEG (the corpus has fMRI-side evidence ([khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it](../collection/psychophysics/khaligh-razavi-kriegeskorte-2014-deep-cnn-it/card.md)) but no EEG demonstration). Whether emotion-tuned visual-cortex representations ([kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md)) appear in early-latency EEG.

## Theme 5: Time-resolved EEG/MEG to dynamic naturalistic input

**Defining works**: [dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement](../collection/psychophysics/dmochowski-2012-correlated-eeg-engagement/card.md), [lalor-foxe-2010-natural-speech-trf](../collection/psychophysics/lalor-foxe-2010-natural-speech-trf/card.md), [crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox](../collection/psychophysics/crosse-2016-mtrf-toolbox/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md), [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md), [hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative](../collection/psychophysics/ki-kelly-parra-2016-attention-eeg-narrative/card.md), [cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives](../collection/psychophysics/cohen-parra-2016-memorable-narratives/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md).

EEG and MEG approaches to continuous naturalistic stimuli use temporal response functions (TRFs), correlated component analysis, and band-limited ISC. Most published demonstrations use speech (Lalor, Foxe) or audiovisual narrative (Dmochowski, Cohen, Madsen). Visual-only naturalistic-EEG with shot-locked ERSP is sparse; intracranial EEG with film-cut regressors ([nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md)) is the closest analogue.

**Open questions**: Whether TRF and ERSP analyses converge on the same cortical sources during silent animation; whether 100 Hz sampling (the local ThePresent dataset) caps any beta-band reliability claims (a 500 Hz validation pass is planned but not yet executed).

## Theme 6: Mu rhythm, sensorimotor desynchronization, and action observation

**Defining works**: [hari-1998-mep-action-observation](../collection/action/hari-1998-mep-action-observation/card.md), [pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror](../collection/action/pineda-2005-mu-rhythm-mirror/card.md), [oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md), [rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system](../collection/action/rizzolatti-2004-mirror-neuron-system/card.md), [kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md), [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md), [saygin-2007-sts-lesions](../collection/action/saygin-2007-sts-lesions/card.md), [johansson-1973-biological-motion](../collection/action/johansson-1973-biological-motion/card.md).

Mu-rhythm desynchronization (8-13 Hz) and beta-band rebound suppression (15-25 Hz) over rolandic cortex during action observation are the EEG/MEG signatures most directly compatible with the 0-500 ms ERSP window planned for ThePresent. Mu suppression magnitude correlates with self-reported social skill ([oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism](../collection/action/oberman-2006-mu-mirror-autism/card.md)). Lesion mapping ([saygin-2007-sts-lesions](../collection/action/saygin-2007-sts-lesions/card.md)) places posterior STS and ventral premotor cortex as causally necessary nodes.

**Open questions**: Mirror-neuron interpretation of mu suppression has been challenged outside the corpus, but the corpus does not include strong critiques such as Hickok 2009 (cited only by [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md) in passing). Whether mu suppression to observed animal action (puppy, not human hand) is graded by species or by kinematic similarity is untested in the cards available. The reliability of mu suppression in pediatric cohorts and across the autism spectrum within HBN is open.

## Theme 7: Affective dynamics and emotion-driven engagement

**Defining works**: [saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md), [nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony](../collection/emotion/nummenmaa-2012-emotion-synchrony/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md), [cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions](../collection/emotion/cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions/card.md), [lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md), [sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis](../collection/emotion/sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis/card.md), [ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads](../collection/emotion/ledoux-2010-amygdala-many-roads/card.md), [etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion](../collection/emotion/etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion/card.md), [wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature](../collection/emotion/wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature/card.md), [schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md), [davidson-2000-affective-style](../collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md), [coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry](../collection/emotion/coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry/card.md), [reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md).

Affective dynamics in cinematic stimuli are studied through MVPA decoding of discrete emotions, emotion-modulated ISC, distributed-network meta-analyses, frontal alpha asymmetry, and alpha-band desynchronization. The corpus connects emotion to spectral signatures via two routes: power decreases in occipital alpha during emotional pictures ([schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md)) and frontal asymmetry as an approach-withdrawal index ([davidson-2000-affective-style](../collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md)).

**Open questions**: Frontal alpha asymmetry as an EEG affective marker is supported by [davidson-2000-affective-style](../collection/emotion/davidson-2000-affective-style/card.md) and [coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry](../collection/emotion/coan-allen-2004-frontal-asymmetry/card.md) but partly retracted by [reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry](../collection/emotion/reznik-allen-2018-frontal-asymmetry/card.md), which finds smaller effects and reliability concerns. The 500 ms window in ThePresent is much shorter than the seconds-to-minutes window in which classical asymmetry has been validated; whether asymmetry is an appropriate measure at sub-second timescales is a live methodological tension. Discrete-emotion versus dimensional-emotion taxonomies ([saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md) versus [cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions](../collection/emotion/cowen-keltner-2017-27-emotions/card.md)) disagree on the granularity.

## Theme 8: Social cognition, theory of mind, and mentalizing during movie watching

**Defining works**: [castelli-2000-animations-mentalising](../collection/language/castelli-2000-animations-mentalising/card.md), [castelli-2000-heider-simmel](../collection/action/castelli-2000-heider-simmel/card.md), [castelli-2002-autism-animations](../collection/language/castelli-2002-autism-animations/card.md), [saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom](../collection/emotion/saxe-kanwisher-2003-tpj-tom/card.md), [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition](../collection/emotion/mar-2011-narrative-social-cognition/card.md), [tamir-2015-fiction-default-network](../collection/emotion/tamir-2015-fiction-default-network/card.md), [zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural](../collection/emotion/zaki-ochsner-2012-empathy-neural/card.md), [singer-2004-empathy-pain](../collection/emotion/singer-2004-empathy-pain/card.md), [iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy](../collection/action/iacoboni-2009-mirror-empathy/card.md), [sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network](../collection/action/sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network/card.md), [buckner-2008-default-network](../collection/language/buckner-2008-default-network/card.md), [simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc](../collection/language/simony-2016-dmn-narrative-fc/card.md).

Across strands, movie watching is treated as a probe of mentalizing networks (medial prefrontal cortex, TPJ, posterior STS, temporal poles) and of an experience-sharing branch (anterior insula, anterior cingulate) that supports empathy. Silent-animation paradigms (Heider-Simmel triangles, Pixar shorts) demonstrate that motion alone, without speech or human form, is sufficient to engage these networks. Developmental-fMRI work with Partly Cloudy ([richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md)) localizes ToM and pain networks in children as young as three.

**Open questions**: Whether single-agent shots (boy-only or puppy-only, the locked Contrast 1 in ThePresent) engage the social-interaction-specific network of [sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network](../collection/action/sliwa-2017-macaque-social-network/card.md), or whether that network is reserved for two-agent reciprocal exchange; whether DMN ISC during silent animation tracks mentalizing or general engagement.

## Theme 9: Language models and transcript-based regressors as comparator

**Defining works**: [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms](../collection/language/caucheteux-2022-brains-algorithms/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture](../collection/language/schrimpf-2021-neural-architecture/card.md), [toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain](../collection/language/toneva-2019-interpreting-nlp-brain/card.md), [antonello-2023-scaling-laws](../collection/language/antonello-2023-scaling-laws/card.md), [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md), [huth-2016-semantic-atlas](../collection/language/huth-2016-semantic-atlas/card.md), [kutas-2011-n400-review](../collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md), [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md), [brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg](../collection/language/brennan-2017-phrase-structure-meg/card.md), [lipkin-2022-language-atlas](../collection/language/lipkin-2022-language-atlas/card.md).

Transformer-based language model regressors aligned to spoken or read transcripts are the dominant high-level regressor framework in 2020-2023 naturalistic neuroimaging, but the entire family does not transfer to silent animation: ThePresent has no transcript, so per-word surprisal, contextual embeddings, and N400-style semantic-prediction metrics have no analogue. The corpus includes these works as boundary cases that establish what cannot transfer.

**Open questions**: Whether vision-side analogues (visual-feature regressors from deep CNNs, EmoNet-style emotion-schema regressors, scene-difference embeddings) can play the same role for silent video as language-model surprisal does for speech. The corpus does not yet contain a single paper-card that uses a multimodal vision model as a regressor for naturalistic-EEG ERSP.

## Theme 10: Audiovisual integration and cross-modal coupling

**Defining works**: [vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech](../collection/language/vanwassenhove-2005-visual-speech/card.md), [senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence](../collection/language/senkowski-2008-crossmodal-coherence/card.md), [schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md), [magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md), [lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency](../collection/language/lankinen-2014-meg-movie-consistency/card.md).

Audiovisual coupling, gamma-band cross-modal synchronization, and low-frequency phase alignment to attended events are the substrate of multisensory naturalistic-stimulus processing. ThePresent is silent in stretches; the audiovisual-integration findings are mostly comparator material rather than direct dependencies, except for [magliano-2011-continuity-editing](../collection/language/magliano-2011-continuity-editing/card.md), which is paradigm-general for film cuts and transfers to silent narrative.

**Open questions**: Whether silent animation with non-speech sound (ThePresent has a music score) recruits the same low-frequency entrainment described by [schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations](../collection/language/schroeder-2009-low-frequency-oscillations/card.md). The corpus does not isolate the silent-stimulus contribution from the music-score contribution.

## Theme 11: Free-viewing EEG with eye-movement coregistration

**Defining works**: [dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading](../collection/psychophysics/dimigen-2011-frp-natural-reading/card.md), [ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction](../collection/psychophysics/ploechl-2012-eye-eeg-artifact-correction/card.md), [dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md), [dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies](../collection/psychophysics/dorr-2010-eye-movements-natural-movies/card.md), [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md), [madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync](../collection/psychophysics/madsen-parra-2022-brain-heart-eyes-sync/card.md), [nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts](../collection/psychophysics/nentwich-2023-semantic-novelty-cuts/card.md).

The free-viewing EEG literature establishes the FRP framework, ICA-based eye-artifact correction, and regression-deconvolution for overlapping events. Eye-tracking during movie viewing is an established tool for indexing attention allocation and individual differences. HBN-EEG R3 lacks a synchronous eye tracker; the corpus thus identifies a methodological boundary that limits the planned analysis.

**Open questions**: Whether shot-locked saccade dynamics confound shot-onset EEG analysis when eye tracking is unavailable; whether ICA-based artifact rejection is sufficient without time-resolved gaze data; whether the regression-deconvolution framework of [dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg](../collection/psychophysics/dimigen-ehinger-2021-deconvolution-eye-eeg/card.md) extends from time-domain ERPs to time-frequency ERSPs.

## Theme 12: Pet, animal, and baby-schema affective response

**Defining works**: [stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md), [glocker-2009-baby-schema](../collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md), [borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md).

Baby-schema (Kindchenschema) features (large eyes, round face) parametrically modulate cuteness ratings and ventral-striatal reward-system activation in adults; children as young as three show the same parametric cuteness effect for human and animal (puppy, kitten) faces. Stoeckel mothers viewing own child versus own dog activate a partly shared affiliative network with reward-region selectivity for child and fusiform selectivity for dog. This is the smallest theme in the corpus, with three cards, and it is the load-bearing primary-source set for the puppy-shot interpretation.

**Open questions**: Whether neural baby-schema response to animal faces in children (the precise developmental and species crossover) has been established. The corpus contains the behavioral developmental anchor ([borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md)) and adult fMRI ([glocker-2009-baby-schema](../collection/emotion/glocker-2009-baby-schema/card.md), [stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog](../collection/emotion/stoeckel-2014-mother-child-dog/card.md)) but no child-cohort EEG or fMRI study of pet-evoked affect.

## Theme 13: Developmental neuroimaging in cinematic paradigms

**Defining works**: [richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development](../collection/emotion/richardson-saxe-2018-social-brain-development/card.md), [petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos](../collection/emotion/petroni-cohen-2018-isc-naturalistic-videos/card.md), [vanderwal-2015-inscapes](../collection/language/vanderwal-2015-inscapes/card.md), [borgi-2014-baby-schema-children](../collection/emotion/borgi-2014-baby-schema-children/card.md), [klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism](../collection/action/klin-2002-visual-fixation-autism/card.md), [klin-2009-biological-motion-autism](../collection/action/klin-2009-biological-motion-autism/card.md).

Across the corpus only six cards primarily report on a child or developmental cohort, and they cluster around fMRI of Pixar shorts (Partly Cloudy), Inscapes for fMRI compliance, EEG ISC across ages 6-44, and behavioral or eye-tracking work in autism. Most other cards explicitly carry developmental-justification caveats stating that adult findings have unknown transfer to ages 5-21.

**Open questions**: The corpus contains zero cards reporting EEG ERSP at shot onsets in a developmental cohort viewing animation; this is a gap rather than a contradiction (see gap analysis).

## Theme 14: Multivariate distributed signatures versus regional locationism

**Defining works**: [lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md), [saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions](../collection/emotion/saarimaki-2016-discrete-emotions/card.md), [wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature](../collection/emotion/wager-2013-neurologic-pain-signature/card.md), [kragel-2019-emonet-visual](../collection/emotion/kragel-2019-emonet-visual/card.md), [chen-2017-shared-memories](../collection/action/chen-2017-shared-memories/card.md).

Distributed multivariate signatures (NPS, EmoNet, MVPA decoders, shared-memory pattern reinstatement) are the corpus's response to the failure of strict regional locationism for emotion and high-level cognition. For EEG independent component clusters, this argues for distributed cluster contrasts rather than single-IC decoding.

**Open questions**: Whether MVPA signatures derived from fMRI transfer to EEG IC space; whether IC clustering during ThePresent should mirror fMRI parcellation or follow the EEG-internal AMICA structure; whether the constructionist distributed-network account ([lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis](../collection/emotion/lindquist-2012-emotion-meta-analysis/card.md)) and the basic-emotion locationist account ([sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis](../collection/emotion/sergerie-2008-amygdala-meta-analysis/card.md), [etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion](../collection/emotion/etkin-2011-acc-mpfc-emotion/card.md)) can be discriminated at EEG IC scale during a 0 to 500 ms window in ThePresent given the available sample size.

## Theme 15: Predictive processing across observation, perception, and language

**Defining works**: [kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror](../collection/action/kilner-2007-predictive-coding-mirror/card.md), [zacks-2007-event-segmentation](../collection/action/zacks-2007-event-segmentation/card.md), [heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions](../collection/language/heilbron-2022-hierarchy-predictions/card.md), [caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy](../collection/language/caucheteux-2023-predictive-hierarchy/card.md), [goldstein-2022-shared-computational](../collection/language/goldstein-2022-shared-computational/card.md), [delong-2005-n400-prediction](../collection/language/delong-2005-n400-prediction/card.md), [kutas-2011-n400-review](../collection/language/kutas-2011-n400-review/card.md), [carandini-heeger-2011-normalization](../collection/psychophysics/carandini-heeger-2011-normalization/card.md), [schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion](../collection/emotion/schubring-schupp-2023-alpha-emotion/card.md).

Predictive coding appears as a unifying framework in the corpus: the mirror system as hierarchical Bayesian inference over goal and intention, event segmentation as prediction-error-driven boundary detection, language-model surprisal as a regressor, and alpha-beta interactions as top-down expectations. ThePresent's narrative structure (gift, frustration, reveal) makes prediction error a candidate driver of shot-locked spectral perturbations.

**Open questions**: Whether prediction error in the visual-narrative domain produces the same band-limited signature as in language; whether shot transitions are best modeled as boundary moments (high prediction error) or as continuous low-level features; whether HMM-based event decoding from EEG (analogous to [baldassano-2017-event-boundaries](../collection/action/baldassano-2017-event-boundaries/card.md)) is feasible at the sample sizes available.
