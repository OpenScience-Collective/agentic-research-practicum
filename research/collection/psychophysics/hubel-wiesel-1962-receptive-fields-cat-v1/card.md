## TL;DR
Foundational single-unit work in cat primary visual cortex (V1) that introduced the simple-cell / complex-cell hierarchy, columnar organization of orientation preference, and ocular-dominance columns, providing the receptive-field vocabulary that all later vision research, from Adelson-Bergen energy models to deep convolutional neural networks, builds upon.

## Summary
Hubel and Wiesel recorded from individual cells in cat V1 and discovered two stereotyped classes: simple cells whose receptive fields had elongated, oriented ON and OFF subregions (responding selectively to bars or edges of a particular orientation and position), and complex cells whose orientation tuning was preserved over larger areas of visual space, suggesting position invariance. They mapped how preferred orientation rotated systematically across the cortical surface (orientation columns), and how cells driven primarily by the left eye, right eye, or both formed ocular-dominance columns. They proposed a feedforward hierarchical model in which complex cells receive convergent input from simple cells of the same preferred orientation but different spatial positions. The paper anchors all later receptive-field-based theories of vision.

## Relevance to the review
Background only. The boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP project on "The Present" does not measure single-cell activity, and the human electroencephalography (EEG) signal is many orders of magnitude removed from V1 simple-cell receptive fields. This paper is included as the historical anchor for the receptive-field framing that runs through Adelson-Bergen, HMAX, and deep-CNN entries in this strand. Phase 3 synthesis may cite it to ground the claim that early visual responses to shot onsets reflect oriented-edge processing.

## Notable details
- Cat V1; extracellular single-unit recordings.
- Simple cells: oriented, position-specific, ON/OFF substructure.
- Complex cells: orientation-tuned, position-invariant within a larger field.
- Orientation-preference columns and ocular-dominance columns described.
- Hierarchical convergence proposed as the simple-to-complex computation.
- Foundational; Nobel Prize work.

## Open questions / limitations
- Cat, not human; primate V1 differs in column geometry and size.
- No naturalistic-stimulus characterization; receptive fields are mapped with bars and edges.
- Hierarchical proposal has been refined many times since (e.g., gain control, lateral interaction).
- The paper predates ERP/EEG entirely; bridging from single-cell to scalp signals requires later work.
