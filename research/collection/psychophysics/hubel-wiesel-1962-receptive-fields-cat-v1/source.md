## Citation only

This paper has no formal abstract in PubMed. The work first describes:

- Single-cell recordings from cat primary visual cortex (V1).
- Definition of "simple" cells with edge-like, oriented, and position-specific receptive fields, organized into ON/OFF subfields.
- Definition of "complex" cells with orientation tuning that is invariant to position within a larger receptive field.
- Columnar organization: orientation-preference columns running perpendicular to the cortical surface; ocular-dominance columns from monocular through binocular cells.
- Hierarchical proposal: complex cells receive convergent input from simple cells of the same preferred orientation.

Hubel and Wiesel were awarded the 1981 Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in part for this and related work.
