## TL;DR
Inter-subject correlation (ISC) during naturalistic-video viewing emerges in electroencephalography (EEG), gaze position, pupil size, and heart rate, but not in respiration or head movements; ISC magnitudes co-modulate with attentional state across all four predictive signals and predict later recall, supporting a unified theory in which ISC reflects effective cognitive processing of a shared stimulus relayed through brain-body couplings.

## Summary
Madsen and Parra ran an attentive-versus-distracted manipulation while subjects watched informative videos, recording EEG plus eye tracking, ECG, respiration, and head movement. They computed ISC for each signal and showed that EEG, gaze, pupil, heart rate, and saccade-rate signals all show robust ISC that co-modulates across modalities and predicts post-experiment recall, while respiration and head movement do not. Within-subject coupling between brain and each physiological signal mirrors the ISC pattern: signals that show ISC are also the signals that show within-subject brain-body coupling. The authors argue that ISC arises whenever cognitive processing of a shared stimulus engages a brain-body channel; signals without such a channel do not show ISC.

## Relevance to the review
Direct dependency for interpreting the boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP project. The HBN-EEG R3 ThePresent task has fixed stimulus, multiple subjects, and "passive viewing" (with no explicit task), so ISC at the EEG level is the operating regime. Madsen and Parra clarify that ISC magnitude is itself an attentional readout, which means any boy-versus-puppy condition difference in ERSP should be checked against a condition difference in shot-locked ISC. The peripheral-physiology results also flag that gaze and pupil are jointly synchronized; we lack eye tracking on R3, but the cross-modal predictions sit in the background of any EEG-only ISC interpretation.

## Notable details
- ISC observed in EEG, gaze position, pupil size, heart rate, saccade rate; absent in respiration and head movement.
- ISC is co-modulated across the predictive signals: same attentional manipulation moves them together.
- Brain-body within-subject coupling pattern parallels the ISC presence pattern: this is the proposed mechanistic explanation.
- 60 subjects across multiple stimulus conditions; informative-video stimuli.
- Open Access (CC BY 4.0).

## Open questions / limitations
- HBN-EEG R3 has no synchronous eye, ECG, or pupil data, so we cannot replicate the multi-modal co-modulation here.
- The attentional manipulation in this paper is binary; the natural variation in attention across the developmental HBN sample is uncontrolled.
- ISC is computed at the whole-stimulus level; the paper does not report shot-locked ISC, which is what "The Present" Phase 5 needs.
- "The Present" is animated and silent for stretches; the cognitive-processing claim was tested on informative speech-bearing video and may need recalibration.
