## TL;DR
Canonical review of primate area MT/V5: anatomy, direction- and speed-tuning, integration of component into pattern motion, and the causal microstimulation evidence linking single MT neurons to perceptual decisions about motion direction.

## Summary
Born and Bradley synthesize three decades of MT physiology to answer "what does MT do?" They detail the area's input from V1 magnocellular layers and direct K-stream contributions, its near-uniform direction selectivity, the columnar organization of preferred direction, and the opponent-suppression motif that makes MT respond preferentially to motion energy of a single direction. They review classical Newsome microstimulation experiments showing that MT activity biases monkey choices in motion-discrimination tasks, and the integration of component motion into pattern motion at the MT/MST level. The review's framing of MT as the cortical implementation of an Adelson-Bergen-like spatiotemporal energy filter is the standard reference for motion-energy regressors in human imaging.

## Relevance to the review
"The Present" is computer-animated, so motion is a constant feature of the stimulus and shot transitions create large global-flow changes (camera moves, cuts) that activate MT-class generators in human visual cortex. Boy versus puppy condition contrasts are not motion contrasts per se, but global motion energy is a candidate confound that the log luminance ratio (LLR) regressor alone does not absorb. Phase 3 may need a motion-energy regressor (Adelson-Bergen output integrated over each shot's first 500 ms) to disentangle MT-driven event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) from social-content effects. This review is the textbook entry point for that argument.

## Notable details
- MT direction tuning is roughly columnar; opponent suppression is the defining microcircuit feature.
- Speed tuning is approximately log-Gaussian, with peak speeds spanning 1-128 deg/s.
- Microstimulation of direction columns shifts behavioral choices; this is the strongest causal evidence for an MT-perception link.
- MT pattern-motion neurons appear later in the response and integrate component motions.
- Human MT/V5+ is functionally homologous but spatially less segregated than macaque MT.

## Open questions / limitations
- The review is single-unit-centric; how MT activity translates to scalp electroencephalography (EEG) and MT contributions to specific event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) frequency bands is not the focus.
- Naturalistic-motion (animation, film) responses are minimally covered; the dominant evidence is from drifting gratings and random-dot kinematograms.
- The interaction of MT with attentional gain (relevant when boy versus puppy attracts differential attention) is mentioned but not the core focus.
