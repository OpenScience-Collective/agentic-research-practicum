# Inter-Subject EEG Correlation Reflects Time-Varying Engagement with Natural Music

**Authors:** Blair Kaneshiro, Duc T. Nguyen, Anthony M. Norcia, Jacek P. Dmochowski, Jonathan Berger
**Venue:** bioRxiv preprint 2021.04.14.439913 (posted August 2021)
**DOI:** 10.1101/2021.04.14.439913

## Abstract

Musical engagement can be conceptualized through various activities, modes of listening, and listener states; among these is a state of focused engagement. Recent research has reported that this state can be indexed by the inter-subject correlation (ISC) of electroencephalography (EEG) responses to a shared naturalistic stimulus. While statistically significant ISC has been reported during music listening, these reports have considered only correlations computed across entire excerpts and do not provide insights into time-varying engagement. Here we present the first EEG-ISC investigation of time-varying engagement within a musical work. From a sample of 23 adult musicians who listened to a cello concerto movement, we find varying levels of ISC throughout the excerpt. In particular, significant ISC is observed during periods of musical tension build to climactic highpoints, but not at the highpoints themselves. In addition, we find that a control stimulus retaining the envelope characteristics of a musical work intact, but little other temporal structure, also elicits significant neural correlation, though to a lesser extent than the original excerpt. In all, our findings shed light on the temporal dynamics of listener engagement during music listening, establish connections between salient musical events and EEG ISC, and clarify the specific listener states that are indexed by this measure.

## Coverage

- 23 adult musicians; 60-channel EEG; cello concerto excerpt plus envelope-matched control.
- Time-resolved EEG ISC computed via correlated-component analysis (Dmochowski 2012).
- Tension-build periods drive ISC; highpoints themselves do not.
- Envelope-only control still drives reduced ISC, isolating low-level versus structural contributions.
