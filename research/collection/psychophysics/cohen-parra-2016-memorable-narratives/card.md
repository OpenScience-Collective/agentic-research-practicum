## TL;DR
Cross-subject electroencephalographic (EEG) synchrony during encoding of biographical narratives predicts memory three weeks later, and the predictive synchrony is largely supramodal (present whether the stimulus is auditory, visual, or audiovisual), suggesting reliability of stimulus-evoked activity is the operative mnemonic substrate.

## Summary
Cohen and Parra recorded EEG from 72 subjects while they encoded biographical narratives presented auditorily, visually (paired animation), or audiovisually. Three weeks later they tested verbatim memory for content delivered in the auditory channel. Inter-subject correlation (ISC) computed via the Dmochowski 2012 correlated-component analysis predicted memory performance, and crucially the predictive component of ISC was largely independent of presentation modality, indicating a supramodal neural reliability signature for memorable encoding. Adding a temporally incongruent visual stream still improved memory for auditory content, consistent with the interpretation that visual context drives multisensory salience and the resulting reliability boost rather than direct integration of incongruent information.

## Relevance to the review
Tangential but important context. "The Present" is short and the HBN-EEG R3 protocol does not test memory, so this paper is not a direct dependency. However, the supramodal finding informs our prior over which IC clusters should show condition-locked reliability: not just primary occipital (boy and puppy are visual) but also frontal and parietal supramodal sources. The 72-subject sample is closer in scale to the developmental HBN cohort than typical naturalistic-EEG studies, so the effect-size scaffolding here is relevant for power planning.

## Notable details
- 72 subjects; encoding-to-test interval of 3 weeks.
- ISC computed with Dmochowski 2012 correlated-component analysis.
- Supramodal predictive component is the key novel result; same ISC weights generalize across stimulus modality.
- Visual context improves memory even when temporally incongruent with the auditory narrative.
- Open Access via eNeuro (CC BY 4.0).

## Open questions / limitations
- Memory test is verbatim recognition of narrative content; "The Present" boy-vs-puppy contrast does not have an obvious memory readout.
- ISC is computed at the whole-stimulus level; per-shot ISC at the 0-500 ms event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP) scale is not analyzed.
- Adult sample; developmental ISC differences are not tested.
- The inferred supramodal source is not directly localized; component topographies are reported but not source-modeled.
