## TL;DR
Cross-subject reliability of fast electroencephalographic evoked responses to naturalistic audiovisual narratives is strongly modulated by attentional state: directing attention inward to mental arithmetic dramatically reduces inter-subject correlation, with the effect strongest for cohesive audiovisual narratives and weakest for non-narrative speech.

## Summary
Ki, Kelly, and Parra recorded electroencephalography (EEG) while subjects either attended to naturalistic narrative clips or performed a mental-arithmetic distractor task during identical stimulus presentations. Using the correlated-component analysis from Dmochowski et al. 2012, they measured between-subject reliability and showed that attended trials had substantially higher ISC than distracted trials. The effect grew with narrative cohesion (audiovisual story > meaningful speech > scrambled speech) and reached classification ceiling for the strongest contrasts: per-subject attentional state could be decoded with near-perfect accuracy. Eye-movement and order-of-presentation controls ruled out trivial confounds. The work establishes naturalistic-stimulus EEG ISC as a sensitive, non-task index of attentional engagement.

## Relevance to the review
Direct dependency for the boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP question on "The Present". Ki et al. show that attentional state itself modulates the magnitude of stimulus-locked EEG responses; in the HBN-EEG R3 sample (~6-21 years old), differential attention to social cues (the boy or the puppy) is one of the candidate mechanisms underlying any condition difference. This paper grounds the worry that boy-versus-puppy condition differences could reflect attention rather than perception, and motivates including attentional or arousal indices (eye fixation density, alpha power baseline) as nuisance regressors. It also validates the inter-subject reliability framing that Phase 5 cluster statistics implicitly rely on.

## Notable details
- Audiovisual narrative > meaningful speech > scrambled speech in ISC magnitude.
- Mental arithmetic distractor reduces ISC dramatically; control experiments rule out eye-movement and presentation-order confounds.
- Per-subject classification of attentional state from ISC alone reaches 100% for the strongest stimuli.
- Effect localized in scalp topography to occipital and central electrodes; the strongest correlated component has a posterior generator.
- Open Access via J Neurosci Author Open Choice; freely redistributable.

## Open questions / limitations
- Adult subjects, healthy participants; the developmental HBN sample may show smaller and more variable attention-modulated ISC.
- Stimuli are full audiovisual narratives; "The Present" is shorter and silent for stretches, so the absolute ISC magnitudes may not transfer.
- The paper measures whole-stimulus ISC; the boy-vs-puppy comparison is at the shot level, where ISC differences will be much subtler.
- 100 Hz sampling on local R3 caps the upper-band reliability, but the ISC components reported are dominated by frequencies <30 Hz, so this is acceptable.
