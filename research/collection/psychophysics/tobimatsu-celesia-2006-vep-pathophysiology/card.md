## TL;DR
Comprehensive clinical review of visual evoked potential (VEP) recording with luminance and contrast stimuli, mapping VEP components to magnocellular versus parvocellular pathways and to striate versus extrastriate generators.

## Summary
The review summarizes established VEP methodology, the parallel-pathway framing of human visual processing (M, P, K streams from retina to primary visual cortex), and modern extensions including multifocal VEPs and motion-onset VEPs. The authors emphasize that no single stimulus type is optimal: pattern-reversal full-field VEPs probe anterior pathways, hemi-field stimulation isolates post-chiasmal lesions, and high-contrast pattern onset taps cortical generators. The piece functions as a methodological reference for matching electroencephalography (EEG) responses to stimulus dimensions, and articulates the connection between low-level luminance and contrast manipulations and recordable scalp components.

## Relevance to the review
Useful framing for the bottom-up component of "The Present" boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP analysis. The log luminance ratio (LLR) regressor is a between-shot luminance-contrast manipulation, and this review is the standard clinical reference for how such manipulations map to VEP components in time and topography (P100 around 100 ms, N75 earlier). Because human electroencephalography (HBN-EEG) on R3 is recorded at 100 Hz, only the slowest VEP components are resolvable in the local pipeline, which sets expectations for what to look for in the boy versus puppy shot-onset event-related spectral perturbation (ERSP). Not a direct dependency, but a guard against over-interpreting fast VEP-like effects on under-sampled data.

## Notable details
- Pattern reversal VEPs at 1-2 reversals per second yield the most reproducible P100 component; pattern onset/offset preserved separately for cortical generators.
- Magnocellular versus parvocellular dissociation uses high temporal frequency / low contrast versus low temporal frequency / chromatic-isoluminant stimuli.
- Motion-onset VEP (translating pattern, brief duration) targets V5/MT generators and is the closest clinical analogue of shot-onset transients in animated film.
- Multifocal VEPs allow per-location assessment of striate function.
- Clinical algorithm in the paper proposes a sequential decision tree for which paradigm to use given a clinical question.

## Open questions / limitations
- The review predates large-scale naturalistic-stimulus VEP work (Lalor-Foxe TRF / VESPA, Dmochowski ISC); naturalistic-movie evoked components are not addressed.
- Time-frequency / ERSP analyses are not in scope; the review is focused on time-domain VEP peaks.
- VEP latencies described assume standard sampling rates (>500 Hz); applicability to 100 Hz EEG (the local R3 dataset for "The Present") requires aliasing-aware reinterpretation.
