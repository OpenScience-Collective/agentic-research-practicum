## TL;DR
Inter-subject correlation in the electroencephalography (EEG) of a small naive sample (12-16 viewers) watching pre-broadcast television predicts the behavioral response of a much larger audience as measured by Twitter activity and ratings, and a follow-up functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) study localizes the reliability covariate to higher-order visual and auditory cortices.

## Summary
Dmochowski and colleagues recruited a small EEG sample (16 subjects) to view "The Walking Dead" pilot and Super Bowl ads, then correlated their across-subject EEG reliability time courses with social media (Twitter) activity and audience ratings collected during the original broadcasts. Neural reliability predicted audience preference, often more accurately than the small sample's own reported preferences would. A follow-up fMRI experiment with a separate cohort showed that the EEG reliability time course correlates with blood-oxygen-level-dependent (BOLD) activity in higher-order visual and auditory regions. The work extends the Dmochowski 2012 EEG-ISC framework into a predictive-validity demonstration and is a touchstone for the population-generalization claim in naturalistic-EEG research.

## Relevance to the review
Indirect dependency. The boy-shot vs puppy-shot question on "The Present" is not about audience prediction; it is about within-stimulus condition contrasts. But this paper is the canonical reference for EEG-ISC as a meaningful real-world signal, which strengthens the case that any boy-versus-puppy ERSP difference in the HBN sample reflects shared, stimulus-driven processing rather than idiosyncratic responses. The fMRI fusion result also predicts that occipitotemporal IC clusters are the right regions to look for condition-modulated reliability.

## Notable details
- 12-16 naive EEG subjects predict aggregate audience response of millions.
- Twitter time courses serve as the audience-behavior ground truth.
- Follow-up fMRI on different subjects localizes EEG-reliability covariates to higher-order visual and auditory cortex.
- Open Access (CC BY).
- Stimuli are commercially produced; production values may inflate ISC relative to amateur footage (per Dorr 2010 entry).

## Open questions / limitations
- Adult sample, US television; the developmental HBN cohort and "The Present" (animated short) may show smaller effects.
- Audience preference is operationalized by Twitter mention count and ratings, both noisy.
- The paper does not break ISC down by stimulus event (cuts, scene boundaries); shot-level ISC, the regime "The Present" cares about, is not analyzed.
- Sample sizes are small enough that the cross-validated predictive accuracy is unstable across re-analyses.
