## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Alpha-band oscillations (8-13 Hz) in the human electroencephalogram (EEG) are sensitive to emotional content. We review studies of emotional picture viewing reporting modulation of alpha-band power, focusing on the time course, topography, and parametric modulation by emotional intensity and valence. Across paradigms, alpha desynchronisation occurs for emotional relative to neutral pictures, peaks 0.2-1 s after picture onset, and is most pronounced over occipital and parietal sites. The effect scales with arousal more reliably than with valence. We discuss interpretations in terms of attentional engagement and predictive coding, integrating with the alpha-beta-gamma framework, and identify methodological recommendations for reducing heterogeneity in future studies.
