# Frontal EEG asymmetry as a moderator and mediator of emotion

## Citation

Coan, J. A., & Allen, J. J. B. (2004). Frontal EEG asymmetry as a moderator and mediator of emotion. *Biological Psychology*, 67(1-2), 7-49. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.biopsycho.2004.03.002

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Elsevier).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Resting frontal EEG asymmetry is widely studied as a marker of trait approach-withdrawal motivation and as a state index of emotional response. We review evidence for the role of frontal EEG asymmetry as both a moderator and a mediator of emotion, integrating findings from healthy and clinical samples and across developmental and individual-differences research. We propose a capability model in which trait asymmetry reflects a dispositional capacity for approach motivation that interacts with situational demands. We review measurement, reliability, and statistical issues, and identify directions for future research.
