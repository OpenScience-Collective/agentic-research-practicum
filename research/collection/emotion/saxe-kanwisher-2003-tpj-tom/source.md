# People thinking about thinking people: The role of the temporo-parietal junction in "theory of mind"

## Citation

Saxe, R., & Kanwisher, N. (2003). People thinking about thinking people: The role of the temporo-parietal junction in "theory of mind". *NeuroImage*, 19(4), 1835-1842. https://doi.org/10.1016/S1053-8119(03)00230-1

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Elsevier NeuroImage).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Humans often try to infer other people's thoughts and feelings. The current functional MRI study identified brain regions selectively involved in such "theory of mind" reasoning. Subjects read stories about people, requiring them to make inferences about a character's belief (false-belief stories) or a fact about an inanimate object (false-photograph stories). Bilateral regions in the temporo-parietal junction (TPJ-M) and the medial parietal cortex were selectively activated during inferences about the beliefs of other persons compared with all of the other conditions. The right TPJ-M was robustly selective for the mental-state reasoning condition. The data support the existence of brain regions specialized for reasoning about the contents of other people's minds.
