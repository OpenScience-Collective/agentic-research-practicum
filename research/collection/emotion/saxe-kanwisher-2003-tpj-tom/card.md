## TL;DR

Bilateral temporo-parietal junction (TPJ), particularly the right TPJ, selectively activates when participants reason about another agent's mental state, establishing TPJ as a candidate domain-specific theory-of-mind region.

## Summary

Saxe and Kanwisher used fMRI in 12 adult participants reading short narratives that required either inferring a character's mental state ("false belief"), considering a character's appearance only ("false photograph"), or reading non-social control text. The right TPJ showed strong selective activation for false-belief over false-photograph and over mechanical-physical-control conditions. Left TPJ showed a similar but weaker effect. The authors argued that the right TPJ is engaged specifically by mental-state reasoning rather than by general social or syntactic processing, and proposed it as a domain-specific theory-of-mind (ToM) region.

## Relevance to the review

Foundational source for Category 2 (social cognition during movie watching). The boy in The Present is a child protagonist whose intentions and feelings the viewer naturally tracks; right TPJ engagement during such tracking is a default prediction supported by Saxe-Kanwisher and replicated in the naturalistic-stimulus literature (Richardson & Saxe 2018). For the boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP contrast, the boy-frames are expected to recruit the ToM network more strongly than the puppy-frames if the puppy is an affective rather than mentalising target. EEG analogues of right TPJ activity (right temporo-parietal independent components) might therefore differ between conditions.

Developmental-justification rule: adult-only sample. The TPJ ToM response in children is well documented in independent work (Richardson & Saxe 2018; Gweon 2012; Gallagher 2003). Mark `relevance: medium` because the paper itself does not include children, but the developmental anchor exists in this collection (see Richardson 2018).

## Notable details

- N = 12 adults.
- Stimuli: short narratives (false-belief, false-photograph, non-social).
- Right TPJ effect size larger than left.
- Activation specifically driven by mental-state attribution, not by social content per se.
- Used in subsequent studies as the canonical right-TPJ ToM localiser.

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult sample, small N.
- Reading-based, not naturalistic film.
- Single right-TPJ region of interest; later work (Saxe & Powell 2006, Mar 2011) decomposes the ToM network into multiple nodes.
- Selectivity of right TPJ has been debated; alternative accounts include attentional reorienting (Mitchell 2008).
