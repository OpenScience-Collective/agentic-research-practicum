## TL;DR

Discrete basic emotions (anger, fear, disgust, happiness, sadness, surprise) elicit distinguishable, spatially overlapping but multivariately separable, neural patterns during emotional film viewing and emotional imagery, consistent with both dimensional and categorical accounts.

## Summary

Saarimaki and colleagues induced six discrete basic emotions in adult participants using two methods, viewing emotional movie clips and guided emotional mental imagery. Functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) data were analysed with multi-voxel pattern analysis (MVPA). Whole-brain pattern classifiers reliably decoded emotion category in both induction methods, and patterns generalised between methods, indicating a method-invariant emotion representation. Most discriminative voxels were located in the anterior and posterior cingulate cortex, medial prefrontal cortex, thalamus, and brainstem regions associated with autonomic regulation. The authors concluded that discrete emotional states have distinguishable neural signatures even when they share many activation regions in mass-univariate analyses.

## Relevance to the review

Anchors the cinematic-emotion-induction literature in Category 1. The boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast is a within-stimulus emotional-content manipulation; Saarimaki demonstrates that even short film clips can elicit decodable emotion-specific patterns. The cingulate / medial prefrontal localisation overlaps with regions implicated in social cognition (Richardson & Saxe 2018) and pet bonding (Stoeckel 2014), suggesting a candidate set of brain regions whose EEG-equivalent independent components might separate boy-shot from puppy-shot.

Developmental-justification rule: adult-only sample. MVPA-decodable emotion patterns in children are less well established. Recent work (e.g., Lebois 2020, Roy 2017) shows similar emotion-network engagement in adolescents but with higher noise. Mark `relevance: high` because this is the canonical naturalistic-emotion-induction paper, and explicitly note the adult-only caveat for any child-cohort transfer.

## Notable details

- N = 18 adults; both movie viewing and imagery induction in a within-subject design.
- Six target emotions; pattern classifier accuracies significantly above chance for all.
- Cross-induction generalisation supports a content-independent emotion representation.
- Most discriminative regions: anterior cingulate, posterior cingulate, medial prefrontal cortex, thalamus, brainstem (likely periaqueductal gray, PAG).

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult-only; small N for MVPA stability.
- 6-emotion taxonomy is contested; Cowen-Keltner 2017 argues for 27 distinguishable categories from short videos.
- Movie clips were chosen to elicit a single dominant emotion; The Present has mixed and shifting affect across shots.
- fMRI temporal resolution prevents direct mapping onto the 0-500 ms ERSP window.
