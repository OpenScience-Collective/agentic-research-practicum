# Optimizing methods for linking cinematic features to fMRI data

## Citation

Kauttonen, J., Hlushchuk, Y., & Tikka, P. (2015). Optimizing methods for linking cinematic features to fMRI data. *NeuroImage*, 110, 136-148. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2015.01.063

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Elsevier).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Cinema provides rich and complex audiovisual stimuli well suited for studying brain responses to naturalistic input. Linking specific cinematic features to brain activity is a methodological challenge. We extracted multi-level features (low-level audio and visual energy, shot and scene structure, character presence) from a feature-length narrative film and used them to model time-resolved fMRI activity in viewers. Different feature classes accounted for activity in different cortical networks: early sensory cortex tracked low-level audiovisual energy, while higher-order regions tracked structural and narrative features. We discuss methodological recommendations for naturalistic film-fMRI studies, including feature extraction, temporal alignment, and statistical inference.
