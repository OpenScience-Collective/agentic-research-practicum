## TL;DR

Children aged 3-6 years show the same parametric "cuteness" effect of baby-schema manipulation as adults, both for human and for animal (cat, dog) faces, and direct their gaze preferentially toward high-baby-schema faces, demonstrating that the Kindchenschema response is established and generalises to companion animals well before adolescence.

## Summary

Borgi and colleagues parametrically manipulated baby schema in human infant, puppy, and kitten face photographs and presented the morphed faces to 3-6-year-old children alongside adults. Children rated high-baby-schema faces as cuter than low-baby-schema versions in all three species, and an eye-tracker showed preferential first-fixation and longer dwell time on high-baby-schema regions. Effects were comparable in magnitude across species. The authors conclude that the Kindchenschema response is functional from preschool age and is not species-specific.

## Relevance to the review

Critical paper for closing the developmental-justification gap on the puppy-shot interpretation. Glocker 2009 and Stoeckel 2014 establish adult neural responses to infant and pet faces; Borgi 2014 demonstrates that the *behavioral and attentional* baby-schema response is present and parametric in children as young as 3, including for puppy faces specifically. This supports the inference that HBN child viewers (ages 5-21) of The Present should respond to the puppy in a manner that recruits cuteness-related attentional and affiliative mechanisms, and grounds the boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast in a developmentally validated phenomenon.

Developmental-justification rule: this paper *is* the developmental anchor cited by adult-only studies in this strand. No transfer caveat needed for the age range it covers (3-6); upward extrapolation through adolescence is supported by continued infant-cuteness sensitivity in children and adults.

## Notable details

- N = 75 children (3-6 years), N = 161 adults; comparable design.
- Stimuli: human infant, puppy, and kitten faces morphed to low/medium/high baby-schema.
- Cuteness ratings showed a significant baby-schema main effect for all species in both age groups.
- Eye-tracking: first fixation and total fixation time biased toward high-baby-schema regions.
- Effect size for cuteness ratings was similar in children and adults, indicating early developmental establishment.

## Open questions / limitations

- Behavioral and gaze-based, not neural; brain-level confirmation in children is still missing.
- Static photographs, not naturalistic motion or narrative context.
- Only preschool children (3-6); does not directly test 7-21 (most of HBN); but the trajectory is reasonable to extrapolate given continued behavioral effects in adults.
- Single-trial cuteness ratings may not generalise to spontaneous responses during free viewing.
