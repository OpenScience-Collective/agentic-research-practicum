# The neural bases of social cognition and story comprehension

## Citation

Mar, R. A. (2011). The neural bases of social cognition and story comprehension. *Annual Review of Psychology*, 62, 103-134. https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-psych-120709-145406

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Annual Reviews).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

The construction of mental representations of others' thoughts, feelings, and intentions—a process commonly called theory of mind or mentalizing—and the comprehension of narratives are intimately related. This article reviews the neural correlates of story comprehension and theory of mind, highlighting the substantial overlap between the brain regions consistently implicated in each. Both domains recruit a network including medial prefrontal cortex, the temporo-parietal junction, posterior cingulate / precuneus, and the anterior temporal poles. We review behavioural evidence linking narrative engagement and social cognition, discuss developmental trajectories, and argue that narrative comprehension can be understood as a form of social cognition.
