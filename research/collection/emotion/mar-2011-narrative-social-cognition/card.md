## TL;DR

Mar synthesises evidence that narrative comprehension and theory-of-mind reasoning recruit largely overlapping brain networks (medial prefrontal cortex, temporo-parietal junction, posterior cingulate / precuneus, anterior temporal poles), supporting the view that processing stories is fundamentally a social-cognitive activity that draws on the same machinery used to understand other minds.

## Summary

This Annual Review of Psychology piece synthesises behavioural and neuroimaging evidence on the brain bases of narrative comprehension. Across paradigms (reading, listening, viewing), narrative tasks consistently activate the medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC), temporo-parietal junction (TPJ), posterior cingulate / precuneus, and anterior temporal poles. These regions overlap substantially with the default-mode network and with the canonical theory-of-mind (ToM) localiser network. Mar argues that processing characters' minds, intentions, beliefs, and emotions is the central computational task of narrative comprehension and that narrative engagement and ToM are best understood as two windows on the same underlying machinery.

## Relevance to the review

Anchor for Category 7. The Present is a brief narrative film with a child protagonist whose emotional arc viewers naturally track. Mar's framework predicts that boy-shot frames in particular should engage TPJ/mPFC mentalising networks; the puppy in turn is an affective object whose mental states are simpler but still trackable as an animate agent. EEG independent components mapped to right-TPJ-adjacent or medial-frontal sources are candidates for differential engagement.

Developmental-justification rule: review draws primarily on adult studies. Story-comprehension ToM networks emerge in early childhood (Richardson & Saxe 2018) and continue maturing through adolescence. Mark `relevance: medium` and document developmental gap.

## Notable details

- Annual Review piece spanning behavioural and neuroimaging evidence.
- Convergent activation: mPFC, TPJ, precuneus, anterior temporal poles.
- Default-mode network and ToM-localiser network heavily overlap.
- Argues for narrative comprehension as fundamentally social-cognitive.
- Includes consideration of fictional vs non-fictional materials, modality differences (text vs audiobook vs film).

## Open questions / limitations

- Review, no new data.
- Adult studies dominate.
- Limited treatment of non-Western or non-literate narrative contexts.
- Does not address the time scale of narrative ToM engagement (slow vs fast).
