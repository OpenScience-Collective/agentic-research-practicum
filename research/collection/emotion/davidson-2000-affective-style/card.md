## TL;DR

Davidson lays out the canonical approach-withdrawal model in which left-prefrontal activation supports approach motivation and positive affect while right-prefrontal activation supports withdrawal motivation and negative affect, with frontal alpha asymmetry as the primary electroencephalographic (EEG) index, framing decades of subsequent affective-style research.

## Summary

This American Psychologist review synthesises Davidson's program of research on affective style, defined as relatively stable individual differences in emotion reactivity and regulation. Frontal alpha asymmetry, the difference in alpha-band power between right and left frontal electrodes (often F4-F3 or F8-F7), is the EEG index. Greater left-frontal activation (relatively reduced left alpha) is associated with approach motivation and positive affect; greater right-frontal activation with withdrawal motivation and negative affect. Davidson links this asymmetry to behaviour, personality, depression risk, infant attachment, and meditation experience.

## Relevance to the review

Foundational source for Category 6. The boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP project measures spectral power on the 0-500 ms post-shot window; frontal alpha asymmetry is one of the most-tested EEG indices of affective valence and is a candidate dependent measure if frontal independent components separate by condition. If the puppy is more "approach"-like (cute, positive affect) than the boy in some shots, Davidson's framework predicts left-frontal alpha reduction during puppy shots, although the 500 ms window is shorter than the typical asymmetry-aggregation window of seconds-to-minutes.

Developmental-justification rule: review references infant and child work alongside adult studies; Davidson and colleagues themselves studied frontal asymmetry in infants and children (Davidson & Fox 1989). The asymmetry index is well attested in HBN's age range, supporting `relevance: medium` and reducing the developmental gap somewhat.

## Notable details

- 2000 American Psychologist invited review summarising 20 years of affective-style work.
- Frontal alpha asymmetry as the canonical EEG marker.
- Left-frontal-greater = approach / positive; right-frontal-greater = withdrawal / negative.
- Cited in subsequent infant-attachment and child-emotion-regulation work.
- Predates the recent meta-analytic critique (van der Vinne 2017; Reznik & Allen 2018).

## Open questions / limitations

- Theoretical framing ahead of empirical resolution.
- Subsequent meta-analyses (van der Vinne 2017; Reznik & Allen 2018) document mixed findings and emphasise low test-retest reliability of single-session asymmetry.
- 500 ms is a short window for asymmetry; classical asymmetry uses minutes.
- Effect sizes in literature are small.
