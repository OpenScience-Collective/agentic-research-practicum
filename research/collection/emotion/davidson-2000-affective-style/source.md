# Affective style, psychopathology, and resilience: brain mechanisms and plasticity

## Citation

Davidson, R. J. (2000). Affective style, psychopathology, and resilience: brain mechanisms and plasticity. *American Psychologist*, 55(11), 1196-1214. https://doi.org/10.1037/0003-066X.55.11.1196

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (American Psychological Association).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Affective style refers to relatively stable individual differences in emotion reactivity and regulation. This article reviews evidence linking affective style to specific brain circuits, particularly the prefrontal cortex and the amygdala. Frontal electroencephalographic asymmetry is described as a major dispositional marker of approach and withdrawal motivation, with increased left-prefrontal activity reflecting approach tendencies and positive affect, and increased right-prefrontal activity reflecting withdrawal tendencies and negative affect. Affective style relates to vulnerability to mood and anxiety disorders and to capacity for resilience, with promising implications for prevention and treatment.
