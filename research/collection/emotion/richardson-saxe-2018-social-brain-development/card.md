## TL;DR

A 5.5-minute animated short film ("Partly Cloudy") robustly localises theory-of-mind (ToM) and pain regions in children as young as 3, and these networks become more selective with age between 3 and 12, demonstrating that naturalistic Pixar-style stimuli are a viable tool to map developmental changes in social-cognitive brain networks.

## Summary

Richardson, Lisandrelli, Riobueno-Naylor, and Saxe scanned 122 children aged 3-12 and 33 adults during free viewing of "Partly Cloudy", a Pixar short. Time-resolved annotations of mental and physical events were used to identify ToM regions (right and left temporo-parietal junction, TPJ; precuneus; medial prefrontal cortex, mPFC) and pain matrix regions (anterior insula, anterior cingulate cortex, ACC; secondary somatosensory cortex). Both networks were detectable in children as young as 3. With age, ToM regions became increasingly selective for mental over physical events (and vice versa for pain regions). The TPJ in particular showed the strongest age-related sharpening of mental-event preference. Adult-like network selectivity was approached by ~7-12 years.

## Relevance to the review

Direct methodological precedent for using a Pixar short film with passive viewing in a child cohort to engage social-cognitive networks. The Present and "Partly Cloudy" share genre (Pixar shorts), narrative complexity, and child-attractive aesthetic. Critically, the paper validates that ToM/social-emotional networks are robustly engaged by the boy-and-pet narrative structure that The Present also uses. This supports the prediction that boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrasts engage social-cognitive (mentalising the boy) versus affective-cuteness (responding to the puppy) processes, both of which have demonstrably present neural substrates across HBN's age range.

Developmental-justification rule: not applicable; this paper *is* the developmental anchor for naturalistic-stimulus social brain engagement in children. Mark `relevance: high`.

## Notable details

- N = 155 (122 children, 3-12 years; 33 adults).
- Stimulus: "Partly Cloudy" (Pixar, 5.5 min) viewed once, no task.
- Trained annotators marked each second for mental and pain events.
- ToM network (TPJ-bilateral, precuneus, mPFC) significant in 3-year-olds and refined through 7-12.
- Pain network (anterior insula, ACC, S2) similarly age-graded.
- TPJ showed the strongest developmental effect on mental-versus-physical selectivity.

## Notable details (project-specific)

- Functional regions of interest (fROI) located via the same Pixar stimulus could in principle be transferred to HBN The Present analysis if a comparable per-second annotation track were generated for the boy and puppy.

## Open questions / limitations

- fMRI, not EEG; spatial resolution differs and the temporal resolution is much coarser than the 0-500 ms ERSP window of interest.
- "Partly Cloudy" is not The Present; specific event structure differs.
- 3-12 years; HBN extends to 21 years, so the upper end of the developmental trajectory is not directly sampled here.
- Adult comparison group is small (N = 33).
- No EEG companion; mapping the fMRI ToM/pain network onto independent component clusters in EEG is non-trivial.
