## TL;DR

Inter-subject correlation (ISC) of electroencephalographic (EEG) responses to naturalistic videos varies systematically with viewer age and sex, peaking in children and declining through adolescence and adulthood, providing a quantitative engagement metric directly applicable to the Healthy Brain Network (HBN) child cohort watching The Present.

## Summary

Cohen and colleagues recorded scalp EEG from a large cross-sectional sample of children, adolescents, and adults (N = 114, ages 6-44) viewing six naturalistic video clips, including animated and live-action narratives. They computed ISC, the consistency of evoked EEG responses across viewers, in early and late time windows. ISC was highest in children, declined into late adolescence, then plateaued in adulthood. Female viewers showed slightly higher ISC than male viewers across age. The effect was driven primarily by attention-related components rather than low-level audiovisual features, supporting ISC as an index of shared engagement that varies developmentally.

## Relevance to the review

This is one of very few EEG-ISC studies that includes a child sample matched in age range to HBN. ThePresent in HBN is similar in modality (animated narrative) to several stimuli used here. The age-dependent ISC trajectory implies that the boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP contrast in HBN is being measured during a developmental window of high inter-subject neural response coherence; this raises both a methodological caution (age-related variance in baseline ISC) and an opportunity (age-stratified analyses can reveal whether emotional-content effects compound with developmental engagement gradients). Directly relevant to Phase 5 group-level statistics and to interpretation of any condition x age interaction.

Developmental-justification rule: not applicable; this paper *is* the developmental anchor for EEG-ISC during naturalistic film. Mark `relevance: high`.

## Notable details

- N = 114 healthy participants, ages 6-44, cross-sectional.
- Stimuli: six videos including animated and live-action shorts; durations 1-5 minutes.
- ISC computed via correlated component analysis on 64-channel EEG.
- Children (6-12) had higher ISC than adolescents (13-18); both higher than adults (19-44).
- Female > male ISC observed but smaller effect than age.
- Attention-related components (modulated by within-subject gaze stability) carried most age variance.

## Open questions / limitations

- Cross-sectional, not longitudinal; cannot dissociate cohort from developmental effects.
- 64-channel cap, different montage from HBN's 128-channel HydroCel sensor net.
- Sampling rate 500 Hz (not the 100 Hz local R3 working set in this project).
- ISC is a coarse global metric; does not directly localise condition-specific (boy versus puppy) effects.
- Stimuli include only short videos; no specific test of The Present.
