## TL;DR

Zaki and Ochsner argue that empathy is best understood as comprising at least two partially dissociable processes (experience sharing supported by sensorimotor / affective resonance; mental-state attribution supported by the canonical mentalising network), each implemented in distinct but interacting brain systems, with overall empathic response also depending on top-down regulation by prefrontal control regions.

## Summary

This Nature Neuroscience review synthesises the empathy literature into a tripartite model. *Experience sharing* (vicarious feeling) is supported by anterior insula, anterior cingulate cortex, and sensorimotor regions; *mental-state attribution* (cognitive perspective-taking) is supported by medial prefrontal cortex, temporo-parietal junction, and posterior cingulate / precuneus; *regulation and motivation* are supported by lateral prefrontal control. The authors argue that integrative empathy depends on coordinated activity across these subsystems and that disentangling them is essential for explaining individual differences and clinical disorders of social cognition.

## Relevance to the review

Synthesises Categories 2 (mentalising) and 7 (empathy in narrative) into a single framework directly applicable to The Present. The boy-shot frames may engage both mental-state attribution (what the boy is thinking/feeling) and experience sharing (vicariously feeling his frustration or delight); the puppy frames are likely to engage experience sharing more than mental-state attribution if viewers treat the puppy as an affective agent rather than a mentaliser-target. This dissociation could in principle separate the two conditions on different networks, an important constraint for interpreting any boy-shot-vs-puppy-shot ERSP differences. Marked `relevance: medium`.

Developmental-justification rule: review draws primarily on adult work. The two-system architecture is broadly supported in development (Decety, Singer 2010s work) but with substantial age-related changes. Mark `relevance: medium` and document gap.

## Notable details

- Published in Nature Neuroscience 2012.
- Tripartite model: experience sharing, mental-state attribution, regulation/motivation.
- Distinct neural substrates for each.
- Integrative framework cited frequently in subsequent empathy work.
- Bridges mirror-neuron and ToM literatures.

## Open questions / limitations

- Review, no new data.
- Boundaries between subsystems are sometimes contested.
- Limited treatment of cross-cultural and developmental angles.
- Recent multivariate work (Wager 2013, Woo 2017) may further refine the boundaries.
