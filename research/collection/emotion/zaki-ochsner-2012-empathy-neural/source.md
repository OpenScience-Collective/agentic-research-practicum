# The neuroscience of empathy: progress, pitfalls and promise

## Citation

Zaki, J., & Ochsner, K. N. (2012). The neuroscience of empathy: progress, pitfalls and promise. *Nature Neuroscience*, 15(5), 675-680. https://doi.org/10.1038/nn.3085

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Springer Nature).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

In recent years, the social neuroscience of empathy has flourished. Researchers have used a variety of paradigms to dissociate brain systems involved in sharing others' affective states (experience sharing) from those involved in inferring their thoughts and feelings (mental-state attribution). We review evidence supporting a model in which empathy is best understood as a set of at least two partially dissociable processes, each supported by distinct neural systems. We discuss how regulatory and motivational systems modulate empathic responding and how this framework can illuminate individual differences and clinical disorders. We close by identifying methodological challenges and promising directions for future work.
