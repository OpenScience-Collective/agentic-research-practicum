## TL;DR

Watching emotionally laden movie scenes synchronises brain activity across viewers, especially in midline cortical, sensorimotor, and limbic regions, and the strength of synchrony scales with rated emotional intensity, supporting an emotion-driven account of inter-subject neural correlation (ISC) during film.

## Summary

Nummenmaa and colleagues recorded fMRI from 71 viewers watching short emotionally evocative movie clips and computed inter-subject correlation (ISC) of brain activity time courses. They found systematically elevated ISC during high-arousal and high-valence-extreme periods relative to neutral periods, particularly in the midline cortex (anterior and posterior cingulate, precuneus), somatosensory cortex, dorsal attention regions, and limbic structures. The authors argue that emotional content acts as an attentional and somatic-resonance amplifier, drawing viewers into a shared neural state and providing a candidate mechanism for "narrative coupling".

## Relevance to the review

Direct primary source for Category 4 (ISC and reliability driven by emotional content). The boy-shot vs puppy-shot ERSP contrast is, at the network level, a question about whether emotionally distinct shots in The Present produce dissociable neural states; Nummenmaa supplies the prior that high-emotion segments drive strong cross-viewer coupling in midline and limbic regions. Although the modality is fMRI, EEG-ISC analogues (Cohen 2018 eNeuro, Dmochowski 2014) replicate the basic phenomenon, supporting joint use as motivation for emotion-content-aware contrasts.

Developmental-justification rule: adult-only sample (mean age ~25). Emotion-modulated ISC has since been shown in children (Cohen 2018 eNeuro and follow-ups), but Nummenmaa specifically does not include developmental data. Mark `relevance: high` because the mechanism is foundational and document the developmental gap as an open question.

## Notable details

- N = 71 healthy adults.
- Stimuli: 137 short movie clips spanning fear, disgust, happiness, sadness, anger, and neutral.
- Brain time-course similarity (ISC) increased with rated valence extremity and arousal.
- Midline cortex, somatosensory cortex, dorsal attention network most strongly modulated.
- Effects survive control for low-level audiovisual features.

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult sample.
- Short clips (5-30 s) rather than long narratives; The Present runs ~3.5 min.
- fMRI; no spectral / time-frequency information.
- Emotion ratings were collected post-hoc, not continuously synchronised to film time.
