# The Coupled Brains of Captivated Audiences: Toward a Naturalistic Neuroscience of Media

## Citation

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Audiences spend large amounts of time engaging with media content. Although engagement and audience captivation are central concepts in media psychology, theorizing about their neural underpinnings has been limited. We summarize emerging evidence from naturalistic neuroscience to argue that inter-subject correlation (ISC) of brain activity provides an objective neural marker of audience captivation. ISC quantifies the consistency of evoked responses across audience members and is elevated for engaging, narrative-rich, and emotionally intense content. We discuss methodological choices, theoretical implications for media psychology, and applications to content design and audience measurement.
