## TL;DR

Subjective ratings of 2185 short emotional video clips by 853 participants reveal at least 27 distinguishable emotion categories connected by smooth gradients, undermining strict basic-emotion taxonomies in favour of a high-dimensional emotion space.

## Summary

Cowen and Keltner had 853 participants rate 2185 short evocative videos on multiple emotion-category and dimensional scales. Self-similarity matrices and dimensionality-reduction techniques revealed that 27 distinguishable categories (e.g., admiration, awe, calmness, confusion, craving, empathic pain, ...) capture the structure better than 6 basic-emotion or 2 dimensional-valence-arousal frameworks. Crucially, the categories are connected by continuous gradients rather than being categorically separated. The authors argue for a high-dimensional, partially graded emotion space.

## Relevance to the review

Provides the high-dimensional emotion space against which the simpler boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast can be framed. The Present likely elicits a mixture of admiration, sadness, amusement, and "aww"-style cuteness; Cowen-Keltner's taxonomy supplies vocabulary and category structure for any post-hoc characterisation of the affective content of individual shots if a per-shot emotion-rating annotation is later collected. Marked `relevance: medium` because the paper itself is not a neural study and its 27 categories are not directly operationalised in the current ERSP analysis.

Developmental-justification rule: adults only. Emotion categories perceived in video have been studied in children but at coarser resolution. The 27-category granularity may be an upper bound for adolescents and an over-fit for younger children. Mark `relevance: medium` and document the developmental gap.

## Notable details

- N = 853 raters; 2185 short videos.
- Each video rated on 34 emotion categories and 14 dimensional scales.
- 27 categories supported by a stable similarity structure.
- Category boundaries are smooth, not sharp; "fuzzy categories with gradients".
- Publicly released video stimulus set.

## Open questions / limitations

- Behavioural and self-report only; no neural data.
- Online sample, US-centric.
- Adult sample; no children.
- 27-category structure depends on the specific stimulus set; the upper bound on distinct categories may be even higher with broader content.
