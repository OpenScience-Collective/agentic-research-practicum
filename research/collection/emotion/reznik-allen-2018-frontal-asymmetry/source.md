# Frontal asymmetry as a mediator and moderator of emotion: An updated review

## Citation

Reznik, S. J., & Allen, J. J. B. (2018). Frontal asymmetry as a mediator and moderator of emotion: An updated review. *Psychophysiology*, 55(1), e12965. https://doi.org/10.1111/psyp.12965

## Open access

Closed access at the publisher (Wiley).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Frontal EEG asymmetry has been studied for decades as a marker of approach-withdrawal motivation, with widespread implications for affective neuroscience and clinical psychology. In this updated review, we revisit the evidence base for asymmetry as both a moderator and a mediator of emotion. We integrate recent meta-analytic findings, address measurement reliability issues, and consider how the asymmetry construct fits within contemporary models of emotion. We conclude that asymmetry continues to provide useful, if modest, predictive value for emotion-related outcomes, but the field requires methodological tightening and pre-registration to support stronger inference.
