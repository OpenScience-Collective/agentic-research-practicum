## TL;DR

A coordinate-based meta-analysis of 91 functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI) and positron emission tomography (PET) studies of emotion finds that no single brain region is consistently and selectively activated by any one basic emotion; instead, distributed networks (amygdala, anterior insula, anterior cingulate cortex, ventromedial prefrontal cortex, orbitofrontal cortex) are recruited across emotion categories with partial differentiation, supporting a constructionist over a basic-emotion locationist account.

## Summary

Lindquist, Wager, Kober, Bliss-Moreau, and Barrett conducted a coordinate-based meta-analysis of neuroimaging studies of fear, anger, sadness, disgust, and happiness using activation likelihood estimation. They found no one-to-one mapping between any single brain region and any single emotion category. Instead, the amygdala, anterior insula, anterior cingulate cortex (ACC), ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC), and orbitofrontal cortex (OFC) were activated across multiple emotion categories with partial selectivity. The authors interpret these findings as more consistent with a constructionist model in which emotions emerge from interactions among general-purpose networks (core affect, conceptualisation, executive attention, language) than with a strict basic-emotion locationist model where each emotion has a dedicated brain region.

## Relevance to the review

Sets the canonical reference for the "amygdala / vmPFC / insula" Category 3 region set in this review. Lindquist's distributed-network finding is methodologically important for the boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast: a clean dissociation between the two conditions in any single emotion-category region is not expected; differences are more plausible at the distributed, multivariate level. For independent component clustering during the EEG analysis, this argues for examining a small set of co-activated network nodes rather than expecting one IC to "encode the boy-shot affect".

Developmental-justification rule: meta-analysis aggregates almost exclusively adult studies. Distributed emotion-network engagement in children is supported by Richardson 2018 and others. Mark `relevance: high` because the paper directly defines the regional vocabulary for Category 3, and document the developmental constraint.

## Notable details

- Coordinate-based meta-analysis (activation likelihood estimation, ALE).
- 91 PET/fMRI studies, ~1600 participants total.
- Five emotion categories: fear, anger, sadness, disgust, happiness.
- Distributed network recruitment with partial selectivity across categories.
- Argues for "constructionist" account; substantial commentary and replies appended in the BBS issue.

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult sample.
- Coordinate-based meta-analysis sensitive to publication bias and threshold heterogeneity.
- Study set ends ~2010; later naturalistic and MVPA work reframes some conclusions.
- Constructionist vs locationist debate is ongoing.
