# Emotional processing in anterior cingulate and medial prefrontal cortex

## Citation

Etkin, A., Egner, T., & Kalisch, R. (2011). Emotional processing in anterior cingulate and medial prefrontal cortex. *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*, 15(2), 85-93. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.tics.2010.11.004

## Open access

PMC: PMC3035157 (NIH author manuscript, freely readable; not formally redistributable).

## Abstract (publisher-provided)

Negative emotional stimuli activate a broad network of brain regions, including the medial prefrontal (mPFC) and anterior cingulate (ACC) cortices. An emerging body of work has begun to dissociate the contributions of these brain regions to emotional processing. Specifically, dorsal aspects of the mPFC and ACC are involved in the appraisal and expression of negative emotion, whereas ventral aspects of these regions show a role in generation of positive emotion and the regulation of emotional responses, mediated by structures such as the amygdala. Maladaptive function of these regions is implicated in major depression, post-traumatic stress disorder and other affective disorders. We discuss how a dorsal/appraisal versus ventral/regulation dissociation can integrate disparate findings and frame future research questions.
