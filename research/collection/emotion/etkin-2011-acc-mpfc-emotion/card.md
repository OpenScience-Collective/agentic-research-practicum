## TL;DR

Etkin, Egner, and Kalisch propose a functional dissociation in medial prefrontal cortex during emotional processing, with dorsal anterior cingulate cortex (dACC) and adjacent dorsomedial prefrontal cortex involved in *appraisal and expression* of negative affect, and ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC) including subgenual ACC involved in *generation of positive affect and extinction of negative affect*.

## Summary

This Trends in Cognitive Sciences review synthesises lesion, functional magnetic resonance imaging (fMRI), and pharmacological evidence to argue for a dissociation in medial prefrontal cortex (mPFC) function during emotion. The authors propose that dorsal ACC and adjacent dorsomedial prefrontal cortex are recruited for *appraisal and expression* of negative emotion, supporting threat detection, conflict monitoring, and recall of negative memory. In contrast, ventromedial prefrontal cortex (vmPFC) including subgenual ACC supports *generation of positive affect, extinction of conditioned fear, and regulation of negative emotion*. Disorders such as depression and post-traumatic stress disorder show characteristic disruption of this balance.

## Relevance to the review

Defines the regional vocabulary for vmPFC and ACC contributions in Category 3. The boy-shot vs puppy-shot contrast in The Present is unlikely to engage strong negative-affect appraisal (the film is positive-affective overall), so according to this dissociation the vmPFC component of mPFC engagement is the more likely candidate region for affect-related differences, alongside the social-cognition-relevant dorsomedial PFC. Useful for prioritising independent component clusters for follow-up. Marked `relevance: medium`.

Developmental-justification rule: review is adult-centric. ACC and vmPFC undergo prolonged maturation through adolescence (Casey 2008; Steinberg 2005). The dorsal-ventral dissociation may emerge gradually. Mark `relevance: medium` and document gap.

## Notable details

- Synthesises lesion, fMRI, pharmacological, and clinical evidence.
- Distinct dACC/dmPFC versus vmPFC/sgACC functions.
- vmPFC implicated in fear extinction and generation of positive affect.
- dACC/dmPFC implicated in appraisal and expression of negative affect.
- Clinical relevance for major depressive disorder and post-traumatic stress disorder.

## Open questions / limitations

- Review, no new data.
- Sharp dorsal/ventral dissociation has been challenged by subsequent work (e.g., Shackman 2011 negative-affect-pain-cognition meta-analysis; Wager 2008).
- Developmental data limited.
