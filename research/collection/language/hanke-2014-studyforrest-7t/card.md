# A high-resolution 7-Tesla fMRI dataset from complex natural stimulation with an audio movie

## TL;DR

Open 7-Tesla fMRI dataset of 20 adults listening to a 2-hour audio-described version of Forrest Gump, foundational for the Studyforrest naturalistic-stimulation initiative.

## Summary

Hanke, Baumgartner, Ibe, et al. released a 7T fMRI dataset of 20 right-handed adults listening to a 2-hour German audio-described version of "Forrest Gump", with detailed annotations of the audio track. It introduced the Studyforrest paradigm of releasing naturalistic-stimulation data with rich annotations. Subsequent extensions added 7T audiovisual viewing, eye-tracking, retinotopy, and emotion annotations. The 2014 release is the audio-only foundation.

## Relevance to the review

Medium relevance. Studyforrest itself is the most-mentioned naturalistic-fMRI dataset and contains both audio-only and audiovisual cohorts, so any silent-vs-spoken contrast in the literature points back here. For ThePresent, useful primarily as a methodological reference for releasing naturalistic-stimulation neuroimaging data with annotation files, not as a stimulus-domain match. Original 2014 release is audio-only, so the silent-stimulus boundary is not addressed in this specific paper, but the Studyforrest umbrella does include silent-only cohorts.

## Notable details

- 20 adults, 2-hour audio-described "Forrest Gump", 7T fMRI.
- Detailed annotations of the audio (speech, music, sound effects).
- Foundation for the Studyforrest extension series.
- Public release, BIDS-compatible.

## Open questions / limitations

- Audio-only stimulus in this initial release.
- Adult, German-speaking sample.
- No EEG component.
