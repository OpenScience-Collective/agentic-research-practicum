# Movement and mind: a functional imaging study of perception and interpretation of complex intentional movement patterns

## TL;DR

Watching silent animations of geometric shapes whose motion implies social interaction selectively activates a mentalising network including medial prefrontal cortex, temporoparietal junction, and superior temporal sulcus, even with no language and no agents that resemble humans.

## Summary

Castelli, Happe, Frith, and Frith scanned adults watching three classes of silent animations: random motion, goal-directed (a triangle pushing another), and mentalising (e.g., one shape coaxing or surprising another). Mentalising clips selectively engaged medial prefrontal cortex, temporoparietal junction, posterior superior temporal sulcus, and temporal poles. The result was foundational for the silent Heider-Simmel paradigm and showed that mentalising is triggered by motion patterns, not by linguistic or audiovisual social content. Established that the social brain can be probed without any speech.

## Relevance to the review

High relevance, full survival of silent-stimulus boundary case. ThePresent is exactly this kind of silent animation: motion-implied agent interaction without dialogue. Predicts that shots emphasising agent interaction (boy + puppy) should engage the social/mentalising network more than shots without it, even in the absence of any speech. Direct conceptual transfer.

## Notable details

- Three animation classes: random, goal-directed, mentalising.
- Silent stimuli throughout; no language.
- mPFC, TPJ, pSTS, temporal poles selectively engaged for mentalising.
- Foundational paper for the silent-Heider-Simmel paradigm in fMRI.

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult sample.
- Geometric-shape stimuli are far simpler than a Pixar animated short.
- Slow BOLD timescale; no ms-resolution dynamics.
