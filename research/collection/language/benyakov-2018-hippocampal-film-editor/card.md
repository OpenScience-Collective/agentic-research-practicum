# The Hippocampal Film Editor: Sensitivity and Specificity to Event Boundaries in Continuous Experience

## TL;DR

Hippocampal BOLD response during continuous movie watching is sharply elevated at narrative event boundaries but not at within-event camera cuts, showing the hippocampus encodes meaningful event structure rather than shot transitions per se.

## Summary

Ben-Yakov and Henson tested whether the hippocampus responds to every shot transition or specifically to event boundaries. Using a continuous movie with both within-event camera cuts and across-event boundaries, they showed that hippocampal BOLD response was selective: only event boundaries elicited a transient response. The finding refines the event-segmentation literature: the hippocampus is sensitive to narrative structure, not low-level discontinuity. Direct relevance for any movie-watching paradigm that decomposes the stimulus by shots vs. event boundaries.

## Relevance to the review

High relevance. ThePresent has 56 shots but probably fewer narrative event boundaries (introduction, gift, frustration, reveal, ending). Predicts that not every shot will trigger an event-boundary signature; only the narratively meaningful transitions will. Suggests the boy-vs-puppy contrast may be moderated by where each shot sits in the event-boundary structure. Boundary survival: paradigm uses sound but the demonstration is about visual cut-vs-boundary contrast, which transfers to silent animation. Critical citation for shot-vs-event interpretation.

## Notable details

- Within-event cuts produce minimal hippocampal response.
- Across-event boundaries produce a sharp transient response.
- Stimulus is "Forrest Gump" excerpts in the Studyforrest tradition.
- Argues hippocampus encodes event units, not perceptual cuts.

## Open questions / limitations

- fMRI; no ms-scale dynamics.
- Audiovisual stimulus with dialogue.
- Adult sample.
- Event-boundary annotations are observer-defined, introducing subjectivity.
