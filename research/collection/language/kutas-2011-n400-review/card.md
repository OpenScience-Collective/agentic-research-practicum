# Thirty years and counting: finding meaning in the N400 component of the event-related brain potential (ERP)

## TL;DR

Authoritative review summarizing 30 years of evidence that the N400 ERP component indexes the contextually facilitated retrieval of meaning, scaling with lexical predictability, semantic similarity, and discourse expectation.

## Summary

Kutas and Federmeier review the literature on the N400, the negative-going centroparietal ERP peaking around 400 ms after a meaningful stimulus. They argue N400 reflects access to long-term semantic memory under contextual constraint. Amplitude is attenuated by congruent context, repetition, and high cloze probability; it is enhanced by anomaly. The component generalises across written words, spoken words, signed language, pictures, and videos, but the prediction-and-integration literature is dominated by sentence-level linguistic stimuli. Sources include left temporal cortex and a distributed semantic network.

## Relevance to the review

Background reference because the 0-500 ms ERSP window in HBN ThePresent overlaps the N400 latency. Marked medium because the canonical N400 paradigms use words or short sentences with explicit cloze contexts; ThePresent has no words. However, the review notes that N400-class effects extend to pictures and short video clips with semantic congruity manipulations, so the existence of a 0-500 ms semantic-access component is not strictly tied to language, just usually demonstrated with it.

## Notable details

- N400 generalises to pictures and videos (Kutas and Federmeier 2011, Sec. 4).
- Latency 250-500 ms; centroparietal scalp distribution.
- Cloze probability is the dominant linear predictor of amplitude in word paradigms.
- Sources include left middle temporal gyrus, anterior temporal lobe, supramarginal gyrus.

## Open questions / limitations

- Review centered on linguistic stimuli; non-linguistic N400 evidence is sparse.
- No EEG dataset of its own; conclusions depend on the cited primary literature.
- Open question: whether silent-animation shot transitions trigger N400-class semantic-access activity.
