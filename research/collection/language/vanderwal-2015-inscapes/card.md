# Inscapes: A movie paradigm to improve compliance in functional magnetic resonance imaging

## TL;DR

A purpose-built silent abstract animation, "Inscapes", reduces head motion and improves fMRI compliance compared with rest, while preserving the functional connectivity architecture that motivates naturalistic paradigms.

## Summary

Vanderwal, Kelly, Eilbott, Mayes, and Castellanos developed "Inscapes", a silent 7-minute movie of slowly evolving abstract shapes designed as a rest-substitute for children and other compliance-sensitive populations. They scanned adults under rest, Inscapes, and a Hollywood-clip condition. Inscapes reduced head-motion compared with rest and produced functional-connectivity maps similar to rest, while inducing higher inter-subject synchrony. It became a widely adopted compliance tool in pediatric and clinical fMRI, including HBN.

## Relevance to the review

High relevance. Inscapes is itself silent and animated; it is the closest paradigmatic precedent for ThePresent at the methodological level. Boundary survival is total: Inscapes was deliberately designed without speech to be language-agnostic. Demonstrates that a silent animation can drive reliable network-level brain activity and that this design improves compliance in pediatric scans. Critical reference for the HBN paradigm and the silent-stimulus argument.

## Notable details

- 7-minute silent animation of abstract shapes.
- Reduced head motion vs. rest in adults.
- Functional-connectivity maps similar to rest, not Hollywood-clip.
- Adopted by HBN and many pediatric imaging studies as a compliance tool.

## Open questions / limitations

- fMRI dataset; no EEG.
- Adult sample in original paper; pediatric data in subsequent extensions.
- Stimulus is non-narrative abstract animation, not a Pixar narrative short.
