# Intersubject Synchronization of Cortical Activity During Natural Vision

## TL;DR

Watching the same movie produces remarkably synchronized BOLD fluctuations across visual and higher-order regions in different individuals, validating naturalistic free-viewing as a constrained probe of cortical function.

## Summary

Hasson, Nir, Levy, Fuhrmann, and Malach scanned five subjects while they watched 30 minutes of "The Good, the Bad and the Ugly". They computed voxelwise inter-subject correlation (ISC) of BOLD time courses. Up to 45 percent of cortex showed reliable across-subject synchronization, including ventral visual stream, motion-sensitive regions, and several higher-order parietal and temporal regions. The paper introduced ISC as a model-free analysis for naturalistic stimuli and helped legitimize movie-watching as an fMRI paradigm. Audiovisual movie was used; later work extended to silent visual content and audio-only content.

## Relevance to the review

Direct foundational reference for any movie-driven brain-activity analysis, including HBN-EEG ThePresent. Marked high relevance because the methodological frame of "synchronized brain response to a shared movie" is the comparator framework against which ThePresent ERSP results will be interpreted. Boundary survival: the original stimulus is audiovisual, but the ISC method has been deployed extensively for silent and audio-only contrasts in follow-ups, so the conceptual transfer to a silent animation is direct.

## Notable details

- 5 subjects, 3T fMRI, 30 minutes of audiovisual movie.
- Up to 45 percent of cortex synchronized across viewers (voxelwise ISC).
- Founded ISC as a standard model-free analysis for naturalistic neuroimaging.
- Showed that higher-order regions (collateral sulcus, temporo-parietal cortex) synchronize, not just early sensory areas.

## Open questions / limitations

- Audiovisual stimulus; the speech and music stream cannot be isolated from the visual stream in this dataset.
- Small sample (5 adults).
- No event-locked analysis; voxelwise correlation is on the slow BOLD timescale, not 0-500 ms.
