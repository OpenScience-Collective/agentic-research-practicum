# Topographic Mapping of a Hierarchy of Temporal Receptive Windows Using a Narrated Story

## TL;DR

Cortical regions form a hierarchy of temporal receptive windows (TRWs) ranging from milliseconds in early auditory cortex to minutes in default-mode regions, revealed by scrambling a narrated story at word, sentence, and paragraph timescales.

## Summary

Lerner, Honey, Silbert, and Hasson played a 7-minute narrated story to fMRI participants either intact or scrambled at the word, sentence, or paragraph level. They mapped voxelwise inter-subject correlation (ISC) under each scrambling condition. Early auditory cortex tracked all versions equally, indicating short TRWs; precuneus, temporo-parietal junction, and medial prefrontal cortex only tracked the intact narrative, indicating TRWs on the order of paragraphs or longer. The result was the foundational topographic map of cortical timescales, later extended by Hasson and colleagues to a "process memory" framework. All findings depend on temporally aligned speech input.

## Relevance to the review

Establishes that DMN-side regions integrate narrative over multi-second to multi-minute windows, which motivates looking beyond stimulus-locked early ERPs in any movie task. However, the TRW gradient as published is anchored to a narrated story; the long-window contrast is between scrambled and intact spoken narrative. For the silent ThePresent stimulus, the speech-based scrambling manipulation does not transfer; the conceptual claim that long-window networks track coherent narrative does, but only as background context. Boundary case: the framework survives the silent-stimulus boundary (visual narrative also has a hierarchy), but the operationalization in this paper does not.

## Notable details

- Three scrambling levels: word, sentence, paragraph; intact as ceiling.
- Voxelwise ISC computed per condition; gradient inferred from where ISC drops with scrambling.
- 7-minute narrated story (audio only); 36 participants.
- Coined the topographic-TRW formalism that became standard in naturalistic neuroimaging.

## Open questions / limitations

- Speech-only stimulus; no visual narrative manipulation.
- Window labels (word, sentence, paragraph) are linguistic constructs without obvious analogues in silent animation.
- Block-design scrambling cannot resolve sub-second event-related dynamics relevant to a 0-500 ms ERSP window.
