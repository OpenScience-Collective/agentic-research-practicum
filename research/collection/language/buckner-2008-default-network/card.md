# The Brain's Default Network

## TL;DR

Authoritative review consolidating the default-mode network as a stable functional system that supports internally directed cognition, scene construction, and narrative processing.

## Summary

Buckner, Andrews-Hanna, and Schacter review evidence from rest fMRI, task-deactivation fMRI, and lesion studies establishing the default-mode network (DMN) as a coherent system spanning medial prefrontal cortex, posterior cingulate/precuneus, lateral parietal cortex, and medial temporal lobe. They argue the DMN supports memory-based simulation, self-referential thought, and narrative-frame construction. It became the canonical reference for treating DMN as a "narrative" or "scene-construction" network. Subsequent narrative-fMRI work explicitly built on this framework.

## Relevance to the review

Background reference for category 4. Marked medium because the review is broad and predates much of the narrative-fMRI literature; the DMN-narrative link is asserted but not tested with movies. For ThePresent, the relevance is the prediction that DMN activity should track narrative coherence, but the paper does not study EEG and does not engage with shot-boundary timing.

## Notable details

- Three-component DMN model (mPFC, PCC/precuneus, lateral parietal) plus MTL subsystem.
- Resting-state and task-deactivation evidence convergent.
- Coined the "scene construction" framing now standard in narrative fMRI.
- Cited heavily in subsequent ISC and narrative-DMN papers.

## Open questions / limitations

- Review; no original data.
- fMRI-centric; no EEG component.
- Pre-dates dynamic-FC and naturalistic-narrative literature it inspired.
