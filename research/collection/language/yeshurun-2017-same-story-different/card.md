# Same Story, Different Story

## TL;DR

When two groups receive different framing context for the same ambiguous story, their default-mode-network activity diverges in ways that mirror their interpretive divergence, demonstrating that narrative meaning, not stimulus surface, drives DMN response.

## Summary

Yeshurun, Swanson, Simony, Chen, Lazaridi, Honey, and Hasson played the same audio short story to two groups with different prior framing (cheating vs. paranoid). fMRI ISC was high in early auditory cortex regardless of group (stimulus-driven), but DMN regions split into two within-group ISC patterns matching the framing. The result anchored the claim that DMN activity reflects subjective interpretation, not just sensory input. Followed by extensions in fMRI and EEG that test interpretation effects on naturalistic stimuli.

## Relevance to the review

Medium relevance: demonstrates that narrative interpretation, not raw stimulus structure, drives high-level cortical responses. For ThePresent the implication is that variability in interpretation (how attentive a child is, whether they identify with the boy) could swamp stimulus-locked effects in DMN-overlapping IC clusters. Boundary survival: framework transfers to silent animation; the original stimulus is audio-narrative but the divergent-interpretation finding is paradigm-general.

## Notable details

- Same audio narrative, two priming contexts ("cheating" vs "paranoid").
- Within-group ISC > between-group ISC in DMN, not in early auditory cortex.
- 40 participants total.
- Contributed the methodological device of group-conditioned ISC.

## Open questions / limitations

- Audio-narrative paradigm.
- Adult sample only.
- Effect requires explicit framing manipulation; not directly applicable to passive movie viewing.
