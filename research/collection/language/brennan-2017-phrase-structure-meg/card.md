# Neurophysiological dynamics of phrase-structure building during sentence processing

## TL;DR

Intracranial recordings during sentence reading reveal nested oscillatory dynamics in the temporal lobe and inferior frontal gyrus that track phrase- and sentence-level constituent structure, even in the absence of overt prosody.

## Summary

Nelson, Hale, Pallier, Dehaene, and colleagues recorded intracranial electrophysiology from epilepsy patients during sentence reading (extending earlier auditory work). High-gamma power and phase-locked low-frequency activity in posterior superior temporal sulcus and inferior frontal gyrus increased monotonically with the number of open syntactic nodes during phrase building, then dropped at constituent closure. The pattern was consistent with predictions of a pushdown automaton model of syntactic merge. The dataset is the canonical electrophysiological evidence for hierarchical phrase building during natural sentence comprehension.

## Relevance to the review

Often cited together with Brennan and Hale 2019 ("Hierarchical structure guides rapid linguistic predictions during naturalistic listening") as electrophysiological evidence that natural-language comprehension drives nested syntactic dynamics. For ThePresent the relevance is low because there is no syntax in a silent animation; phrase-structure regressors cannot be computed from visual events. Listed as comparator: this is exactly the class of paradigm that does not transfer to wordless storytelling.

## Notable details

- Intracranial recordings (sEEG/ECoG) in epilepsy patients during sentence reading.
- High-gamma envelope and theta-band phase-locking tracked open-node count during merge.
- Pushdown-automaton model from Hale used for predictions.
- Companion analyses available across MEG and ECoG modalities (Nelson et al. 2017 PNAS).

## Open questions / limitations

- Intracranial sample (epilepsy patients) limits population generalisation.
- Reading paradigm; results are about written sentences, not auditory dialogue or visual narrative.
- Syntactic regressors require a written or spoken transcript with constituent-parse information.
