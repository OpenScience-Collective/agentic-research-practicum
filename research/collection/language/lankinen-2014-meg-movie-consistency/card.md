# Intersubject consistency of cortical MEG signals during movie viewing

## TL;DR

During free movie viewing the cortical MEG signal is reliable across viewers in occipital and temporal regions for both broadband power and time-locked components, demonstrating that ms-scale electrophysiology captures stimulus-driven content during naturalistic stimulation.

## Summary

Lankinen, Saari, Hari, and Koskinen recorded MEG from 8 healthy adults while they watched a silent excerpt of the audiovisual movie "Crash" (and a separate audio condition). Inter-subject correlation of source-space MEG time courses showed reliable synchronization in occipital, parietal, and temporal cortex, consistent across visual-only and combined audiovisual conditions. Time-frequency analysis revealed condition-specific event-related desynchronization patterns. It is one of the earliest MEG demonstrations that movie watching produces reproducible electrophysiology, complementing the fMRI ISC literature.

## Relevance to the review

High relevance because it is the closest electrophysiological analogue to the HBN-EEG ThePresent setup: a continuous movie, naturalistic free-viewing, and analyses combining ISC with time-frequency power. The paper explicitly used a silent-visual condition, addressing the silent-stimulus boundary case directly. Differences: MEG vs. EEG, adults vs. children, no shot-event-locked epoching. Provides the key methodological precedent for ERSP-style analyses on movie data.

## Notable details

- Visual-only and audiovisual conditions allow a clean comparison.
- Source-space MEG time courses; ISC computed in source space, not sensor space.
- Reliable signal in occipital, posterior parietal, and superior temporal cortex.
- Demonstrates that ms-scale electrophysiology survives free-viewing motion artefact.

## Open questions / limitations

- Adult sample, MEG, lab setting; HBN is pediatric, EEG, single passive run.
- Stimulus is a 7-minute Hollywood drama clip, not an animated short.
- No shot-event-locked analysis; ISC and TF were computed across whole clip windows.
