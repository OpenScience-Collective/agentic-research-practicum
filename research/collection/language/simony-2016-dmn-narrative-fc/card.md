# Dynamic reconfiguration of the default mode network during narrative comprehension

## TL;DR

During naturalistic narrative listening, default-mode and language regions reorganize their functional connectivity dynamically, supporting the integration of meaning over long timescales while early auditory cortex stays stimulus-locked.

## Summary

Simony, Honey, Chen, et al. introduced inter-subject functional correlation (ISFC) to isolate stimulus-locked connectivity from intrinsic noise. Across multiple narrative-listening datasets they showed that DMN nodes reconfigure their pairwise connectivity at narrative-coherent temporal scales, while sensory cortex maintains short-window connectivity. Scrambling the narrative at coarse timescales selectively disrupts DMN reconfiguration. The work is foundational for treating the DMN as a dynamic narrative-integration network rather than a passive resting-state system.

## Relevance to the review

Medium-relevance comparator. The dynamic-FC framework is method-agnostic and could in principle be applied to ThePresent, but ISFC requires multiple subjects, slow-timescale stimuli, and synchronized stimulus onsets. For HBN-EEG, the slow ms-scale of EEG plus the short 3.5-minute movie may make DMN-style multi-minute reconfiguration analyses underpowered. Survives the silent-stimulus boundary in principle, but with reduced power.

## Notable details

- Introduced inter-subject functional correlation (ISFC).
- Story scrambling at paragraph timescale disrupts DMN connectivity reconfiguration.
- Multiple narrative-listening datasets aggregated.
- Open data and code released.

## Open questions / limitations

- Speech-narrative paradigm; visual narrative not tested.
- fMRI ms-scale window too coarse for ERSP-style analyses.
- ISFC requires many subjects with synchronized stimulus.
