# Superior temporal and premotor brain areas necessary for biological motion perception

## TL;DR
Voxel-based lesion-symptom mapping in 60 stroke patients shows that intact biological motion perception requires both posterior superior temporal sulcus (STS) and ventral premotor cortex, providing causal (not merely correlational) evidence that action perception engages a fronto-temporal network.

## Summary
Saygin tested 60 unilateral stroke patients on a battery of biological motion (BM) tasks using point-light displays (PLDs), including detection embedded in noise and direction discrimination. Voxel-based lesion-symptom mapping (VLSM) regressed task performance on per-voxel lesion status, controlling for general visuoperceptual ability with non-biological motion controls. Two regions independently predicted BM deficits: posterior STS (consistent with the STS-fMRI literature) and ventral premotor cortex (vPMC), the human homolog of macaque area F5. Critically, lesions in either region alone produced deficits, demonstrating that BM perception in humans does not localize to STS alone; the action-execution network contributes causally to action perception. Damage to early visual areas did not selectively impair BM beyond generic motion deficits.

## Relevance to the review
High relevance: provides the strongest causal evidence that action-observation networks are necessary for processing movies of moving agents, not optional. For the boy-vs-puppy ERSP contrast, this anchors the prediction that signal differences should emerge over both temporal (STS-projecting) and central (premotor-projecting) electrode clusters, and that any null result over central sites cannot be explained away as STS being the only relevant generator. Also justifies expecting larger effects for shots that depict goal-directed action versus passive figure-ground content.

## Notable details
- N=60 unilateral chronic stroke patients (left and right hemisphere damage represented).
- VLSM with permutation-based threshold; controlled for non-biological motion baseline.
- BM tasks: detection in noise (PLD walker among scrambled noise dots), direction discrimination.
- STS effect was right-lateralized for some sub-tasks; vPMC effect bilateral.
- Conclusion: BM perception engages the action-execution network causally, not just as an epiphenomenal mirror.

## Open questions / limitations
- Adult stroke sample; no developmental data, so HBN cohort 5-21 yr generalization is by inference only.
- PLDs are minimal kinematics; whether full-form animated agents (boy, puppy in ThePresent) require the same vPMC engagement is untested here.
- Lesions are not random; vPMC and STS lesions can co-occur in middle-cerebral-artery infarcts, complicating dissociation.
- Behavioural readout, not electrophysiological; says nothing about timing or oscillatory signature of the network engagement.
