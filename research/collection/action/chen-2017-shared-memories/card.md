# Shared memories reveal shared structure in neural activity across individuals

## TL;DR
fMRI of participants watching the BBC Sherlock pilot then verbally recalling it shows that event-specific neural patterns in default-mode regions are similar across participants during both encoding and retrieval, with cross-subject pattern similarity at retrieval predicting subsequent free-recall accuracy and demonstrating that high-level event representations are partially shared across people.

## Summary
Chen and colleagues scanned 17 participants watching a 50-minute Sherlock clip (encoding) and again as they verbally recalled the story aloud (retrieval). Event-segmented patterns in default-mode regions (mPFC, posterior cingulate, angular gyrus) showed cross-subject similarity at encoding and at retrieval, and the same pattern was reactivated during recall as during the corresponding event in the original viewing. Pattern similarity between participants at retrieval predicted accuracy: better-recalled events showed more shared neural patterns. Provides foundational evidence for shared, communicable event representations in default-mode cortex, and establishes a paradigm (Sherlock-watch + free-recall) widely used in subsequent naturalistic-movie EEG/fMRI work.

## Relevance to the review
High relevance: ThePresent is a Pixar short, similar in genre to the Sherlock paradigm in being a continuous narrative used in naturalistic fMRI/EEG. Chen et al. demonstrate that event-level representations are reproducible across participants and meaningful for memory, supporting the prediction that early ERSP responses to shot onsets in ThePresent should also show cross-subject consistency that the boy-vs-puppy contrast can leverage. Provides methodological precedent: continuous-narrative fMRI is informative; cross-subject ISC and intersubject pattern similarity carry signal. The HBN-EEG cohort viewing ThePresent should generate cross-subject phase-locked responses analogous to the BOLD ISC.

## Notable details
- N=17 adult participants.
- Stimulus: ~48-minute BBC Sherlock pilot (encoding); free-recall (retrieval) directly afterward.
- Behavioral event boundaries from independent group.
- Default-mode regions show event-specific cross-subject pattern similarity.
- Encoding-retrieval pattern reinstatement: same neural signature at recall as during original viewing.
- Cross-subject similarity at retrieval predicts which events are remembered.

## Open questions / limitations
- Sherlock is a complex, multi-character live-action movie; ThePresent is a short Pixar animation with two main agents. Generalization is plausible but not direct.
- Adult sample; HBN cohort (5-21 yrs) may show developmental differences in event-specific representations.
- fMRI; says nothing directly about ERSP timing.
- Free-recall task not part of HBN protocol; cross-subject EEG ISC at shot onsets is the analogous metric.
