# Imitation, empathy, and mirror neurons

## TL;DR
Annual review consolidating evidence that the human mirror system extends beyond hand-action observation to support imitation, emotion contagion, empathy, and intention-understanding, with limbic projections (insula and amygdala) integrating action observation with affective evaluation.

## Summary
Iacoboni reviews ~five years of human imaging work on the mirror system, focusing on extensions to facial expression, emotion, and intention. He discusses fMRI evidence that observing emotional facial expressions activates a network including inferior frontal gyrus pars opercularis, anterior insula, and amygdala, suggesting a route from action observation through emotion contagion to empathy. Intention-understanding evidence is reviewed, including studies showing that anterior IPL responses differentiate observed actions placed in distinct contexts (drinking from cup vs cleaning cup; Iacoboni et al. 2005). Evidence for mirror-system dysfunction in autism (Williams, Dapretto, others) is summarized. The review positions the mirror system as a mechanistic bridge between perception and social cognition.

## Relevance to the review
Medium relevance: ThePresent shots show emotional dynamics (boy's frustration, puppy's persistence) where mirror-system involvement may extend beyond pure motor representation into emotion understanding. The boy-vs-puppy contrast may engage insula/amygdala asymmetrically because human-on-human emotional resonance differs from human-on-canine. Useful for framing why early ERSP differences (within 500 ms) might reflect not only kinematic but also affective recognition. Less direct than Rizzolatti or Hari for mu-band predictions; treated as medium relevance because the empathy and emotion-contagion literature is more relevant to the emotion strand than to action.

## Notable details
- Mirror system extended to facial expressions: pars opercularis, premotor cortex, insula, amygdala.
- Intention-understanding study (Iacoboni et al. 2005, PLoS Biol): anterior IPL discriminates context-defined intentions.
- Autism mirror-system dysfunction: Dapretto et al. 2006 showed reduced pars opercularis activity during emotion imitation.
- Empathy: ventral premotor + insula activation when observing pain (Singer et al. 2004, related literature).
- Limitation acknowledged: correlation between activity and behavior, not causal.

## Open questions / limitations
- Pre-2010 review; predates substantive critiques of mirror-neuron interpretation (Hickok 2009).
- Does not engage with predictive-coding alternatives in depth.
- Animal interaction (human watching dog) not addressed; cross-species mirror responses are plausible but underexplored.
- No oscillatory or temporal-dynamics analysis; helpful for fMRI predictions, less so for ERSP timing.
