# Visual perception of biological motion and a model for its analysis

## TL;DR
Ten or fewer moving light points attached to the major joints of a walking human are sufficient to evoke a vivid, immediate percept of articulated body motion, defining biological motion as a perceptual category that depends on relative kinematics rather than form.

## Summary
Johansson filmed actors with reflective markers on joints in the dark and showed that the resulting point-light displays (PLDs) carry no static figural information yet are perceived as walkers, runners, or dancers within ~200 ms. He proposed a vector-decomposition model in which the visual system extracts a common translatory component (whole-body motion) and residual relative motions among points, producing a hierarchically organized percept. Experiments varied number of points (10 to 12), display duration, and inversion. Inversion abolishes the percept, demonstrating an upright-specific mechanism. Johansson positioned biological motion as a special class of motion processing distinct from generic optic flow.

## Relevance to the review
Establishes the foundational perceptual claim the action strand inherits: agentive motion can be recovered from minimal kinematic input. ThePresent is animated, not point-light, so the boy and puppy carry full surface form alongside articulated motion; the inference here is that biological motion processing should still engage even when surface cues are stylized, because the kinematic signature of articulated agents remains intact in animation. Anchors why a movie shot opening with a moving agent (boy or puppy) is a candidate driver of early visual and superior temporal sulcus (STS) activity rather than only ventral object-recognition responses.

## Notable details
- 10-12 points at major joints reliably evoke walker percept; fewer points degrade gracefully.
- Inversion (180-degree rotation) eliminates biological motion perception, evidence for upright-specific processing.
- Vector model: translatory + relative motion decomposition; hierarchical organization of body parts.
- No surface, no contour, no luminance form needed; pure relative kinematics suffice.
- Display duration as short as a few hundred milliseconds is sufficient for recognition.

## Open questions / limitations
- Pre-fMRI work; says nothing about which cortical areas instantiate the mechanism (that is Saygin 2007 territory).
- All stimuli are humans; generalization to non-human agents (dogs, animated characters) is implicit, not demonstrated.
- No developmental data; whether the perceptual readout exists in 5-21 year olds (HBN cohort) at adult-like fidelity is taken on faith from later work.
