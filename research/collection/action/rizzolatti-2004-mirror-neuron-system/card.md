# The mirror-neuron system

## TL;DR
Authoritative review consolidating monkey single-unit findings (area F5 mirror neurons, area PF/PFG in inferior parietal lobule) with human imaging and TMS evidence into a parieto-premotor mirror system that supports action understanding and imitation, with bilateral inferior parietal cortex and ventral premotor cortex as core nodes.

## Summary
Rizzolatti and Craighero summarize macaque physiology showing that ~17% of F5 neurons fire both during action execution and during observation of the same action by another agent. They distinguish strictly congruent (~30%) and broadly congruent (~60%) mirror neurons. They map the system to homologous human regions (inferior frontal gyrus pars opercularis, ventral premotor cortex, inferior parietal lobule) using PET, fMRI, and TMS evidence. Functional roles surveyed include action understanding, imitation learning, language evolution (Broca's area homology), and developmental dysfunction (autism). The review treats mirror neurons as a substrate for direct matching between observed and executed actions, anchoring a generation of human research.

## Relevance to the review
Direct dependency: the mirror system is the canonical mechanistic story for why watching the boy or puppy initiate movement should engage motor cortex, generate mu/beta-band desynchronization, and drive the action-observation literature. For ThePresent, this review establishes that movie shots opening with goal-directed action (boy reaching, puppy running) should preferentially engage the parieto-premotor mirror network compared to shots with static or non-agentive content. Provides the conceptual scaffold for distinguishing biological-motion processing (STS) from action-understanding processing (IPL/vPMC); the boy-vs-puppy contrast probes both.

## Notable details
- Macaque area F5: ventral premotor; ~17% of recorded units have mirror properties.
- Macaque area PF/PFG: inferior parietal lobule; mirror neurons more abundant.
- Strictly congruent (effector-specific) vs broadly congruent (goal-specific) mirror neurons.
- Human homologs: inferior frontal gyrus (Broadmann area 44/45), vPMC, inferior parietal lobule.
- TMS evidence: motor-evoked potentials (MEPs) in observer's hand muscles increase during action observation, in muscle-specific manner (Fadiga et al. 1995).
- Authors note: not all action observation requires mirror neurons; perceptual recognition may proceed via STS without mirror engagement.

## Open questions / limitations
- Pre-2010 review: predates mounting critiques (Hickok 2009; Caramazza et al. 2014) about whether mirror neurons constitute action understanding.
- Macaque single-unit data; human evidence is correlational, no human single-unit recordings of putative mirror neurons until 2010 (Mukamel et al.).
- Action understanding hypothesis is controversial; alternative accounts (action prediction, motor priming) not engaged here.
- No naturalistic stimuli reviewed; movies, animation, multi-agent scenes are absent.
