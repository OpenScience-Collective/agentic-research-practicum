# Two-year-olds with autism orient to nonsocial contingencies rather than biological motion

## TL;DR
Toddlers with autism fail to preferentially attend to upright point-light biological motion when it competes with audiovisual physical contingencies, while typically developing toddlers show clear biological-motion preference, indicating that early social-orienting deficits in autism reflect a redirection toward physical-causality cues rather than absence of attention.

## Summary
Klin and colleagues used preferential-looking eye tracking with 21 toddlers with autism (mean age ~24 months), 16 typically developing, and 16 developmentally delayed controls. Stimuli paired upright biological motion with inverted or scrambled point-light displays of the same kinematics, with audio synchronized to the upright walker. Typically developing and delayed children preferentially fixated upright biological motion, but autism toddlers did not. Post-hoc analysis revealed the autism children were instead drawn to audiovisual synchrony (footsteps audio matching upright walker footstep timing): when stimuli were re-encoded so audiovisual synchrony favored the inverted walker, autism toddlers oriented to inverted motion. The result reframes social-orienting failure as preferential attention to physical contingencies over biological agents.

## Relevance to the review
Direct dependency for two reasons. First, it establishes that biological motion is a privileged stimulus class that recruits orienting attention in typically developing humans by age 2, supporting the prediction that ThePresent shots opening with the boy or puppy in motion should produce reliable early ERSP responses across the HBN cohort (5-21 yrs). Second, it raises a practical caveat: the HBN cohort is heterogeneous in neurodevelopmental status, and a meaningful share of participants carry autism or ADHD diagnoses; group-level ERSP contrasts may carry between-subject variance traceable to differential biological-motion orienting.

## Notable details
- N: 21 autism, 16 TD, 16 developmentally delayed; mean age ~24 months.
- Eye tracking with synchronized audio.
- TD and DD: strong upright-walker preference (>= 70% looking).
- Autism: no biological-motion preference; orientation driven by audiovisual contingency.
- Manipulation check: re-pairing audio with inverted walker shifted autism gaze to inverted, confirming contingency-driven attention.

## Open questions / limitations
- Toddler sample at 24 months; behavioral phenotype across the HBN 5-21 yr range may be partially compensated.
- PLDs only; whether the contingency-vs-agent dissociation transfers to animated full-form stimuli (such as ThePresent) is untested.
- No EEG; says nothing directly about ERSP signature, but predicts attentional weighting differences that would propagate to evoked oscillatory responses.
- Small N per group; between-subject heterogeneity in autism phenotype (verbal vs minimally verbal) not stratified.
