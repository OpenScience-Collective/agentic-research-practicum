# Human brain activity time-locked to narrative event boundaries

## TL;DR
fMRI evidence that posterior cortical regions (including posterior cingulate, posterior temporal cortex, and motion-sensitive MT+) show transient activity peaks time-locked to behaviorally identified event boundaries during passive listening to written narratives, demonstrating that the brain marks event boundaries automatically without explicit segmentation task.

## Summary
Speer, Zacks, and Reynolds had participants passively listen to spoken excerpts of written narrative while in the fMRI scanner. A separate behavioral group identified fine and coarse event boundaries in the same stimuli. The authors then aligned fMRI BOLD time courses to behavior-derived boundaries. Posterior cortical regions, including posterior cingulate, supplementary motor area, MT+ complex, and posterior superior temporal sulcus, showed peaks of activity at boundary moments. Effects were stronger at coarse boundaries than fine, consistent with hierarchical updating. The paradigm establishes that event-boundary signals are intrinsic and can be recovered without instructing participants to attend to structure.

## Relevance to the review
High relevance: provides the first clear neuroimaging demonstration that boundaries in continuous narrative drive transient cortical responses. Direct support for the prediction that ThePresent shot boundaries (visual analog of narrative boundaries) will elicit measurable transient responses, and that those responses should be modulated by boundary content (boy vs puppy onset). Supports treating shot-onset epochs as event-boundary epochs in the ERSP framework, with motion-sensitive and posterior regions as candidate generators of early-window (0-500 ms) effects.

## Notable details
- 18 adult participants; passive listening to ~5-minute narrative excerpts.
- Behavioral boundary identification by separate group of ~16 raters.
- Fine and coarse boundaries; multi-grain analysis.
- Activations: posterior cingulate, MT+, posterior STS, SMA, anterior cingulate.
- Effect peaks ~3-5 s after boundary (BOLD lag); EEG/ERSP equivalent timing would be 0-500 ms (sub-second).
- Boundary signal distinct from sensory transients in early visual cortex.

## Open questions / limitations
- Auditory narrative, not movie viewing; visual shot boundaries differ in low-level transient profile.
- BOLD timing (3-5 s) does not directly constrain the 0-500 ms ERSP analysis window; need EEG/MEG event-boundary studies for direct timing prediction.
- Adult sample; child cohort not tested.
- No oscillatory analysis; says nothing about which frequency bands carry the signal.
