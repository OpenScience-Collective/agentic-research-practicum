# The human mirror neuron system: a link between action observation and social skills

## TL;DR
EEG mu-rhythm suppression during action observation correlates with self-reported social-skill measures across neurotypical adults: stronger mu suppression to observed grasping co-occurs with higher social-skill scores, supporting a graded, individual-differences-level link between mirror-system engagement and social functioning.

## Summary
Oberman, Pineda, and Ramachandran recorded EEG (electrodes including C3, Cz, C4) from 27 neurotypical adults watching videos of hand actions and rest controls. They quantified mu suppression as the log-ratio of 8-13 Hz power during observation versus a baseline. Participants completed self-report measures of social skill (Social Skills Inventory, Empathy Quotient, Autism Spectrum Quotient). Mu suppression at central electrodes correlated with social-skill measures: greater mu suppression accompanied higher social-skill scores. The result demonstrates that mu-suppression magnitude is not constant across individuals but graded with social-cognitive ability, complementing the autism-specific story (Oberman et al. 2005) with continuous variation in a non-clinical population.

## Relevance to the review
High relevance: anchors the prediction that ThePresent boy-vs-puppy mu-band ERSP differences should be larger in participants with higher self-reported social skills. The HBN cohort includes the Social Responsiveness Scale (SRS), Strengths and Weaknesses Assessment of Normal Behavior (SWAN), and other social-cognition measures, allowing test of an individual-differences extension: the boy-puppy contrast should interact with social-skill scores at central electrodes. Also direct methodological precedent: log-ratio normalization, C3/Cz/C4 pooling, mu band 8-13 Hz.

## Notable details
- N=27 neurotypical adults.
- Video stimuli: hand grasping actions vs static-hand control.
- Mu suppression measured at C3, Cz, C4 over 8-13 Hz.
- Log-ratio normalization (observation vs baseline).
- Correlations with Social Skills Inventory and Empathy Quotient (positive, r ~0.3-0.5).
- Autism Spectrum Quotient negatively correlated with mu suppression.

## Open questions / limitations
- Self-report social-skill measures have limited reliability; correlations are moderate.
- Adult sample; HBN child cohort may show different relationships as social cognition develops.
- Single-session observation; test-retest reliability of mu suppression is moderate.
- No within-subject manipulation of action type (boy vs puppy is a between-stimulus manipulation in our case).
- N=27 is modest for individual-differences correlations (recommended N >= 100 per recent psychometrics).
