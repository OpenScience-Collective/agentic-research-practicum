# Predictive coding: an account of the mirror neuron system

## TL;DR
Theoretical paper recasting mirror neurons as part of a hierarchical predictive-coding architecture: top-down predictions about an agent's intentions descend through inferior parietal and premotor cortex to predict observed kinematics, and bottom-up prediction errors update the inferred goal, dissolving the "direct-matching" puzzle of how kinematics map to intentions.

## Summary
Kilner, Friston, and Frith argue that the standard mirror-neuron framing (action observation activates the same neurons as execution, therefore observers understand actions by simulation) faces a circularity: kinematics underdetermine intention. They reformulate the mirror system as a Bayesian hierarchy with three levels (intention, goal, kinematics). Top-down predictions from intention through goal to kinematics flow over the same parieto-premotor circuit observed in mirror-neuron studies; bottom-up prediction errors at each level update beliefs. Action understanding becomes inverse inference: given observed kinematics, infer the most likely intention by minimizing prediction error. The framework predicts that mirror responses scale with predictability of observed action and with the observer's prior over the agent's goals.

## Relevance to the review
High relevance: ThePresent is a cinematic narrative with strong top-down priors about agent goals. Children watching the boy try to escape his cast or the puppy disrupt his sulk should generate strong intention-level predictions, which under Kilner et al.'s framework predicts modulated parieto-premotor responses at shot-onset moments where the prediction is violated or refined. Provides a principled prediction that ERSP signatures should be larger for shots that change agent identity or shift inferred goal (boy-only -> puppy-only transitions) than for shots that continue an inferred trajectory.

## Notable details
- Three-level hierarchy: intention -> goal -> kinematics.
- Top-down: predictions descend; bottom-up: prediction errors ascend.
- Recasts strictly congruent vs broadly congruent mirror neurons as kinematic-level vs goal-level units.
- Connects to free-energy principle: action-observation systems minimize free energy by inferring causes of sensory input.
- Predicts modulation by predictability: more predictable actions should produce smaller error signals, hence smaller mirror responses; less predictable actions, larger responses.

## Open questions / limitations
- Theoretical paper; no new empirical data.
- Hierarchical inference framing is rich but produces few quantitative point predictions; predictive-coding work since 2007 has only partly empirically validated specific computations.
- Does not address developmental trajectory; HBN cohort spans wide age range with varying narrative-priors strength.
- Says little about oscillatory band specifics; later work (Bastos et al. 2012 on alpha/beta vs gamma) added neurophysiological detail.
