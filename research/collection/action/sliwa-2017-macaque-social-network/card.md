# A dedicated network for social interaction processing in the primate brain

## TL;DR
Macaque fMRI reveals a dedicated network in superior temporal sulcus and medial prefrontal cortex that responds preferentially to videos of agent-agent social interactions over scene-only or single-agent videos, demonstrating that primate brains have specialized circuitry for processing observed social exchanges, not just single-agent actions.

## Summary
Sliwa and Freiwald scanned awake macaques watching short videos of three classes: physical interactions between two agents (e.g., monkeys grooming, fighting), single-agent action (one monkey eating, scratching), and physical-object interactions without agents (e.g., bouncing ball). Whole-brain fMRI revealed a network including posterior STS, anterior STS, and medial prefrontal cortex that responded selectively to social interactions over both control conditions. Importantly, the social-interaction selectivity was not reducible to face or body presence (both controls also depicted bodies); the network specifically encodes agent-agent reciprocal exchange. Comparable network exists in humans (medial prefrontal, posterior STS, temporal pole) per separate human studies cited. Establishes that observation of social interactions, not just isolated actions, recruits dedicated cortical machinery.

## Relevance to the review
Medium relevance: ThePresent shows extended boy-puppy interaction sequences. Some shots depict interaction (boy interacting with puppy, both gazing); others depict single-agent moments (boy alone, puppy alone). If the social-interaction network operates similarly in human children watching cartoons, then shots with both boy and puppy present (excluded from the locked Contrast 1 boy-only vs puppy-only) would have engaged this network differently. Provides a principled justification for why the chosen mutually exclusive contrast (boy-only vs puppy-only, no overlap) factors out social-interaction processing, isolating single-agent processing. Marked medium because direct relevance is via the rejected contrasts and via sanity-check validation, not the primary boy-only vs puppy-only contrast.

## Notable details
- Macaques: 4 awake animals scanned with contrast agent fMRI.
- Three video classes: social interaction, single-agent action, physical object interaction.
- Network: posterior STS, anterior STS, medial prefrontal cortex.
- Selectivity not driven by face or body presence alone (controlled).
- Connects to human equivalents in medial prefrontal cortex and STS.

## Open questions / limitations
- Macaque, not human; cross-species generalization is plausible but uncertain.
- Adult macaques; pediatric or developmental data absent.
- ~30-second video clips; not movie-length naturalistic stimuli.
- fMRI temporal resolution; says nothing about EEG timing or oscillatory signatures.
- Focused on inter-agent interaction; the boy-only vs puppy-only contrast in our project specifically excludes interaction frames.
