# Source: Hasson, Malach & Heeger 2010, Reliability of cortical activity during natural stimulation

- DOI: 10.1016/j.tics.2009.10.011
- Journal: Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 14(1), 40-48
- Publisher: Elsevier (Cell Press)
- License: publisher-paywall; PMC author manuscript at PMC2818432.

## Abstract (verbatim)

Cognitive neuroscience research has been characterized by the use of strictly controlled stimulus sets and tasks. By contrast, recent work has demonstrated that natural stimulation, such as the viewing of films or listening to music, can elicit highly reliable patterns of cortical activity across individuals. Although this approach has shown great promise, the methods needed to analyze the resulting data are still in development. In this review, we summarize recent attempts to characterize the cortical activations elicited by natural stimulation. We discuss methodological developments, the relationship between natural stimulation and traditional experimental designs, and the implications for understanding cortical function.

## Topics covered (paraphrased)

1. **ISC method**: pairwise or leave-one-out correlation across participants per voxel.
2. **Reliability across cortex**: high in primary sensory, intermediate in association, near-zero in stimulus-independent regions.
3. **Stimulus-locked vs context-locked**: low-level vs high-level synchrony.
4. **Naturalness gradient**: continuum from artificial controlled stimuli to fully natural; animation is intermediate.
5. **Methodological caveats**: head motion, attention, individual differences.
6. **Connections to traditional designs**: ISC complements rather than replaces controlled experiments.

## Authors' conclusion

Naturalistic stimulation is a legitimate and powerful paradigm; ISC is a robust method; the cortex shows reliable responses across a wide range of stimulus naturalness, supporting its use across cognitive neuroscience.
