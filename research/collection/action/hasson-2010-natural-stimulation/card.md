# Reliability of cortical activity during natural stimulation

## TL;DR
Trends-in-Cognitive-Sciences review arguing that natural stimuli (movies, real-life soundscapes) elicit highly reliable cortical responses across participants in regions ranging from primary sensory to high-level association cortex, validating intersubject correlation (ISC) as a method and natural stimulation as a legitimate paradigm for cognitive neuroscience.

## Summary
Hasson, Malach, and Heeger review fMRI evidence accumulated since 2004 showing that movie-watching produces highly reliable BOLD responses across observers in early visual cortex (~30% of variance) and substantial reliability in higher-order regions (~10-20%) including STS, IPL, mPFC. They distinguish "stimulus-locked" activity (synchronized to low-level features) from "context-locked" activity (synchronized to higher-level meaning and narrative). They argue that the spectrum from controlled artificial stimuli to fully natural stimuli is a continuum, and naturalness gradient (animation intermediate between artificial geometric stimuli and full live-action) modulates which regions show reliable responses. Methodological recommendations: ISC analysis, leave-one-out validation, sliding-window approaches.

## Relevance to the review
Medium relevance: ThePresent is animation, intermediate on the naturalness gradient between Heider-Simmel triangles (Castelli 2000) and live-action movies (Hasson 2004 Sherlock; Chen 2017). The review establishes that intermediate-naturalness stimuli still produce reliable cross-subject responses, validating the ThePresent paradigm. Provides framework for predicting which regions should show shot-onset locked responses in EEG: early sensory cortex (occipital electrodes) most reliably, with higher-order locking weaker but present. Treated medium because it is a methodological/conceptual review, not a direct constraint on the boy-vs-puppy ERSP contrast.

## Notable details
- ISC: pairwise or leave-one-out correlation across participants per voxel/region.
- Reliability ranges: ~30% in primary visual; ~10-20% in higher-order; near-zero in regions with stimulus-independent activity.
- Naturalness gradient: from artificial controlled stimuli (high reliability in early sensory only) to fully natural (broader reliability across hierarchy).
- Animation (Pixar shorts): treated as intermediate; expected to engage early-to-mid visual hierarchy reliably.
- Dual-coded distinction: stimulus-locked vs context-locked activity within same region.

## Open questions / limitations
- Review focused on adult fMRI; pediatric and EEG analogs need separate validation.
- Naturalness gradient is heuristic, not formalized; what counts as "more natural" is judgment-call.
- Doesn't directly address shot-boundary phenomena; broader reliability framework.
- Intersubject phase-locking in EEG (the ERSP analog) has different statistical properties than BOLD ISC.
